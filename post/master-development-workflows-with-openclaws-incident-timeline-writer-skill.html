Key Features and Workflow

1. Inputs

To effectively use this skill, it requires specific inputs:

  • Desired Outcome & Deadline: Clearly define the goal and the timeline.
  • Constraints: Time, tools available, and risk tolerance.
  • Available Artifacts: Code snippets, documentation, screenshots, logs, and other relevant materials.

2. The Playbook: Structured Process for Efficiency

The skill follows a structured approach to ensure a logical and efficient workflow:

  1. Define Success & Failure Criteria: Establish measurable goals and conditions that determine success or failure.
  2. Build a Minimal Viable Path: Start with the smallest implementation path and scale as needed.
  3. Checkpoint Execution: Execute the plan in stages, recording evidence at each step to ensure transparency and accountability.
  4. Apply Quality Gates: Evaluate progress against predefined quality standards before advancing to the next phase.
  5. Deliver Actionable Outputs: Provide clear, implementation-ready recommendations and action plans.

3. Quality Gates: Ensuring Standards Are Met

The skill enforces rigorous quality checks to maintain standards:

  • Evidence-Based Claims: Only validated findings are included.
  • Transparency of Assumptions: Explicitly state assumptions and tradeoffs.
  • Prioritize Reversible Changes: Where possible, prefer modifications that can be easily reverted if issues arise.
  • Clear Ownership & Timelines: Assign clear responsibilities and estimated completion times for each task.
  • Fallback Options: Include alternative plans if the main strategy fails.

4. Output Format: Structured and Actionable

The final output is organized for clarity and usability:

  • Situation Summary (5 lines max): A concise overview of the scenario.
  • Top Findings (Ranked by Impact): Key insights prioritized by their significance.
  • Action Plan (Today/This Week): Immediate and short-term tasks to take.
  • Risks & Mitigations: Potential challenges and strategies to address them.
  • Exact Checklist/Commands: Step-by-step instructions or executable commands for implementation.

5. Example Prompts for Ease of Use

  • "Use this skill to turn my messy notes into a production-ready plan."
  • "Run this skill and give me a risk-first action sequence with acceptance checks."
