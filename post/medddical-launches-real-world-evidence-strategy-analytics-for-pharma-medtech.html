Payers, providers, and regulators want to see how drugs perform outside of the highly controlled environment of a trial. Real-World Evidence has become the cornerstone of evidence-based medicine, influencing everything from drug development to market access and post-market surveillance.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is Real-World Evidence (RWE) and why is it important?

Real-World Evidence is clinical evidence about the usage and potential benefits or risks of a medical product derived from analysis of Real-World Data (RWD). It is essential because it provides insights into how products perform in diverse patient populations and real-world clinical practices, complementing the data obtained from randomized controlled trials.
