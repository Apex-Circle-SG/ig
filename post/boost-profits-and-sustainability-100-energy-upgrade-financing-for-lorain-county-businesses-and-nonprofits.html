How the Financing Works

This is not a traditional loan that burdens your credit or restricts your liquidity. Often utilizing mechanisms like PACE (Property Assessed Clean Energy) financing, the structure is fundamentally different from a conventional bank loan.

Key Features of the Financing Structure

  • 100% Upfront Coverage: You can cover the entire cost of the project—including labor, materials, and soft costs (engineering fees, permitting, etc.)—with zero or minimal money down.
  • Long-Term Repayment: Financing terms are often tied to the expected life of the equipment, sometimes extending up to 20 or 25 years. This aligns repayment with the lifecycle of the upgrade.
  • Non-Recourse/Tax Assessment Approach: In many PACE structures, the financing is attached to the property, not the business owner. If you sell the property, the financing obligation can often be transferred to the new owner, who will also enjoy the lower utility bills.
  • Cash Flow Positive from Day One: The goal of these projects is to design them so that the projected annual energy savings are equal to or greater than the annual cost of the financing repayment.

Step-by-Step Implementation Guide

Navigating energy upgrade projects may seem daunting, but the process is structured to guide you from assessment to completion.

1. Energy Audit and Feasibility Study

The first step is understanding where your energy is going. A professional energy audit identifies waste—leaky windows, outdated insulation, inefficient motors, or lighting that could be optimized. This study provides the baseline for the project.

2. Project Scoping and Design

Based on the audit, contractors and engineers develop a project plan. They prioritize upgrades that offer the best return on investment (ROI). For example, replacing a failing chiller or converting a warehouse to LED lighting often yields the fastest payback.

3. Securing Financing

With a clear scope of work and guaranteed projected savings, the financing application is processed. Because the projects are technically vetted and the repayment is often tied to the property, the approval process is streamlined compared to unsecured business loans.

4. Construction and Verification

The upgrades are installed. Once complete, a verification process often ensures that the installed systems perform as expected, ensuring the actual savings align with the projected savings.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

1. What if I am renting my space?

This depends on the specific financing program, but often, the property owner (landlord) must approve and participate in the financing since it is attached to the property. However, landlords are often incentivized to do this because the building becomes more valuable and easier to lease.

2. Will this affect my business’s credit rating?

Because many of these programs are structured as property-based assessments rather than personal or business debt, they typically do not show up on your personal or business credit report, thereby preserving your credit capacity for other operational needs.

3. What happens if I sell my business or the building?

In most PACE-style programs, the financing obligation is tied to the property, not the owner. When the property is sold, the new owner inherits the equipment and the repayment obligation, continuing to benefit from the reduced energy costs.

4. Are there any tax implications I should be aware of?

Yes. Many energy-efficient upgrades qualify for federal tax incentives, credits, and accelerated depreciation. It is highly recommended that you consult with a tax professional to understand how your specific project can leverage these incentives alongside the financing.

5. How do I get started?

Start by requesting a professional energy audit of your facility. Many local utility companies offer subsidized or free energy assessments. Once you have that report, contact a commercial energy efficiency contractor or a regional development agency in Lorain County that manages these financing programs.
