How to Apply for Global Entry (And How to Get It for Free)

International travel is exhilarating, but the return trip often ends with the dreaded long lines at customs and immigration. If you are a frequent traveler, Global Entry is the ultimate travel hack to bypass these bottlenecks. By becoming a trusted traveler, you can breeze through U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) in minutes rather than hours.

What is Global Entry?

Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows pre-approved, low-risk travelers to receive expedited clearance upon arrival into the United States. When you arrive at a participating airport, instead of waiting in a traditional line, you head to a Global Entry kiosk, scan your passport, complete a facial recognition check, and proceed to baggage claim.

Perhaps the biggest perk is that Global Entry includes TSA PreCheck membership. This means you also get to enjoy expedited security screening at domestic airports, allowing you to keep your shoes on, laptops in your bag, and avoid the standard security queues.

How to Apply for Global Entry: A Step-by-Step Guide

The application process for Global Entry requires patience, as the program is highly popular and vetting is thorough. Here is the breakdown of how to get started.

1. Create a Login.gov Account

The entire application process happens through the Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) website. You will need to create a Login.gov account to securely access the portal. Do not use third-party websites that claim to help you apply; they are often scams or services that charge extra fees without providing any added value.

2. Fill Out the Online Application

Once logged in, select “Global Entry” and fill out the detailed form. You will need to provide your full employment history, residential history for the past five years, and international travel history. Be scrupulously honest; any discrepancies or omissions can lead to a denial.

3. Pay the Non-Refundable Application Fee

As of 2024, the application fee is $120. This fee is non-refundable, regardless of whether your application is approved or denied. This is where strategic credit card usage comes into play (more on that below).

4. Wait for Conditional Approval

After submission, your application undergoes a background check. This process can take anywhere from a few days to several months. You will receive an email notification in your TTP portal once your status changes to “Conditionally Approved.”

5. Schedule and Complete Your Interview

Conditional approval is not full membership. You must schedule an in-person interview at an Enrollment Center. These centers are often located at major international airports. Be prepared to bring your passport and a secondary form of identification (like a driver’s license). The officer will verify your documents, take your fingerprints, and ask a few questions about your travel habits.

What if Enrollment Centers are Booked?

One of the biggest hurdles to obtaining Global Entry is finding an interview slot. If your local airport has a six-month wait, consider these strategies:

  • Enrollment on Arrival (EoA): This is the secret weapon for many travelers. If you have been conditionally approved, you can complete your interview upon your return from an international flight at participating airports. No appointment is necessary; just follow the signs for Global Entry and let the officer know you are there for an EoA interview.
  • Monitor for Cancellations: People cancel appointments daily. Check the TTP website frequently, especially in the mornings or during the week when people are rearranging their schedules.
  • Third-Party Notification Services: There are services and browser extensions that monitor the TTP website and alert you via text or email when a new appointment slot opens up at your preferred location.

Is Global Entry Worth It?

If you travel internationally more than once every two years, the time saved at customs is well worth the $120 investment. When you factor in the included TSA PreCheck membership, the value proposition increases significantly. For frequent flyers, the ability to skip the long security lines at domestic airports alone makes the program a “must-have” travel accessory.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Does everyone in my family need to apply?

Yes. Every applicant must have their own Global Entry membership, regardless of age. Even infants must have their own application and interview.

How long is Global Entry valid?

Global Entry membership is valid for five years. You can apply for renewal up to one year before your current membership expires.

What happens if I get a new passport?

If you renew your passport, you must update your document information in your TTP portal account immediately. Failing to update this information can cause issues at the kiosks.

Is Global Entry the same as TSA PreCheck?

Global Entry includes TSA PreCheck, but TSA PreCheck does not include Global Entry. Global Entry is the superior program because it covers both international and domestic travel benefits.
