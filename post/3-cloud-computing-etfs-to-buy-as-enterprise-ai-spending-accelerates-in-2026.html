3 Cloud Computing ETFs to Buy as Enterprise AI Spending Accelerates in 2026

2026-04-28

The Convergence of Cloud Computing and Artificial Intelligence

As we approach 2026, the technology landscape is undergoing a seismic shift driven by the rapid adoption of artificial intelligence. Enterprise organizations are no longer merely experimenting with AI; they are integrating it into the core of their business operations. This transition requires massive computational power, scalable storage, and advanced networking capabilities, all of which are hosted in the cloud. Consequently, cloud computing is the essential bedrock upon which the future of enterprise AI is being built.

For investors looking to capitalize on this trend, individual stock picking can be risky and time-consuming. Instead, exchange-traded funds (ETFs) offer a diversified approach to capturing the growth of the entire cloud ecosystem. By investing in ETFs that focus on cloud infrastructure, software-as-a-service (SaaS), and AI-driven platforms, you can gain exposure to the companies leading this digital transformation.

Why Cloud Infrastructure is the Backbone of AI

Artificial intelligence models, particularly Large Language Models (LLMs), require immense amounts of data and processing power. Enterprise AI spending is accelerating because companies realize that leveraging AI can provide a significant competitive advantage. Whether it is predictive analytics, automated customer support, or generative design tools, these applications rely on the high-performance computing resources provided by major cloud service providers (CSPs) and their supporting tech stack.

The synergy between cloud providers and AI developers has created a virtuous cycle. As AI becomes more sophisticated, it demands more cloud capacity. As cloud capacity increases, it enables more advanced AI capabilities. This cycle is expected to intensify throughout 2026, making cloud-focused ETFs a strategic addition to a growth-oriented investment portfolio.

Top 3 Cloud Computing ETFs for 2026

1. First Trust Cloud Computing ETF (SKYY)

The First Trust Cloud Computing ETF (SKYY) is perhaps the most well-known fund in this sector. It provides exposure to companies involved in the cloud computing industry, including those that provide cloud infrastructure, platform-as-a-service (PaaS), and software-as-a-service (SaaS). The fund uses a proprietary selection process to identify companies that are pure-play cloud providers or have significant cloud operations.

Why SKYY is a top pick for 2026: The fund is heavily weighted toward the major cloud giants that provide the infrastructure for AI. As enterprise spending shifts toward hybrid and multi-cloud environments, SKYY is positioned to benefit from the continued dominance of these key players. Its focus on both hardware and software ensures that investors are not tied to a single segment of the cloud value chain.

2. Global X Cloud Computing ETF (CLOU)

The Global X Cloud Computing ETF (CLOU) offers a more targeted approach. It seeks to invest in companies positioned to benefit from the increased adoption of cloud computing technology. This includes companies that derive the majority of their revenue from cloud services, such as data center REITs, cloud and edge computing infrastructure, and cloud-based software applications.

Why CLOU is a top pick for 2026: CLOU is particularly strong at capturing the growth in the secondary layers of the cloud ecosystem. While it holds major tech firms, it also provides exposure to mid-cap companies that are innovating in the cloud security and edge computing spaces. As enterprise AI moves closer to the point of data collection (edge AI), these smaller, specialized players could see outsized growth.

3. WisdomTree Cloud Computing Fund (WCLD)

The WisdomTree Cloud Computing Fund (WCLD) takes a different approach by focusing on the growth-oriented software side of the cloud. It tracks an index of companies that are primarily focused on the cloud-based software delivery model. This fund is ideal for investors who believe that the next wave of AI value will be captured by the applications and platforms that businesses use every day.

Why WCLD is a top pick for 2026: As enterprises move past the initial infrastructure build-out phase, they will be looking for AI-powered software solutions to drive productivity. WCLD is heavily tilted toward high-growth SaaS companies that are integrating generative AI into their products. This makes it a high-beta play on the software innovation cycle that is expected to peak in 2026.

The Role of Enterprise AI in Long-Term Growth

The acceleration of enterprise AI spending is not a temporary trend; it is a structural change in how businesses operate. Companies are investing in AI to automate workflows, reduce operational costs, and create new revenue streams. This requires a robust cloud foundation. Cloud computing providers are essentially the utility companies of the AI era. Just as electricity powered the industrial revolution, cloud computing powers the artificial intelligence revolution.

Investors should look for ETFs that balance both the ‘picks and shovels’ of the industry—the hardware and data centers—with the ‘gold miners’—the software companies building AI applications on top of that infrastructure. The three ETFs discussed provide a comprehensive way to capture this dual-sided growth.

Risks and Considerations for Cloud Investors

While the outlook for cloud computing and AI is bullish, investors must remain aware of the inherent risks. Market volatility in the tech sector can be significant. Furthermore, regulatory scrutiny regarding AI ethics and data privacy could impact the growth trajectories of major cloud players. Additionally, high interest rates can sometimes weigh on growth-oriented technology stocks, as their valuations are often based on future earnings potential.

It is recommended to maintain a long-term perspective. The integration of AI into enterprise workflows is a multi-year process. Dollar-cost averaging into these ETFs can help mitigate the impact of short-term market fluctuations and allow investors to accumulate positions over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes these ETFs different from a standard S&P 500 index fund?

While an S&P 500 fund provides broad market exposure, these cloud-specific ETFs are concentrated in the technology sector. They provide higher exposure to the specific companies driving AI development, potentially leading to higher returns during tech-led bull markets, though they also carry higher sector-specific risk.

Are these ETFs suitable for long-term retirement accounts?

These ETFs can be excellent for long-term growth portfolios, especially for investors with a time horizon of five to ten years. However, because they are sector-specific, they should typically represent a portion of a well-diversified portfolio rather than the entire allocation.

How does enterprise AI spending specifically impact cloud revenue?

Enterprise AI requires massive data processing. Companies must rent cloud servers to train and run their AI models. This creates a direct revenue stream for cloud providers, as enterprises pay for the compute, storage, and networking resources required to support their AI initiatives.

Should I worry about the valuation of these tech companies?

Technology stocks often trade at high price-to-earnings ratios because of their high growth expectations. While valuations are a valid concern, the massive scale of the AI opportunity is currently justifying these premiums. It is important to evaluate these ETFs based on their holdings’ innovation potential and market leadership rather than just current valuation metrics.
