The Iconic Candle Designers Swear By Just Got Its First Makeover in 63 Years

2026-04-24

A Legacy Refined: The Iconic Candle Makeover

For over six decades, one specific candle silhouette has graced the coffee tables, mantels, and bedside tables of the world’s most discerning interior designers. It is the quintessential object of quiet luxury—a vessel that transcends trends, offering a masterclass in minimalist sophistication. Now, for the first time since its inception 63 years ago, this iconic design has received a modern makeover. This update is not merely an aesthetic shift; it is a thoughtful evolution that honors the heritage of the original while embracing the contemporary needs of modern home styling.

In the world of high-end interior design, the importance of home fragrance cannot be overstated. Designers often refer to scent as the invisible layer of a room’s architecture. When a product becomes a staple in this industry, any change to its design is met with both excitement and scrutiny. This latest redesign manages to capture the essence of the original while introducing subtle refinements that make it feel entirely new.

The History of an Icon

To understand why this makeover is such a significant moment in the design industry, we must look back at the origins of the piece. Launched over 60 years ago, the candle was designed during an era where functionality met mid-century modern aesthetics. Its clean lines, weighted glass, and signature wax blend set the standard for what a luxury candle should be.

Throughout the decades, the design remained largely untouched. It became a collectible item, with vintage versions often appearing in high-end auction houses and resale markets. Designers favored it because it was chameleon-like; it could sit perfectly on a rustic oak table or a polished marble surface without looking out of place.

Why Designers Are Obsessed

Why do industry professionals swear by this specific brand? The answer lies in the balance of form and function. Designers prioritize products that enhance a space without overwhelming it. This candle offers:

  • Timeless Silhouette: The proportions have remained a blueprint for luxury vessel design.
  • Premium Fragrance Throw: The proprietary wax blend ensures an even burn and a sophisticated scent profile that lingers without being cloying.
  • Versatility: The vessel itself is designed to be repurposed, serving as a chic catch-all or vase long after the wax has vanished.

What Has Changed in the New Design?

The 63-year wait for a redesign has culminated in a series of updates that are intentional and precise. The brand focused on sustainability and tactile experience, two pillars of modern luxury. The new vessel features a slightly weighted base, providing a more substantial feel in the hand, and the glass has transitioned to a recycled, artisanal-blown format that catches the light differently than the original.

The label, which has always been a point of contention for minimalists, has been updated to a more subtle, debossed effect on the glass. This shift moves the branding from being a billboard to being an understated detail that reveals itself only upon closer inspection. It is a masterclass in quiet luxury.

Sustainability at the Core

In addition to the visual changes, the brand has overhauled its manufacturing process. The new iteration uses a custom-blended coconut and apricot wax, which is cleaner burning and more environmentally friendly than the paraffin blends of the past. The wick, now crafted from organic cotton, has been tested for over 500 hours to ensure a soot-free experience, addressing one of the few complaints long-term users had about the original.

Integrating the New Icon Into Your Home

For those looking to incorporate this updated classic into their own decor, the options are endless. Designers suggest grouping the candle with other textures to create a layered look. Place it atop a stack of coffee table books, pair it with a sculptural ceramic bowl, or let it stand alone on a bathroom vanity to elevate your daily routine.

Because the design is so neutral, it works in almost any interior style. Whether you lean toward Scandi-minimalism, maximalist eclecticism, or traditional elegance, this candle acts as a grounding force. The new, slightly more translucent glass allows the glow of the flame to permeate the room, creating a warmer, more inviting ambiance than the original opaque vessels.

The Future of Home Fragrance

This makeover signals a broader trend in the home decor industry: the return to heritage brands reinventing themselves for a new generation. As consumers become more conscious of what they bring into their homes, brands are being forced to justify their existence through quality, longevity, and ethical production. This iconic candle has successfully bridged the gap between legacy and innovation, proving that even the most perfect designs can still find room to grow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the new design compatible with original lids?

The brand has confirmed that while the vessel dimensions have been slightly adjusted for a more ergonomic feel, they have maintained the signature lid diameter. This ensures that collectors who have accumulated original lids over the years can still use them with the new vessels.

Does the scent profile change with the new wax blend?

The brand has worked closely with master perfumers to ensure that the scent throw remains identical to the original formulations. While the burn is cleaner due to the new wax blend, the olfactory experience remains the signature scent that customers have loved for decades.

Where can I purchase the updated collection?

The new collection is currently available through high-end retailers, select boutique home stores, and the brand’s official website. Due to high demand following the announcement, some limited-edition colorways may be on backorder, so it is best to check availability early.

Is the glass vessel dishwasher safe?

While the glass is high-quality, the brand recommends hand-washing the vessel once the wax is removed to preserve the integrity of the debossed logo and the finish of the glass. The vessel is designed to be a permanent fixture in your home, not a disposable item.

Why did it take 63 years to update the design?

The brand maintained a philosophy of perfectionism. They did not want to change the design for the sake of trends. The recent update was driven by advancements in sustainable material technology and a desire to improve the user experience, rather than a need to chase seasonal fads.
