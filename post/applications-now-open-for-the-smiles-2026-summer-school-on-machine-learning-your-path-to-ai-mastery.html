What is the SMILES-2026 Summer School?

The SMILES-2026 Summer School is a highly anticipated annual event designed to bridge the gap between theoretical machine learning research and practical, real-world applications. Over the course of two intensive weeks, participants will engage with world-class faculty, participate in hands-on workshops, and collaborate on projects that push the boundaries of what is possible with neural networks, deep learning, and generative AI.

The Mission of SMILES

The primary mission of the SMILES (Summer Machine Learning Intensive & Educational Symposium) initiative is to democratize access to high-level AI education. By bringing together the brightest minds from both academia and industry, the program fosters an environment of innovation, critical thinking, and ethical development. Whether you are a doctoral candidate specializing in computer vision or a software engineer looking to pivot into data science, SMILES-2026 offers a curriculum tailored to your growth.

Key Features of the Curriculum

The 2026 program has been meticulously curated to cover the most relevant topics in the current technological landscape. Our faculty consists of pioneers who have shaped the industry, ensuring that every lecture is grounded in the latest breakthroughs.

Core Learning Tracks

  • Foundations of Deep Learning: A deep dive into architectures, loss functions, and optimization techniques.
  • Large Language Models (LLMs): Practical training on training, fine-tuning, and deploying modern generative models.
  • Reinforcement Learning: Exploring agents, environments, and decision-making systems in complex scenarios.
  • AI Ethics and Governance: Addressing the critical challenges of bias, safety, and transparency in machine learning systems.
  • MLOps and Scalability: Learning how to transition from a Jupyter notebook to a production-grade system.

Application Requirements and Selection Process

Due to the high volume of interest, the selection process for SMILES-2026 is competitive. We are looking for candidates who demonstrate a strong mathematical foundation, a passion for machine learning research, and a clear vision for how they intend to apply their knowledge. The application portal requires a CV, a statement of purpose, and two letters of recommendation.

Important Dates

The application period is currently active and will close on March 31, 2026. Applicants are encouraged to submit their materials early to ensure thorough consideration. Decisions will be communicated by late April, allowing successful candidates ample time to arrange travel and housing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to apply for SMILES-2026?

The program is primarily designed for graduate students (Masters and PhD) and early-career researchers. However, we also welcome applications from industry professionals with a strong background in computer science, statistics, or mathematics who are looking to transition into research-heavy roles.

Is there a registration fee?

Yes, there is a registration fee that covers all academic materials, access to computing clusters, and daily networking events. We also offer a limited number of scholarships and travel grants for students from underrepresented backgrounds and those from institutions with limited funding.

Will the content be available online?

While the core experience is designed for in-person participation to maximize collaboration, key lectures will be recorded and made available to the public on our website following the conclusion of the event. However, workshops and networking sessions will remain exclusive to in-person attendees.

What is the expected time commitment?

SMILES-2026 is an intensive, full-time program. Participants should expect to engage in lectures, labs, and group projects for approximately 8 to 10 hours per day throughout the two-week duration. It is highly recommended that attendees clear their schedules of other professional commitments during this time.
