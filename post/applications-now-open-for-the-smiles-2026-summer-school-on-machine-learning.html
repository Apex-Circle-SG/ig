Why Attend SMILES-2026?

The SMILES-2026 Summer School is more than just a series of lectures. It is a comprehensive immersion into the world of advanced data science. Attendees will benefit from a curated program that balances rigorous mathematical foundations with real-world implementation techniques.

Access to World-Class Experts

One of the primary benefits of attending SMILES-2026 is the direct access to our faculty. Our instructors are drawn from the top tiers of both academia and industry. You will have the opportunity to engage in Q&A sessions, participate in roundtable discussions, and receive mentorship from individuals who are currently shaping the future of machine learning algorithms.

Hands-On Workshop Experience

We believe that you cannot truly learn machine learning without getting your hands dirty with code. The 2026 curriculum features intensive workshops where participants will work on real-world datasets. From natural language processing (NLP) to computer vision, you will apply the latest techniques to solve complex problems under the guidance of experienced practitioners.

Networking and Collaborative Opportunities

The community at SMILES is one of our greatest assets. By attending, you will be joining a global network of peers who are equally passionate about AI. Many past participants have gone on to form long-term research collaborations, co-author academic papers, and launch successful startups together.

The SMILES-2026 Curriculum Highlights

The program is structured to accommodate varying levels of expertise while pushing everyone toward mastery. The curriculum is divided into three core tracks:

  • Foundations of AI: A deep dive into optimization, probability, and linear algebra as applied to modern architectures.
  • Advanced Neural Architectures: Exploring transformers, diffusion models, and the latest breakthroughs in large-scale generative AI.
  • Responsible AI: A critical look at bias, fairness, transparency, and the regulatory landscape of machine learning systems.

Each track is supported by practical coding labs that utilize the latest industry-standard tools, ensuring that participants leave with a portfolio of work they can showcase to potential employers or academic committees.

Application Requirements and Process

The selection process for SMILES-2026 is competitive, as we aim to maintain a high-quality environment that fosters meaningful interactions. To apply, prospective attendees must submit the following:

  • A current CV detailing academic and professional experience.
  • A statement of purpose explaining your interest in machine learning and how this summer school will contribute to your career goals.
  • Two letters of recommendation from academic or professional supervisors.
  • A brief portfolio or links to GitHub repositories that showcase your previous work in programming or data analysis.

We encourage applicants from diverse backgrounds to apply. We are committed to fostering an inclusive environment that values different perspectives and experiences in the pursuit of technological innovation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible to apply for SMILES-2026?

The program is primarily designed for graduate students, PhD candidates, and early-career researchers or engineers who have a solid foundation in mathematics and programming. However, we welcome applications from anyone who can demonstrate the necessary technical background to excel in an intensive environment.

Is the summer school held in-person or online?

SMILES-2026 will be held as a hybrid event. We offer an in-person track for those who wish to experience the full networking benefits and a remote track that provides access to all lectures and lab materials for global participants.

Are there scholarships available?

Yes, we offer a limited number of merit-based and need-based scholarships to cover registration fees. Please indicate your interest in financial assistance during the application process, and provide the necessary documentation as requested.

What programming languages are required?

Proficiency in Python is essential, as it is the primary language used for all workshops and labs. Familiarity with libraries such as PyTorch, TensorFlow, or JAX is highly recommended to get the most out of the technical sessions.

When is the deadline to apply?

Applications close on March 31, 2026. Given the high demand and limited spots, we strongly encourage interested candidates to submit their applications as early as possible. Late applications will not be considered under any circumstances.
