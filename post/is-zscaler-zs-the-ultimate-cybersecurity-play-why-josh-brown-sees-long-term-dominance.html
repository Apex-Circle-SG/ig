Is Zscaler (ZS) the Ultimate Cybersecurity Play? Why Josh Brown Sees Long-Term Dominance

In the rapidly shifting landscape of enterprise security, few names command as much respect as Zscaler (ZS). Recently, financial commentator and CEO of Ritholtz Wealth Management, Josh Brown, threw his weight behind the stock, labeling it a ‘dominant’ player in the cybersecurity space that is poised to lead for years to come. But what exactly makes Zscaler stand out in a crowded market of competitors, and why is this endorsement causing waves among growth investors?

The Core Thesis: Why Zscaler Commands Attention

Josh Brown’s bullish outlook on Zscaler isn’t rooted in short-term hype; it is grounded in the company’s fundamental shift in how corporations secure their data. Zscaler operates on the Zero Trust Exchange architecture, a paradigm shift from traditional network security.

Moving Beyond the ‘Moat’

Traditionally, security relied on a ‘castle and moat’ approach—guarding the perimeter of the corporate network. However, as workforces become decentralized and applications move to the cloud, that perimeter has effectively vanished. Zscaler has capitalized on this by:

  • Eliminating the need for complex VPNs that create vulnerabilities.
  • Providing direct, secure connections between users and applications, regardless of location.
  • Reducing the attack surface significantly compared to legacy hardware-centric firewalls.

Competitive Advantage: The Power of SASE

Zscaler is at the forefront of Secure Access Service Edge (SASE). SASE integrates network security and wide-area networking into a single cloud-native service. For CIOs and CISOs, this represents a consolidation of vendors, saving time, money, and management complexity.

Compared to legacy players like Cisco or Palo Alto Networks, Zscaler’s born-in-the-cloud architecture provides:

  • Scalability: It handles massive volumes of traffic without the latency associated with backhauling data to a physical data center.
  • Visibility: Security teams gain granular control over who accesses what data, creating a stronger compliance and governance framework.
  • Threat Intelligence: Leveraging data from billions of daily transactions, Zscaler’s engine updates its threat detection models in near real-time.

Financial Growth and Market Sentiment

Josh Brown’s comments come at a time when the cybersecurity sector is seeing a flight to quality. Investors are moving away from speculative, unprofitable tech stocks and toward companies that can demonstrate sustainable growth and a clear path to profitability. Zscaler fits this narrative by maintaining impressive revenue growth rates while steadily improving its operational efficiency.

Why ‘Years to Come’ Matters

The tech sector is notoriously volatile, but enterprise cybersecurity is increasingly viewed as non-discretionary spending. Companies will cut marketing budgets or delay office renovations before they cut their cybersecurity budgets. By positioning itself as a platform rather than a single-point solution, Zscaler is weaving itself into the essential fabric of corporate IT infrastructure, creating long-term ‘stickiness.’

Risks Every Investor Should Monitor

While Brown sees long-term dominance, no investment is without risk. Prospective investors in Zscaler should remain aware of:

  1. Valuation Multiples: As a high-growth stock, ZS often trades at a premium. Market corrections can hit these stocks harder than legacy names.
  2. Increased Competition: Major players like Palo Alto Networks are aggressively building out their own cloud-native security offerings.
  3. Macroeconomic Headwinds: Extended sales cycles in enterprise software can impact quarterly revenue projections if CFOs tighten IT budgets globally.

The Verdict: A Foundational Asset?

The endorsement from experts like Josh Brown serves as a reminder to look past quarterly earnings volatility and focus on structural shifts in the industry. Zscaler’s ability to solve the fundamental problems created by remote work and cloud migration suggests that its platform is not merely a ‘nice-to-have’ but a foundational component of modern business. For those looking to gain exposure to the digital transformation of enterprise security, ZS remains a stock to watch closely.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the core business model of Zscaler (ZS)?

Zscaler provides a cloud-native security platform based on a Zero Trust architecture. It allows users to connect securely to applications without the need for traditional VPNs or physical network perimeters.

What is SASE, and why is it important for Zscaler?

SASE stands for Secure Access Service Edge. It is a security model that consolidates networking and security services into a single cloud platform. Zscaler is a leader in this space, helping companies simplify their IT stacks.

Why does Josh Brown call Zscaler a ‘dominant’ stock?

Josh Brown views Zscaler as dominant because of its technological head start in the cloud-security market, its ability to scale, and the fact that its platform is becoming essential for modern, decentralized enterprises.

Is Zscaler better than Palo Alto Networks?

Both companies are leaders in cybersecurity, but they have different strengths. Zscaler is cloud-native and focuses heavily on secure access, while Palo Alto Networks offers a broader, more integrated portfolio including hardware-based firewalls and advanced cloud security.

What should I consider before buying ZS?

Consider the stock’s valuation, your personal time horizon, and your risk tolerance. Growth stocks can be volatile, so ensure it fits within a diversified portfolio strategy.
