What Is Global Entry & How Do I Get It? The Ultimate Guide

For international travelers, few things are as frustrating as stepping off a long-haul flight only to be greeted by an agonizingly long queue at customs. If you travel frequently, you have likely heard of Global Entry, a program designed to turn that hours-long headache into a breeze. But what exactly is Global Entry, and is the process of getting it worth your time and money? In this comprehensive guide, we will break down everything you need to know about this U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program.

What Is Global Entry?

Global Entry is a U.S. government program that allows pre-approved, low-risk travelers to receive expedited clearance upon arrival into the United States. Instead of waiting in the standard customs and immigration lines, members can proceed to dedicated kiosks at participating airports. By scanning your machine-readable passport or U.S. permanent resident card, placing your fingerprints on the scanner, and completing a customs declaration, you can often clear the process in minutes.

Perhaps the most attractive perk for frequent flyers is that Global Entry membership automatically includes TSA PreCheck. This means you gain the dual benefit of faster international arrivals and expedited domestic security screening, making it a powerful tool for any avid traveler.

The Benefits of Having Global Entry

Beyond the primary benefit of expedited reentry, there are several reasons why frequent travelers prioritize Global Entry:

  • Speed: Avoid the “standard” customs lines entirely. Kiosks are generally empty or move very quickly.
  • Reduced Paperwork: The process is largely digitized, eliminating the need to fill out paper customs declaration forms.
  • TSA PreCheck Inclusion: As noted, you won’t need to remove your shoes, laptops, liquids, or light jackets at domestic security checkpoints.
  • International Reciprocity: Depending on your citizenship, you may also gain access to expedited entry programs in other countries.
  • Efficiency for Families: Every person in your party, including children, must have their own Global Entry membership to use the expedited lanes.

Who Is Eligible for Global Entry?

Global Entry is intended for low-risk travelers. The CBP conducts a rigorous background check on every applicant. You may be deemed ineligible if you have been convicted of a criminal offense, have pending criminal charges, or have violated customs, immigration, or agricultural regulations. While citizenship is not limited to U.S. citizens, citizens from specific countries that have formal agreements with the U.S. are eligible to apply. Permanent residents (Green Card holders) are also eligible.

How to Apply: A Step-by-Step Guide

The application process requires patience, as it involves both an online submission and an in-person interview. Follow these steps to get started:

1. Create a Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) Account

Everything starts at the official Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) website. Do not use third-party websites that claim to help you apply; these are often scams or sites that charge unnecessary fees. Create an account, fill out your personal information, and be prepared to provide details about your address history, employment, and international travel over the past five years.

2. Pay the Application Fee

As of the current policy, the application fee is $100 for a five-year membership. This fee is non-refundable, regardless of whether your application is approved or denied. Pro Tip: Many premium travel credit cards now offer a statement credit for Global Entry or TSA PreCheck fees. Check your credit card benefits before paying to see if you can be reimbursed.

3. Wait for Conditional Approval

Once you submit your application, the CBP will perform a background check. This phase can take anywhere from a few days to several months. You will receive an email notification in your TTP account once your status changes to “Conditionally Approved.” Being conditionally approved is not the final step; it is simply an invitation to schedule an interview.

4. Schedule Your Interview

Once you are conditionally approved, you must schedule an in-person interview at a Global Entry Enrollment Center. These are typically located at major international airports. Demand for appointments can be extremely high, and you may find that local centers are booked for months. Keep an eye out for cancellations or consider “Enrollment on Arrival,” a program that allows you to complete your interview while clearing customs after an international flight.

5. Complete the Interview

During the interview, a CBP officer will review your documents, verify your identity, and ask a few questions about your travel history. Bring your passport and proof of residency (such as a utility bill or driver’s license). Once approved, you will receive your Known Traveler Number (KTN), which you must add to your airline profiles to benefit from TSA PreCheck.

Is Global Entry Worth the Wait?

The application process can be lengthy and involves a thorough background check, but for those who travel internationally even once or twice a year, the time saved at the airport is immense. Not having to stand in a two-hour customs line after a ten-hour flight is worth the $100 investment—especially when you factor in the value of the included TSA PreCheck membership.

If you find yourself frequently frustrated by airport security or customs bottlenecks, Global Entry is undoubtedly one of the best investments you can make for your travel lifestyle.

Final Tips for Applicants

Keep your contact information updated in the TTP portal, and ensure your passport information is accurate before your interview. If you change your passport, you must update your TTP account immediately to ensure your Global Entry benefits continue to work seamlessly. Happy travels!
