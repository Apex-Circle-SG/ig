Decoding the OpenClaw Stock Evaluator v3

For investors navigating the complexities of modern financial markets, having access to rigorous, data-backed analysis is not just an advantage—it is a necessity. The OpenClaw ecosystem has introduced a powerful tool in its repertoire: the stock-evaluator-v3 skill. Designed to bridge the gap between casual observation and professional-grade investment research, this tool offers a structured, comprehensive approach to evaluating potential stock investments.

What is the Stock Evaluator v3?

At its core, the Stock Evaluator v3 is an institutional-grade analytical engine. Unlike tools that simply provide price charts or basic headlines, this skill is purpose-built to guide users through the decision-making process when considering buying a stock, analyzing a potential addition to a watchlist, or seeking a definitive recommendation on an investment opportunity. It moves beyond subjective opinions to provide a quantitative framework involving valuation analysis, fundamental research, technical assessment, and clear risk-adjusted positioning advice.

The Critical Deliverables Checklist

One of the most defining features of the Stock Evaluator v3 is its mandatory deliverables checklist. It enforces a high standard of research, ensuring that no stone is left unturned. Every analysis produced by the skill must incorporate:

  • Technical Analysis: A deep dive into price action, key support and resistance levels, and indicators like the Ichimoku Cloud.
  • Fundamental Analysis: A thorough examination of the business model, financial health, and competitive positioning (the “moat”).
  • Advanced Financial Metrics: The tool computes or retrieves essential safety and quality indicators, including the F-Score, Z-Score, and M-Score, to identify potential bankruptcy risks or accounting red flags.
  • Investor Persona Scores: By evaluating a stock through the lens of eight legendary investor frameworks, the tool provides context on whether a stock aligns with a specific strategy (e.g., value, growth, or income).
  • Valuation Assessment: Utilizing multiple methods with a strict margin of safety, the tool helps prevent overpaying for quality assets.
  • Bull vs. Bear Case: A balanced look at both the upside potential and the downside risks.
  • Clear Recommendations: Every analysis concludes with a unambiguous BUY, HOLD, or SELL rating, paired with a conviction rating to help the investor understand the weight behind the analysis.
  • Alternative Candidates: If the recommendation is a SELL, the tool provides 3-5 superior alternatives to ensure the capital is not left idle.

The Zero Fabrication Policy: Data Integrity Above All

Perhaps the most vital aspect of the Stock Evaluator v3 is its adherence to a Zero Fabrication Policy. In an age of AI hallucinations, this tool is designed to be grounded strictly in reality. The skill is programmed to never estimate or hallucinate numeric data points. Instead, every metric displayed on the dashboard must be sourced from:

  • Verified company filings (10-K, 10-Q).
  • Official financial data providers.
  • Real-time web search results (cited and verified).

If data is unavailable, the system is designed to return “N/A” or “–” rather than guessing. This commitment to data integrity ensures that the user can rely on the provided figures for critical decision-making.

The Enhanced Quant-Style Dashboard

The output of the stock evaluator is presented as an enhanced, React-style dashboard. This dashboard is packed with over 60 metrics, providing a comprehensive “snapshot” of a company’s health. The metrics are categorized for clarity:

  • Price and Valuation vs. Financial Performance: Balancing market price with fundamental metrics like P/E ratios, PEG, ROE, ROA, and various profit margins.
  • Growth and Risk: Focusing on reported revenue and earnings growth, alongside debt-to-equity ratios and Altman Z-scores.
  • Liquidity and Sentiment: Incorporating FCF yield, dividend metrics, insider trading activity (via SEC Form 4), and short interest.
  • Quality Scores: High-level assessments including CQVS (Comprehensive Quality & Valuation Score) and the Buffett Moat analysis.

Standardized Benchmarks: Objective Analysis

To avoid subjective interpretation, the tool uses a standardized set of benchmarks to color-code metrics as Good (Green), Neutral (Yellow), or Warning (Red). This allows users to quickly assess, for instance, if a company’s ROE of 15% is actually considered neutral compared to the required 20% benchmark, or if a high debt-to-equity ratio is signaling a warning. By using standardized thresholds for metrics like RSI, Piotroski F-Score, and FCF Yield, the investor can instantly distinguish between fundamentally sound companies and those carrying unnecessary risks.

Operational Nuances

The tool is designed with a European context as the default, using the Euro (€) for all monetary displays. Furthermore, for growth metrics, it explicitly mandates the use of reported growth rates (GAAP/IFRS) rather than “adjusted” or “underlying” figures. This ensures that the investor is looking at the actual, raw performance of the business, accounting for the realities of FX impacts and corporate acquisitions.

Conclusion

The OpenClaw Stock Evaluator v3 represents a shift toward more responsible, systematic, and data-driven retail investing. By enforcing a rigorous analysis checklist, prioritizing data integrity, and providing an easy-to-read, comprehensive dashboard, it provides investors with a professional toolkit for navigating volatile markets. Whether you are a novice looking to understand why a stock might be a “value trap” or an experienced investor looking to quickly assess the fundamental strength of a new candidate, this skill offers a robust, reliable path forward.
