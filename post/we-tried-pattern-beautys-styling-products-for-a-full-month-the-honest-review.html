We Tried Pattern Beauty’s Styling Products for a Full Month: The Honest Review

When Tracee Ellis Ross launched Pattern Beauty, it wasn’t just another hair care line—it was a movement designed specifically for the curly, coily, and tight-textured community. As someone who has spent years chasing the perfect wash-and-go without the crunch or the flakes, I was skeptical. Could a single brand really cater to such a wide spectrum of hair needs while delivering on its promise of hydration and definition? To find out, I committed to using only Pattern Beauty styling products for an entire month. Here is my unfiltered journey, the results, and whether this collection belongs in your vanity.

Understanding the Pattern Beauty Philosophy

Pattern Beauty is built on a foundation of hydration. The products are formulated to celebrate textured hair, not tame it into submission. Unlike many traditional brands that use heavy silicones or drying alcohols, Pattern leans into ingredients like aloe vera, jojoba oil, and shea butter. The goal is to provide moisture that keeps hair elastic and defined, rather than stiff.

The 30-Day Testing Protocol

For this experiment, I focused on three staples from the line: the Curl Gel, the Styling Cream, and the Leave-In Conditioner. My hair type is 3C/4A, prone to dryness, and easily weighed down by heavy oils. Over the month, I tracked three key performance indicators:

  • Moisture Retention: How long did my hair feel hydrated before needing a refresh?
  • Definition: Were my curls distinct, or did they clump into undefined frizz?
  • Ease of Use: How much product was required to achieve a style?

The Results: Week-by-Week Breakdown

Week 1: The Learning Curve

The first few days were all about trial and error. I initially used too much of the Styling Cream, which left my hair feeling slightly tacky. By day four, I adjusted my application method: applying the Leave-In Conditioner on soaking wet hair, followed by a light layer of the Styling Cream. The results were significantly better—my curls felt soft to the touch and the shine was undeniable.

Weeks 2-3: Consistency and Cohesion

By the second week, my hair had fully adjusted to the new routine. One of the most surprising outcomes was how well the products layered. I found that I didn’t need to refresh my hair every morning. A simple misting of water and a tiny dab of Curl Gel was enough to perk up second and third-day hair. This was a massive time-saver compared to my previous routine, which required a complete re-wash every two days.

Week 4: The Final Verdict

After a full month, the most noticeable difference was the health of my ends. They appeared less frayed and significantly more hydrated. While no product can “repair” split ends, the deep moisture profile of the Pattern Styling Cream effectively masked dryness and prevented further breakage. The Curl Gel provided a flexible hold that didn’t leave my hair feeling like straw—a win in any curly girl’s book.

Pros and Cons of the Pattern Beauty Styling Line

The Pros

  • Moisture-First Formulas: The inclusion of high-quality humectants makes these products ideal for dry hair.
  • Scent Profile: Subtle, floral, and not overwhelming—perfect for those sensitive to fragrance.
  • Packaging: The pump and tub designs are not only aesthetically pleasing but functional and easy to use in the shower.
  • Scalp Health: None of the products caused buildup or irritation, even with consistent use.

The Cons

  • The Price Point: Compared to drugstore brands, Pattern is a premium investment.
  • Product Density: If you have finer hair, you must be careful with the Styling Cream, as it can be quite rich.
  • Learning Curve: It takes time to find the exact “ratio” for your specific texture.

How to Maximize Your Results

To get the most out of your Pattern Beauty products, follow these expert tips:

  1. Work on Soaking Wet Hair: Textured hair thrives on moisture. Apply products while your hair is still dripping to trap water inside the cuticle.
  2. Sectioning is Key: Do not rush the process. Divide hair into at least four sections to ensure even distribution.
  3. The “Scrunch” Method: After applying the Curl Gel, scrunch your hair upwards to encourage the curl pattern to spring back.

Is Pattern Beauty Right for You?

If you are struggling with dryness, frizz, or a lack of definition, Pattern Beauty is worth the investment. It is particularly effective for those with 3B to 4C hair types who need more than just a surface-level styling product. However, if you prefer a very “hard” gel hold or have very fine, low-porosity hair, you may need to use these products sparingly or pair them with a lighter primer.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Does Pattern Beauty cause product buildup?

With proper washing, no. Pattern products are generally water-soluble. However, because they are rich in oils and butters, it is important to use a clarifying shampoo every 2-3 weeks to keep the scalp clean.

Can I mix Pattern Beauty with other brands?

Absolutely. While the brand is designed to work as a system, the Leave-In Conditioner acts as an excellent base for various other gels and mousses.

Are these products safe for color-treated hair?

Yes, the ingredients are gentle and free from sulfates and parabens, making them safe for color-treated and chemically processed hair.

How long do the styling products last?

A little goes a long way. Depending on your hair length and density, a single tub of Styling Cream can easily last 2-3 months with regular use.

Conclusion

My month-long experiment with Pattern Beauty yielded positive results. It isn’t just marketing hype; it is a thoughtful, science-backed approach to managing textured hair. By prioritizing moisture and elasticity, the brand has created a line that genuinely improves the look and feel of curls over time. If you’re ready to upgrade your wash-day game, Pattern is certainly a brand to watch.
