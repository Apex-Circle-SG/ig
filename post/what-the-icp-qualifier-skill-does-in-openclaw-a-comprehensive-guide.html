What the ICP Qualifier Skill Does in OpenClaw: A Comprehensive Guide

The ICP Qualifier Skill in OpenClaw is a powerful tool designed to help businesses identify and prioritize their ideal customer profiles (ICPs). This skill is particularly useful for companies like Visual Dept, which offers AI-powered product photography services tailored to direct-to-consumer (DTC) e-commerce brands.

Understanding the ICP Qualifier Skill

The ICP Qualifier Skill is an AI-driven agent that analyzes leads and scores them against a predefined set of criteria to determine their fit with the company’s ideal customer profile.

Key Mission

The primary mission of the ICP Qualifier Skill is to analyze leads and assign them a score based on how well they match the company’s ICP criteria. This scoring system helps businesses prioritize their outreach efforts, ensuring they focus on the most promising leads.

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) Criteria

  • Demographics: Female founders aged 28-45, running DTC Shopify stores in beauty, wellness, home, or fashion sectors, based in the United States.
  • Business Metrics: Revenue between $250K and $2M (with a sweet spot of $500K-$1M), product price points between $50-$300, 100-500 orders per month, and in business for 1-3 years.
  • Primary Verticals: Beauty & cosmetics, skincare & serums, wellness & supplements, candles & home fragrance, fashion & accessories, and home goods.
  • Pain Points: DIY/amateur product photos, high spending on photoshoots, need for constant social content, conversion rates stuck at 1-2%, and reliance on generic Canva templates.

ICP Scoring System

The ICP Qualifier Skill uses a 100-point scoring system to evaluate leads. Each criterion is assigned a specific point value based on its importance to the ICP.

Scoring Criteria

  • Visual Quality: +25 points for poor/amateur visual quality, +20 points for mediocre visual quality.
  • Follower Count: +20 points for a follower count between 1K-50K.
  • Niche Match: +15 points for being in one of the primary verticals.
  • US Location: +15 points for being based in the United States.
  • Website/Shopify Store: +10 points for having a website or Shopify store.
  • Instagram Presence: +5 points for having an Instagram account.
  • Contact Information: +5 points for having a contact name and +5 points for having an available email.

Lead Tiers

  • HOT LEAD (70-100 points): Prioritize immediately.
  • WARM LEAD (50-69 points): Good fit, include in outreach.
  • COOL LEAD (30-49 points): Lower priority, nurture.
  • COLD LEAD (<30 points): Skip or revisit later.

Output Format

When qualifying a lead, the ICP Qualifier Skill provides a detailed report that includes:

  • Lead Qualification: The brand name and ICP score.
  • Scoring Breakdown: A table detailing the points assigned for each criterion.
  • Key Observations: Specific pain points identified, visual quality assessment, and opportunity angle.
  • Recommended Outreach Hook: A personalized hook for direct messages or emails.

Research Process

The ICP Qualifier Skill follows a structured research process when given a brand to qualify:

  • Website Check: Look for Shopify indicators, product imagery quality, and price points.
  • Instagram Review: Evaluate follower count, content quality, and engagement.
  • Visual Quality Assessment: Determine if the visuals are amateur or professional and check for consistency.
  • Contact Information: Find the founder’s name and email if available.
  • Location Verification: Confirm the brand is based in the United States.

Disqualification Triggers

Certain triggers can automatically disqualify a lead, classifying them as a COLD LEAD. These include:

  • Already having premium studio photography.
  • Being an Amazon-only seller with no DTC presence.
  • Falling outside the target verticals entirely.
  • Being an international brand with no US presence.
  • Having fewer than 1K followers (too early stage).
  • Having over 100K followers (likely has an agency or in-house team).

Benefits of Using the ICP Qualifier Skill

The ICP Qualifier Skill offers several benefits for businesses:

  • Efficient Lead Prioritization: Helps businesses focus on the most promising leads, increasing the likelihood of successful outreach.
  • Data-Driven Decisions: Provides objective criteria for evaluating leads, reducing reliance on gut feelings or subjective judgments.
  • Scalability: Can be applied to a large number of leads quickly and consistently, making it ideal for scaling outreach efforts.
  • Resource Optimization: Ensures that marketing and sales resources are allocated effectively, targeting leads with the highest potential for conversion.

Conclusion

The ICP Qualifier Skill in OpenClaw is a valuable tool for businesses looking to streamline their lead qualification process. By using a structured scoring system and detailed criteria, it helps companies identify and prioritize their ideal customer profiles, ensuring that outreach efforts are targeted, efficient, and effective.
