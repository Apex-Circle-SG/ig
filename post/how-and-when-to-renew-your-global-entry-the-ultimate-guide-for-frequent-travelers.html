How and When to Renew Your Global Entry: The Ultimate Guide for Frequent Travelers

For the frequent international traveler, Global Entry is not just a convenience—it is a necessity. It turns the exhausting experience of returning to the United States from abroad into a breezy, minutes-long process. However, the system is not designed for permanent usage. Like all Trusted Traveler Programs, it requires periodic renewal to maintain your status. Missing your renewal window can lead to frustration, long security lines, and the loss of your precious TSA PreCheck benefits.

Understanding the Global Entry Renewal Timeline

Many travelers operate under the misconception that they must wait until their expiration date to initiate the renewal process. This is a costly mistake. The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has streamlined the process, but bureaucratic delays are still possible.

The Golden Rule of Renewal

You can submit your Global Entry renewal application up to **one year prior** to your current expiration date. It is highly recommended to mark your calendar and begin the process exactly 12 months out if you want to ensure zero interruption in your benefits.

What Happens If You Renew Early?

A common fear is that by renewing early, you lose the remaining time on your current membership. Fortunately, this is not the case. If you renew your membership before it expires, your new five-year period will be added to the end of your current membership period. There is no penalty for proactive maintenance.

The Renewal Process Step-by-Step

Renewing your Global Entry is significantly easier than the initial application. Because the government already has your biometric and background data on file, the process is usually expedited.

  • Log into TTP: Access your account through the Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) website.
  • Verify Information: Ensure your passport details, address, and employment information are current.
  • Pay the Fee: The current renewal fee is $120. *Pro-tip: Many premium credit cards cover this fee—check your benefits before paying with your personal funds.*
  • Submit and Wait: Once submitted, your application will undergo another background check.

Do You Need a New Interview?

This is the question on every traveler’s mind. The good news is that for the vast majority of applicants, **an interview is not required for renewal.** However, conditional approval is at the discretion of U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP). If your information has changed significantly, or if CBP requires additional clarification, you may be asked to schedule an interview at an enrollment center or via Enrollment on Arrival (EoA).

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

Even a straightforward renewal can go sideways if you aren’t careful. Here are common mistakes that cause delays:

1. Incorrect Passport Information

If you have received a new passport since your last renewal, ensure it is updated in your profile. Using an expired passport number in your application is a guaranteed way to trigger a review delay.

2. Ignoring the ‘Conditional Approval’ Status

If your application status changes to ‘Conditionally Approved,’ it means you need to take action. This might mean scheduling an interview. Do not ignore emails from the TTP portal, as they often go to spam folders.

3. Neglecting to Link Your Membership to TSA PreCheck

Global Entry automatically includes TSA PreCheck, but only if your Known Traveler Number (KTN) is correctly entered into your airline reservations. If you let your membership lapse, you will immediately lose your KTN status.

What to Do If Your Renewal Is Pending

CBP has implemented a grace period. If you submit your renewal application *before* your current membership expires, your Global Entry benefits will be extended for up to 24 months while your renewal application is being processed. This grace period is a lifesaver for travelers facing backlogs.

Conclusion: Proactive Renewal is the Best Strategy

Global Entry is an investment in your time and peace of mind. By renewing early—specifically one year out—you eliminate the risk of losing your status. Keep your profile updated, monitor your email, and take advantage of the grace period if necessary. With these steps, you can ensure that your path through customs remains as efficient as ever.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I renew my Global Entry after it expires?

Yes, you can apply for renewal after your membership expires, but you will not receive the grace period benefits. You will effectively be reapplying, and you may face a long wait time without active benefits.

Is the fee for Global Entry changing?

Fees for Trusted Traveler Programs are subject to periodic review by DHS. Always check the official TTP website for the most current pricing at the time of your application.

Does the Global Entry renewal fee cover my spouse or children?

No, the fee is per individual. Every family member, regardless of age, must have their own Global Entry membership and pay the individual fee.

How do I find my Known Traveler Number (KTN)?

Your KTN is your Global Entry membership number. You can find it by logging into your account on the Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) website or by checking the back of your Global Entry card.

How long does the renewal process take?

Processing times vary drastically based on backlog volume. Some renewals are approved in days, while others take months. This is why submitting as early as possible—up to one year in advance—is essential.
