Understanding Fragrance Throw

When shopping for candles, you will often hear the term fragrance throw. This refers to the ability of the candle to disperse its scent throughout a room. Cold throw is how the candle smells when unlit, while hot throw describes the strength of the scent once the wax starts melting. For a candle to fill your entire home, you need a high-quality blend of natural waxes and concentrated fragrance oils. Tips for Maximizing Your Candle Life

  • Trim the Wick: Always trim your wick to 1/4 inch before lighting to prevent soot buildup and ensure an even burn.
  • The First Burn: Allow the candle to burn until the wax pool reaches the edges of the container for at least two hours to prevent tunneling.
  • Placement Matters: Avoid placing candles in drafty areas like near open windows or air conditioning vents, as this disperses the scent too quickly.
  • Burn Time Limits: Do not burn your candle for more than four hours at a time to maintain the structural integrity of the container.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I make my candle scent last longer?

To maximize the scent throw, ensure your candle is placed in a central location within your home. Additionally, keeping the lid on when the candle is not in use helps preserve the fragrance oils, preventing them from evaporating into the air unnecessarily.

Why is my candle not filling the room?

If your candle isn’t filling the room, it may be due to the size of the wicks or the quality of the wax. If the wax pool is too small, the fragrance oil cannot evaporate sufficiently into the air. Try a candle with a wider diameter or a dual-wick system for larger spaces.

Are these candles safe to burn for hours?

While these candles are high quality, you should never leave a burning candle unattended. Always stick to the 3-4 hour burn rule to prevent the vessel from overheating and to ensure the most effective scent dispersion.

Do soy candles have a better throw than paraffin?

Historically, paraffin had a better hot throw, but modern blended waxes (soy, coconut, and paraffin) often provide the best of both worlds: a clean, sustainable burn with an excellent, long-reaching fragrance throw.
