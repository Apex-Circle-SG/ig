Global Entry for Kids: The Ultimate Guide to Expedited Travel for Families

2026-04-29

Understanding Global Entry for Minors

Traveling internationally with children can be a stressful experience, especially when navigating long customs and immigration lines after a tiring flight. Many parents are surprised to learn that Global Entry is not just for adults. The U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) allows children of all ages to apply for the program, providing families with a seamless travel experience. By enrolling your children, you can bypass traditional queues and get through the airport faster, allowing more time for vacation and less time waiting in security.

It is a common misconception that children do not need their own program membership if their parents are already enrolled. However, Global Entry benefits do not automatically extend to family members. To enjoy the expedited screening process, every traveler—regardless of age—must have their own individual membership. This guide covers the essential requirements and steps to secure this convenience for your family.

Eligibility Requirements for Children

There is no minimum age requirement to apply for Global Entry. Whether you are traveling with a newborn or a teenager, your child is eligible to participate in the program. However, there are specific criteria that must be met for a successful application. Primarily, your child must be a U.S. citizen, a lawful permanent resident, or a citizen of a country that has an existing arrangement with the United States for the program.

Furthermore, children must have a clean background. Since the application process includes a thorough background check, any criminal history or violations of customs and immigration laws may result in a denial. Parents should also ensure that their child has a valid passport, as this document is essential for the application and the subsequent biometric verification process.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Applying for your child is a straightforward process, but it requires attention to detail. Follow these steps to ensure your application is submitted correctly:

  • Create a Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) Account: Each applicant must have their own TTP account. For minors, parents or legal guardians can manage the account, but it must be tied to the child’s identity.
  • Complete the Application: Log in to the TTP website and fill out the detailed application form. You will need to provide personal information, including passport details and residential history.
  • Pay the Non-Refundable Fee: There is a processing fee for each applicant. Note that some premium travel credit cards offer a statement credit for Global Entry fees, which can help offset the cost for the entire family.
  • Wait for Conditional Approval: Once the application is submitted, CBP will conduct a background check. This process can take several weeks or even months.
  • Schedule the Interview: After receiving conditional approval, you must schedule an interview at an enrollment center. Both the parent or guardian and the child must attend this appointment.

Preparing Your Child for the Interview

The Global Entry interview is a mandatory step. While it is not an interrogation, it is designed to verify the information provided in the application and capture biometric data, such as fingerprints and a photograph. For younger children, the interview process is usually quick and simple. The officer will typically ask a few basic questions to confirm the child’s identity and travel habits.

To make the experience go smoothly, explain the process to your children beforehand. Let them know that they will be meeting an officer who will ask a few questions and take a picture. Keeping the atmosphere calm and positive will help your child feel comfortable during the appointment. Remember to bring original documents, including the child’s birth certificate, passport, and proof of residency for the parent or guardian.

Essential Tips for Success

Securing Global Entry for your children is an investment in your family’s travel future. Here are some pro-tips to ensure a smooth experience:

  • Check Credit Card Benefits: Before paying the fee, check if any of your credit cards provide a reimbursement for TTP fees. Many travel cards cover this cost every four to five years.
  • Use Enrollment on Arrival (EoA): If you are already planning an international trip, you can complete the interview portion of the process at many major airports upon your return to the U.S. This is often more convenient than booking a separate appointment at an enrollment center.
  • Keep Records Organized: Maintain a folder with all your children’s travel documents, including their Global Entry confirmation numbers. This makes the check-in process at the airport much faster.
  • Monitor Renewal Dates: Global Entry memberships are valid for five years. Set a reminder to renew your children’s memberships well in advance to avoid any lapse in coverage.

The Benefits of Global Entry and TSA PreCheck

One of the most significant advantages of having Global Entry for children is that it typically includes TSA PreCheck. This allows your family to use the expedited security lanes at U.S. airports. In these lanes, children under 12 are generally not required to remove their shoes, light jackets, or belts, and they do not need to take out their laptops or liquids from their carry-on bags. This convenience significantly reduces the stress of navigating through security checkpoints with young children in tow.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do children really need their own Global Entry membership?

Yes. Every individual traveler, regardless of age, must have their own active Global Entry membership to use the expedited lanes. Parents’ memberships do not cover children.

Is there a reduced fee for children?

Currently, the fee for children is the same as the fee for adults. There is no discounted rate for minors, but the long-term benefits of faster processing often outweigh the initial cost.

What if my child does not have a passport yet?

A passport is a requirement for the Global Entry application. You must apply for and receive your child’s passport before you can submit their Global Entry application.

Can a child be denied Global Entry?

Yes. While rare for children, an application can be denied if the background check reveals issues or if the application contains inaccurate information. Ensure all details are double-checked before submission.

How long does the approval process take?

The time frame for conditional approval varies significantly based on current volume. It can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months, so it is best to apply well in advance of any planned international travel.

What happens if I lose my child’s membership card?

If the physical card is lost, you can request a replacement through your TTP account portal. The digital membership remains valid, but having the physical card is always recommended for international travel.
