6 Best Project Management Software with Time Tracking to Boost Productivity

2026-05-03

Why Integrating Time Tracking into Project Management is Essential

In the modern digital workplace, the ability to track time accurately is not just about logging hours; it is about understanding how your team spends their most valuable resource. When you combine project management software with native time tracking, you eliminate the friction of switching between multiple applications. This integration allows project managers to gain real-time visibility into project health, identify bottlenecks, and ensure that budgets remain on track. By leveraging project management software with time tracking, businesses can move from guesswork to data-driven decision-making.

1. Monday.com: The All-in-One Visual Powerhouse

Monday.com has evolved into one of the most versatile platforms on the market. Its visual interface makes it incredibly easy to manage complex workflows while keeping an eye on the clock. The native time tracking column allows users to start and stop timers directly within a task item.

Key Features for Time Tracking

  • Automated Time Tracking: Easily switch timers on or off and generate detailed reports based on the tracked data.
  • Custom Dashboards: Visualize hours spent across different departments or specific projects.
  • Seamless Integration: Connects with external accounting tools to make client invoicing a breeze.

By using Monday.com, teams can easily see which tasks are consuming the most time, allowing managers to reallocate resources effectively before a deadline is missed.

2. ClickUp: The Customization King

ClickUp markets itself as the one app to replace them all, and its robust time tracking suite lives up to the claim. Whether you are a solo freelancer or part of a large enterprise, ClickUp offers granular control over how time is logged and analyzed.

Why ClickUp Stands Out

  • Global Time Tracking: You can track time from anywhere in the platform, including via browser extensions and mobile apps.
  • Time Estimates vs. Actuals: Compare your initial project estimates against the actual time spent to improve future planning.
  • Detailed Reporting: Generate timesheets and custom reports that can be exported directly for payroll processing.

The ability to label time entries with tags makes it simple to categorize work into billable and non-billable hours, which is a game-changer for agencies.

3. Wrike: Best for Enterprise-Level Scalability

Wrike is a heavy hitter in the project management space, particularly for teams that require high levels of security, reporting, and customization. Its time tracking features are designed to support complex, multi-layered projects where accurate accounting is non-negotiable.

Wrike Time Tracking Capabilities

  • Timelog View: A dedicated view that displays all time entries in a single, organized list.
  • Advanced Analytics: Use Wrike’s reporting engine to identify which phases of a project are the least efficient.
  • Permission Controls: Ensure that only authorized personnel can edit or delete time entries.

Wrike is ideal for organizations that need to maintain strict audit trails for client work and internal project accounting.

4. Teamwork: Built for Client-Facing Agencies

Teamwork was built from the ground up with the agency model in mind. If your business depends on billable hours, this platform is likely your best choice. It simplifies the transition from tracking time to sending an invoice.

The Teamwork Advantage

  • Invoicing Integration: Convert tracked time directly into invoices within the platform.
  • Profitability Tracking: Calculate the exact profitability of a project by comparing costs against tracked hours.
  • Task-Level Tracking: Ensure that every small sub-task is accounted for to prevent revenue leakage.

By keeping project management and billing in the same ecosystem, Teamwork helps agencies reduce administrative overhead significantly.

5. Zoho Projects: The Value-Driven Choice

For small to medium-sized businesses looking for a cost-effective solution, Zoho Projects is a top contender. It is part of the broader Zoho ecosystem, meaning it integrates perfectly with Zoho Books and other essential business tools.

Key Benefits for Teams

  • Timesheet Management: Easily approve or reject timesheets submitted by team members.
  • Budgeting Tools: Set project budgets based on hours and receive alerts when you are nearing the limit.
  • Mobile Accessibility: Track time on the go using the dedicated mobile application.

Zoho Projects offers a clean, straightforward interface that makes it easy for team members to log their time without needing extensive training.

6. Harvest + Asana (The Integration Route)

While Asana is a world-class project management tool, it does not have a native, full-featured time tracker. However, the integration between Asana and Harvest is so seamless that it is widely considered one of the best ways to manage time for Asana power users.

Why Use the Integration?

  • Best-in-Class Tracking: Harvest provides a superior user experience for time entry and reporting.
  • Synchronization: Your tasks from Asana appear in Harvest, allowing you to log time without leaving your workflow.
  • Visual Reporting: Harvest’s reporting dashboard is specialized for productivity and profitability analysis.

This combination is perfect for teams that love the Asana interface but require the specialized accounting features that Harvest provides.

How to Choose the Right Tool for Your Team

Choosing the right project management software with time tracking depends on your specific business requirements. Ask yourself these questions: Do you need to generate invoices directly from your time logs? Are you managing a small team or a global enterprise? Do you need deep analytical reports, or is a simple timer sufficient? Prioritize software that integrates with your existing tech stack, such as your accounting or CRM software. Always opt for a free trial to ensure the user interface is intuitive for your team members, as adoption is the biggest hurdle to successful time tracking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should my team track time if we are on a fixed-price contract?

Even on fixed-price projects, tracking time is vital for internal profitability analysis. It helps you understand if the project was truly profitable or if you spent more hours than anticipated, which helps you price future projects more accurately.

Is it difficult to get employees to adopt time tracking?

It can be a cultural shift. The best way to encourage adoption is to choose a tool with a low-friction interface, such as a browser extension or a one-click timer button. Explain the benefits to the team, such as how it prevents burnout and helps in resource planning.

Can I track time on my phone?

Yes, all the platforms listed above offer mobile applications that allow you to start and stop timers, log hours, and view project progress while on the move, which is ideal for remote and hybrid teams.

Does time tracking software help with payroll?

Absolutely. Most of the platforms mentioned allow you to export detailed timesheets that can be used by your HR or accounting department to streamline the payroll process and ensure accuracy for hourly employees.

How do I know if the software is secure?

Reputable project management platforms prioritize data security, offering features like two-factor authentication, data encryption, and GDPR compliance. Always check the security documentation on the vendor’s website before committing to a subscription.
