Global Entry for Kids: The Ultimate Parent’s Guide to Stress-Free Family Travel

For parents, the dream of traveling internationally often comes with the nightmare of endless customs lines after an exhausting long-haul flight. If you are a frequent traveler, you likely already possess Global Entry, the Department of Homeland Security program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers. But did you know that Global Entry for kids is not only possible but highly recommended? In this guide, we break down everything you need to know about enrolling your children in this program to ensure your next family vacation is as seamless as possible.

Why Should Children Have Global Entry?

Many parents wonder if it is truly worth the effort and expense to get their children enrolled in a Trusted Traveler Program. The answer is a resounding yes. If you travel internationally as a family, having only one parent enrolled in Global Entry can lead to significant logistical challenges. Without their own membership, children cannot use the Global Entry kiosks with you. This forces the family to split up: one parent goes through the expedited line, while the other waits in the standard customs line with the children. By enrolling your children, you ensure the entire family stays together, moving through security and customs at the speed of the most efficient traveler.

The Added Bonus: TSA PreCheck

One of the most frequently asked questions regarding Global Entry is whether it includes TSA PreCheck. The answer is yes. Every Global Entry membership comes with TSA PreCheck benefits. This means your children will also enjoy expedited domestic security screening, allowing them to keep their shoes on and laptops inside their bags. This is a game-changer for parents trying to manage toddlers, strollers, and carry-ons while navigating chaotic airport security lines.

How to Apply for Global Entry for Kids

The application process for minors is identical to that of adults. While the paperwork might seem daunting, breaking it down into steps makes it manageable. Here is the path to getting your child approved:

1. Create a Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) Account

Every applicant, including infants, must have their own individual TTP account. Do not try to add your child to your account. You will need a unique email address for each child. If your child does not have an email, creating a dedicated one for them is the first step.

2. Complete the Application

Log in to the child’s account and complete the Global Entry application. Be prepared to provide details about their address, employment (for infants/children, simply list ‘None’), and travel history. Accuracy is critical, as any discrepancy can result in a denial or significant delays.

3. Pay the Non-Refundable Fee

As of recent updates, the application fee for Global Entry is $120. Keep in mind that some premium travel credit cards offer a statement credit for Global Entry or TSA PreCheck application fees. Check your credit card benefits to see if you can be reimbursed for these costs.

4. Wait for Conditional Approval

After submission, your application enters the review phase. This can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months. Once you receive ‘Conditional Approval’ in your TTP portal, you can proceed to the final step: the interview.

Mastering the Global Entry Interview for Minors

The interview is often the part that makes parents the most nervous, but it is typically a very straightforward process. Even infants must attend their interview in person.

  • Required Documents: You must bring the child’s physical passport, proof of residency (such as a utility bill or parent’s driver’s license), and the appointment confirmation.
  • Who Must Attend: Both parents or legal guardians are usually encouraged to attend, although one is sufficient as long as you have the proper documentation. The child must be present.
  • What to Expect: The CBP officer will ask basic questions to verify the child’s identity and travel plans. It is not an interrogation; it is a routine verification.

Tips for a Smooth Enrollment Experience

To maximize your chances of a quick approval and a stress-free interview, consider these expert tips:

  • Use the Enrollment on Arrival (EoA) Program: You do not necessarily need to schedule a separate interview appointment. If you are returning from an international trip, you can complete your interview at participating airports upon arrival. Check the CBP website for locations that offer EoA.
  • Check Your Credit Card Benefits: Use a card like the Chase Sapphire Reserve or the Platinum Card from American Express to pay the fee. Many of these cards will cover the $120 fee for each family member.
  • Keep Your Information Updated: If your child gets a new passport, ensure you update their profile in the TTP portal immediately to avoid issues at the border.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do infants need their own Global Entry membership?

Yes. Every individual, regardless of age, must have their own Global Entry membership to use the expedited lanes. There is no ‘family plan’ that covers children under their parents’ membership.

How long does Global Entry for kids last?

Global Entry memberships are valid for five years. After that, you will need to renew the membership following a similar process to the initial application.

What if my child is denied?

If your child is denied, you will receive a notification in your TTP account. You have the right to appeal the decision by writing to the Trusted Traveler Ombudsman. However, denials for children are rare unless there is a specific legal or security concern.

Can my child use TSA PreCheck even if they aren’t enrolled?

Children 12 and under can generally accompany a parent with TSA PreCheck through the lane, but this policy can vary by airport and is not guaranteed for older children. Enrolling them in Global Entry is the only way to ensure they have their own Known Traveler Number (KTN) and consistent access.
