Twist Bioscience Expands Clonal Genes Offering to Include Long and Complex Sequences

2026-05-12

Revolutionizing Synthetic Biology with Advanced DNA Synthesis

In the rapidly evolving landscape of synthetic biology, the ability to rapidly and accurately read and write DNA is the cornerstone of innovation. Twist Bioscience, a leader in the field of synthetic DNA, has recently announced a significant expansion of its clonal genes offering. This strategic move aims to include longer and more complex sequences, providing researchers with the tools necessary to tackle some of the most challenging projects in genomics, drug discovery, and metabolic engineering.

Why Length and Complexity Matter in Gene Synthesis

Historically, the synthesis of long and complex DNA sequences has been plagued by errors, high costs, and lengthy turnaround times. Traditional methods often struggle with repetitive regions, high GC content, and secondary structures that can impede the performance of polymerase chain reaction (PCR) and cloning processes. By expanding its capabilities, Twist Bioscience is addressing these technical bottlenecks, enabling scientists to design and construct larger genetic circuits and entire pathways with unprecedented precision.

The Impact of Enhanced Clonal Gene Capabilities

The expansion of the clonal genes portfolio is more than just a technical upgrade; it is a catalyst for scientific discovery. When researchers can access high-quality, long-form DNA, the scope of their experiments expands significantly.

Accelerating Therapeutic Development

In the realm of drug discovery, the ability to synthesize large antibody libraries and complex therapeutic proteins is essential. Longer sequences allow for the engineering of larger, more sophisticated molecules that can target diseases with higher specificity. By reducing the time and cost associated with obtaining these sequences, Twist Bioscience is effectively accelerating the biopharmaceutical pipeline.

Advancing Metabolic Engineering and Biofuels

Metabolic engineering often requires the integration of entire biosynthetic pathways into host organisms. These pathways frequently involve long, intricate DNA sequences that were previously difficult to synthesize in a single go. With this new offering, researchers can now order comprehensive pathway designs, facilitating more efficient metabolic engineering and the development of sustainable biofuels and specialty chemicals.

Improving Genomic Research and Synthetic Genomics

The field of synthetic genomics relies on the synthesis of large DNA constructs, sometimes spanning entire viral or bacterial genomes. The ability to synthesize long and complex sequences with high fidelity ensures that the resulting organisms function as intended, minimizing the need for extensive error correction and iterative synthesis cycles.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are clonal genes and why are they important?

Clonal genes are synthesized DNA sequences that have been cloned into a vector. They are important because they provide a stable, accurate, and ready-to-use source of genetic material for downstream applications like transformation, sequencing, or expression.

How does Twist Bioscience ensure the accuracy of long sequences?

Twist Bioscience utilizes proprietary error correction technologies and rigorous quality control measures, including next-generation sequencing, to verify that every base pair in the final construct matches the design provided by the customer.

Can I order sequences with high GC content?

Yes. One of the primary benefits of the expanded offering is the improved ability to synthesize complex, difficult-to-sequence regions, including those with high GC content or repetitive elements that are traditionally challenging for standard synthesis platforms.

What is the typical turnaround time for these long and complex sequences?

While turnaround times can vary based on the length and complexity of the order, Twist Bioscience is committed to maintaining industry-leading speed. The digital ordering platform provides estimated shipping dates at the time of purchase, ensuring transparency for project planning.

How does this expansion benefit academic researchers?

Academic researchers often operate under tight budget constraints and need reliable, high-quality results. By making long and complex sequences more accessible and affordable, Twist Bioscience enables academic labs to pursue more ambitious research projects without the need for expensive in-house synthesis capabilities.
