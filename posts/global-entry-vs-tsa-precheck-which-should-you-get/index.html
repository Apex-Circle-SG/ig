Global Entry vs. TSA PreCheck: Which Should You Get?

2026-05-06

Understanding the Basics: What Are These Programs?

Frequent travelers know that the biggest bottleneck in any journey is often the airport security checkpoint. To streamline this process, the U.S. government offers two primary trusted traveler programs: TSA PreCheck and Global Entry. While both aim to make your travel experience smoother, they serve slightly different purposes and offer distinct benefits. Choosing the right one depends largely on your travel frequency and whether you primarily travel domestically or internationally.

What is TSA PreCheck?

TSA PreCheck is a program designed to expedite the security screening process at domestic airports within the United States. When you are a member, you gain access to dedicated security lanes where you do not need to remove your shoes, belt, light jacket, laptop, or 3-1-1 liquids bag. This can save significant time during peak travel hours and reduces the stress associated with standard security lines. It is currently available at more than 200 airports across the U.S. and involves over 90 participating airlines.

What is Global Entry?

Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States. When you return from an international trip, instead of waiting in long lines to see an immigration officer, you can head to a Global Entry kiosk. You scan your passport or resident card, provide your fingerprints, and complete your customs declaration in a matter of minutes. Crucially, Global Entry membership includes TSA PreCheck benefits, making it a comprehensive package for international travelers.

The Core Differences: A Side-by-Side Comparison

To make an informed decision, it is essential to look at the differences in application, cost, and utility. While both programs require a background check and an interview, the scope of their utility is the defining factor.

  • TSA PreCheck is strictly for domestic security screening. It does not assist with customs or immigration upon returning to the U.S. from abroad.
  • Global Entry provides the full benefits of TSA PreCheck for your departing flights while also providing expedited entry when returning to the U.S. from international destinations.
  • Cost: TSA PreCheck typically costs $78 for a five-year membership, whereas Global Entry is $120 for five years.
  • Application Process: TSA PreCheck often has a simpler enrollment process, whereas Global Entry requires a more rigorous application and an in-person interview with a CBP officer.

Which Program Should You Choose?

Choosing between these two programs is usually a matter of your travel habits. If your travel is restricted to domestic flights within the United States, TSA PreCheck is likely the most cost-effective and efficient solution for you. It covers all the essential needs for domestic security screening at a lower price point and with a faster, easier application process.

However, if you take even one or two international trips per year, Global Entry is almost always the better investment. The price difference between the two is relatively small when spread over the five-year duration of the membership. By choosing Global Entry, you gain the convenience of bypassing long customs lines, which can sometimes take an hour or more during busy arrival times, while still enjoying the TSA PreCheck benefits for your domestic travel.

Key Factors to Consider Before Applying

Before you jump into the application process, consider these three pillars of decision-making: your budget, your travel frequency, and your destination habits.

1. Budget and Credit Card Perks

Many premium travel credit cards now offer a statement credit for the application fee of either TSA PreCheck or Global Entry. If you have a travel-focused credit card, check your benefits portal. You may find that you can get your membership paid for by your credit card issuer, which effectively negates the cost difference between the two programs.

2. The Interview Process

TSA PreCheck interviews are often available at a wider variety of locations, including many retail centers. Global Entry interviews are conducted at major airports or enrollment centers and can be harder to schedule due to high demand. If you live far from a major international airport, the logistics of a Global Entry interview might be a hurdle to consider.

3. Future Travel Plans

Think about your plans for the next five years. Even if you only travel domestically right now, consider whether you might take an international vacation in the near future. It is much easier to apply for Global Entry from the start than it is to upgrade from TSA PreCheck later.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Global Entry include TSA PreCheck?

Yes, Global Entry membership automatically includes the benefits of TSA PreCheck. You will receive a Known Traveler Number (KTN) that you can add to your airline reservations to ensure you get the PreCheck status on your boarding pass.

Can I get a refund if my application is denied?

No, the application fees for both TSA PreCheck and Global Entry are non-refundable. The fee covers the processing of your application and the background check, regardless of the final approval decision.

How long is the membership valid?

Both TSA PreCheck and Global Entry memberships are valid for five years from the date of approval. You should begin the renewal process several months before your expiration date to avoid any lapse in coverage.

Is there an age limit for these programs?

There is no minimum age requirement for either program. However, children under 12 can often accompany their parents through the TSA PreCheck lane without needing their own membership, provided they are on the same reservation. For Global Entry, every individual, including infants, must have their own membership.

What happens if I forget my Known Traveler Number?

You can find your KTN by logging into your account on the official Department of Homeland Security or Trusted Traveler Program websites. It is also often printed on your membership card.

Final Thoughts on Trusted Traveler Programs

Investing in a trusted traveler program is one of the best ways to improve your airport experience. By reducing the time spent in lines and minimizing the stress of security and customs, you can focus on what really matters: enjoying your trip. If you are a frequent traveler, the modest cost of these programs pays for itself within just a few flights. Evaluate your travel patterns, check your credit card benefits, and start your application today to ensure your next journey is as seamless as possible.
