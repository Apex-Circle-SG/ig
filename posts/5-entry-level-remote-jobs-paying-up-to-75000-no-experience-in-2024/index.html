5 Entry-Level Remote Jobs Paying Up To $75,000 (No Experience) In 2024

2026-05-07

The Rise of High-Paying Remote Opportunities

In 2024, the landscape of employment has shifted dramatically. The traditional office environment is no longer the only pathway to a successful, well-paying career. For job seekers looking to enter the workforce or transition into a new field, the remote work sector offers incredible potential. Many believe that high salaries are reserved for those with decades of experience, but that is simply not the case. With the right skill set and a proactive approach, you can land entry-level remote jobs that pay up to $75,000 per year.

This guide explores five specific roles that prioritize adaptability, soft skills, and technological proficiency over years of industry experience. Whether you are a recent graduate, a career changer, or simply looking for more flexibility, these positions provide a stable foundation for a lucrative future.

1. Customer Success Specialist

A Customer Success Specialist is more than just a support representative. While traditional support focuses on fixing problems, customer success focuses on the entire lifecycle of the client. Your goal is to ensure the user gets the maximum value out of a product or service. Companies are willing to pay a premium for empathetic, organized individuals who can keep clients happy and retained.

Why This Role Pays Well

Because customer retention is vital to the bottom line of SaaS (Software as a Service) companies, they invest heavily in teams that build relationships. Entry-level salaries often start in the $50,000 range and can quickly climb to $75,000 as you manage larger accounts or more complex software portfolios.

Skills You Need

  • Strong verbal and written communication.
  • Basic technical aptitude to learn new software platforms.
  • Problem-solving mindset and patience.
  • Ability to manage multiple client accounts simultaneously.

2. Sales Development Representative (SDR)

If you have a competitive spirit and a knack for persuasion, the role of an SDR is perhaps the fastest way to hit a $75,000 income. An SDR is responsible for identifying potential customers, reaching out through email or phone, and qualifying leads for the sales team. It is the engine room of any growth-oriented business.

The Earning Potential

The beauty of sales is the commission structure. While your base salary might start between $45,000 and $55,000, your on-target earnings (OTE) often push you well into the $75,000 range. Top performers who hit their quotas regularly can earn significantly more, making this one of the most rewarding entry-level roles available.

Skills You Need

  • Resilience in the face of rejection.
  • Exceptional verbal communication and active listening.
  • Organizational skills to track leads in a CRM like Salesforce or HubSpot.
  • A results-oriented mindset.

3. Remote Project Coordinator

Every business is essentially a series of projects. A Project Coordinator ensures that tasks are completed on time, resources are managed effectively, and communication flows between team members. You do not need a formal project management degree to start; you just need to be highly organized and comfortable with digital collaboration tools.

Why Companies Hire Entry-Level

Many organizations look for detail-oriented individuals who can handle the administrative heavy lifting that senior project managers don’t have time for. By proving you can keep a team on track, you demonstrate value immediately. Salaries for these roles are competitive, often starting around $60,000 and scaling up as you take on larger project scopes.

Skills You Need

  • Proficiency in tools like Asana, Trello, or Monday.com.
  • Strong time management and scheduling abilities.
  • Clear, concise documentation skills.
  • The ability to bridge communication gaps between departments.

4. Virtual Executive Assistant

The modern Virtual Executive Assistant (VEA) is a strategic partner to busy founders, CEOs, and high-level executives. This is not just about scheduling appointments; it involves managing email inboxes, coordinating travel, overseeing social media presence, and even handling light bookkeeping. Because you are essentially the ‘right hand’ of a leader, the pay is often much higher than standard administrative roles.

Earning Potential

High-level executives value their time immensely. If you can save them 10 hours a week, they are happy to pay you a salary that reflects that efficiency. Experienced virtual assistants working for multiple clients or high-growth tech founders can easily command $70,000 to $75,000 per year.

Skills You Need

  • Extreme attention to detail and confidentiality.
  • Mastery of Google Workspace and calendar management.
  • Proactive communication (anticipating needs before they are stated).
  • Ability to work autonomously with minimal supervision.

5. Social Media Community Manager

Brands live and die by their online reputation. A Community Manager is the voice of the brand across social media platforms. You will be responsible for engaging with followers, moderating discussions, creating content calendars, and reporting on audience sentiment. Since almost everyone uses social media, the barrier to entry is low, provided you understand how to use these tools for business purposes.

Why it Pays $75,000

Companies are moving away from treating social media as an afterthought. They are hiring dedicated professionals who can turn a ‘like’ into a loyal customer. If you have a portfolio showing how you have grown an audience or managed a brand’s voice, you can negotiate a salary that reflects your ability to drive growth.

Skills You Need

  • Creativity and a strong grasp of current social trends.
  • Experience with analytics tools like Hootsuite or Sprout Social.
  • Strong writing skills for various platforms (Twitter, LinkedIn, TikTok).
  • Ability to maintain a brand voice under pressure.

How to Prepare Your Resume for Remote Work

When applying for these roles, your resume needs to highlight your ability to work independently. Since employers cannot watch over your shoulder in a remote setting, they need to see evidence of self-motivation and digital literacy. Even if your past experience is in retail or hospitality, frame your accomplishments around efficiency, communication, and software usage. Use action verbs and include metrics whenever possible, such as ‘Managed a team of five’ or ‘Reduced scheduling errors by 20%.’

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I really need no experience for these roles?

While some roles prefer a degree, many employers are moving toward a ‘skills-first’ hiring model. If you can demonstrate proficiency in the tools required for the job—such as CRM software, project management boards, or social media analytics—your lack of direct ‘years of experience’ becomes secondary to your actual capabilities.

What is the best way to find these remote jobs?

Use specialized remote-first job boards such as We Work Remotely, FlexJobs, and Remote.co. Additionally, optimize your LinkedIn profile with keywords relevant to the roles mentioned above and set your location to ‘Remote’ to ensure you are appearing in the right searches for recruiters.

How can I stand out in a remote interview?

Preparation is key. Ensure your video conferencing setup is professional, with good lighting and a clean background. During the interview, emphasize your ability to communicate asynchronously, your comfort with remote collaboration tools, and your track record of hitting deadlines without constant supervision.

Are these roles truly remote or hybrid?

The roles listed here are fully remote. However, always double-check the job description to see if the company requires you to reside in a specific country or time zone for tax or collaboration purposes. Being clear about your location and availability is essential during the application process.

Final Thoughts

Landing a high-paying remote job in 2024 is entirely achievable if you target the right roles and present yourself as a professional who can add value from day one. Whether you choose to leverage your communication skills as a Customer Success Specialist or your organizational talents as a Project Coordinator, the path to a $75,000 income is open. Stay persistent, refine your digital skills, and start applying today.
