Why Rey Took The Skywalker Name In Star Wars, Explained

2026-05-15

The Emotional Weight of the Final Scene in The Rise of Skywalker

The conclusion of the Star Wars Skywalker Saga marked the end of an era for fans worldwide. After nine films spanning over four decades, the galaxy far, far away bid farewell to the lineage that had defined its core narrative. However, the final moments of Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker introduced a surprising twist that sparked intense debate and discussion across the fandom. When Rey, standing on the sands of Tatooine, responds to a stranger’s question about her identity by declaring herself Rey Skywalker, she fundamentally alters the legacy of the saga. This decision was not merely a plot point but a thematic culmination of her entire journey, representing the triumph of choice over bloodline and the redefining of what it means to be part of a family.

Understanding why Rey chose this specific name requires a deep dive into her character arc, the spiritual mechanics of the Force, and the broader philosophical messages director J.J. Abrams and writer Chris Terrio aimed to convey. It is a moment that validates the struggles of an orphan scavenger who found her place not through genetics, but through connection, mentorship, and the sheer will to do good in a universe often dictated by destiny.

The Conflict of Bloodline Versus Chosen Family

Throughout the sequel trilogy, Rey’s lineage serves as a primary source of internal conflict. In The Force Awakens, she is defined by her abandonment, waiting for parents who never returned. By The Last Jedi, the revelation that her parents were nobody significant seemed to liberate her, suggesting that greatness could come from anywhere. However, The Rise of Skywalker complicated this by revealing she is the granddaughter of Emperor Palpatine, the ultimate embodiment of evil in the galaxy. This revelation placed her in a direct parallel to Kylo Ren, who struggled with the weight of being a Skywalker.

The central theme here is the dichotomy between biological destiny and chosen identity. While Kylo Ren tried to kill his past and reject his heritage, only to be consumed by it, Rey had the opposite burden. She carried the blood of the galaxy’s greatest tyrant. By taking the Skywalker name, she rejects the idea that blood determines one’s fate. She asserts that family is not defined by DNA but by who supports you, who teaches you, and who you love. The Skywalkers, despite their flaws and tragedies, represented the light side of the Force and the fight for freedom. By adopting their name, Rey aligns herself with their ideals rather than her grandfather’s atrocities.

The Influence of Luke and Leia

Rey’s decision is heavily influenced by her relationships with the last surviving members of the original trio. Luke Skywalker, initially hesitant to train her, eventually becomes her mentor in the ways of the Jedi. Though their time together was brief and fraught with ideological differences regarding the Jedi Order, Luke ultimately sacrifices himself to save the Resistance and inspire the galaxy. He passes the torch to Rey, not as a blood relative, but as a successor.

Even more pivotal is her relationship with General Leia Organa. Leia becomes Rey’s true mother figure, training her in the ways of the Force during the year between films. It is Leia’s unconditional love and guidance that ground Rey when the pull of the dark side threatens to consume her. When Leia passes away, Rey loses the maternal figure she always渴望 ed. Taking the Skywalker name is, in many ways, a tribute to Leia, ensuring that the Organa-Skywalker line continues spiritually if not biologically. It acknowledges that these two warriors fought for her, believed in her, and shaped her into the Jedi she became.

Spiritual Adoption and the Nature of the Force

In the Star Wars universe, the Force often binds individuals together in ways that transcend physical relation. The concept of spiritual adoption is not new to the franchise; Anakin Skywalker himself was a father figure to Ahsoka Tano, who took on his legacy without his blood. Rey’s situation mirrors this ancient tradition of masters and apprentices forming bonds stronger than those of birth. The Force itself seems to guide this union, weaving the destinies of the Palpatine and Skywalker lines together until they converge in Rey.

When the voices of the past Jedi speak to Rey during her battle against the resurrected Emperor, they encourage her. Among them are the voices of Luke and Leia. This spiritual endorsement signifies that the Jedi of old accept her as one of their own. The Force does not care about her surname; it cares about her actions and her heart. By declaring herself a Skywalker, she is recognizing the truth that the Force has already revealed: she belongs to the light, and the light belongs to the family of those who protect it.

Rejecting the Palpatine Legacy

The weight of the Palpatine name carries the burden of genocide, tyranny, and the dark side. For Rey to keep that name would be to forever associate herself with the man who sought to destroy the Jedi and enslave the galaxy. While she could have chosen to rehabilitate the name or simply go by Rey, the act of taking the Skywalker name is a definitive break from her ancestry. It is a declaration that she will not repeat the mistakes of her grandfather. It symbolizes the ultimate victory of the Skywalker legacy: even the blood of the Emperor can be turned to the light, and the name of Skywalker can rise again through someone who chose it freely.

The Symbolism of Tatooine and the Sunset

The setting of this revelation is as important as the words themselves. Tatooine is the birthplace of Anakin Skywalker and the place where Luke Skywalker grew up. It is the geographical anchor of the entire saga. By returning to this desert planet, Rey completes the circle. She buries the lightsabers of Luke and Leia, the physical symbols of their struggle, in the sand where it all began. This act signifies the end of the old wars and the beginning of a new chapter.

When the stranger asks for her name, the twin suns set in the background, mirroring the iconic imagery from A New Hope. This visual callback reinforces the idea of continuity. Just as Luke looked at those suns dreaming of adventure, Rey looks at them having fulfilled a destiny she chose for herself. The location serves as a sacred ground where the past is honored, and the future is named. It is here, on the soil of the Skywalkers’ origin, that Rey cements her place in history, not as a Palpatine, but as the heir to the Skywalker spirit.

Critical Reception and Fan Discourse

The reaction to Rey taking the Skywalker name was mixed, reflecting the polarized nature of the sequel trilogy itself. Some fans praised the move as a beautiful thematic conclusion, arguing that it perfectly encapsulated the movie’s message that anyone can be a Skywalker if they choose to be. They viewed it as a powerful statement on inclusivity and the idea that heroism is an action, not an inheritance. For these viewers, it was a satisfying emotional payoff to see the orphan girl find a family in the very heroes she admired.

Conversely, other fans felt that the move undermined the significance of the Skywalker bloodline and felt unearned given the complex revelations about her biological parents. Critics argued that it erased the unique identity Rey had built for herself and relied too heavily on the nostalgia of the original trilogy. Despite the debate, there is no denying that the moment cemented Rey’s status as a central figure in the Star Wars mythos. Whether one views it as a triumph of theme or a concession to fan service, it remains the defining moment of her character arc, marking the transition from a scavenger to a legend.

FAQ: Understanding Rey’s Decision

Why did Rey choose the name Skywalker instead of keeping Palpatine?

Rey chose the name Skywalker to symbolize her rejection of her grandfather’s evil legacy and her acceptance of the values held by Luke and Leia. It represents the theme that family is chosen rather than inherited. By taking the name, she aligns herself with the light side of the Force and the heroes who mentored her, effectively severing her ties to the atrocities of the Emperor.

Did Luke and Leia approve of Rey taking their name?

Within the context of the film, the spiritual presence of both Luke and Leia supports Rey. Leia trained Rey personally and loved her as a daughter, while Luke sacrificed himself for her cause. The voices of the past Jedi, including both Luke and Leia, encourage her in her final battle, indicating their spiritual approval of her path and her place within their lineage.

Does taking the name make Rey a biological Skywalker?

No, Rey is not biologically related to the Skywalker family. She is the granddaughter of Emperor Palpatine. Her taking the name is a symbolic and spiritual adoption. It emphasizes the Star Wars philosophy that one’s identity is defined by choices and actions rather than genetics or bloodline.

What happens to the Palpatine name after The Rise of Skywalker?

With the death of Emperor Palpatine and Rey’s refusal to carry his name, the Palpatine bloodline effectively ends its association with power and evil in the galaxy. Rey’s decision ensures that the name Palpatine does not continue to cast a shadow over the future, allowing the galaxy to move forward without the fear of the Emperor’s return through his descendants.

How does this decision impact the future of the Star Wars saga?

This decision opens the door for new stories where the definition of a Jedi or a hero is not limited by lineage. It sets a precedent that the legacy of the Skywalker name is open to anyone who embodies its ideals of courage, sacrifice, and hope. Future narratives can explore Rey building a new Jedi Order based on these inclusive principles, free from the rigid dogmas of the past.
