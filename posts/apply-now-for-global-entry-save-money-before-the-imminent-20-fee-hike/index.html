Apply Now For Global Entry: Beat the Upcoming Fee Increase

For international travelers, nothing ruins the excitement of a trip quite like standing in an endless customs line after a long-haul flight. Global Entry has long been the gold standard for expedited entry into the United States, but significant changes are on the horizon. With a 20% fee hike scheduled, savvy travelers have a narrow window of opportunity to lock in current pricing. In this guide, we will break down why you should act now, how the application process works, and how to maximize your investment in this essential travel program.

Why the Global Entry Price Hike Matters

For years, the cost of Global Entry has remained static, making it an incredibly high-value proposition for frequent flyers. However, the U.S. government has announced a restructuring of fees for Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP), including Global Entry. This increase isn’t just a minor adjustment; it represents a notable jump that will impact the cost-benefit analysis for casual travelers.

By applying before the new rates take effect, you are effectively “future-proofing” your travel budget. Given that a membership is valid for five years, securing it at the current price allows you to amortize that cost over a longer period, making each trip through customs even cheaper.

What Exactly is Global Entry?

Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows pre-approved, low-risk travelers to receive expedited clearance upon arrival into the United States. Instead of waiting for a traditional officer interview, members simply head to a designated Global Entry kiosk or use the mobile app, scan their passport, and proceed.

Key Benefits of Membership

  • Expedited Customs Processing: Skip the long lines and head straight to kiosks.
  • TSA PreCheck Included: Global Entry membership automatically grants you TSA PreCheck benefits, allowing you to breeze through domestic security checkpoints without removing shoes, belts, or laptops.
  • Reduced Wait Times: Members typically clear customs in minutes rather than hours.
  • Global Recognition: While primarily for U.S. entry, the program is recognized by various international partner programs, enhancing your travel experience globally.

The Application Process: Step-by-Step

Many travelers hesitate to apply because they assume the process is overly cumbersome. In reality, it is straightforward if you are organized. Here is how to get started immediately:

1. Create a Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) Account

Everything starts on the official government TTP website. Do not use third-party sites that charge additional “processing fees.” Only the official .gov portal should be used.

2. Fill Out the Comprehensive Application

Be prepared to provide your residential history, employment details, and a record of international travel over the past five years. Accuracy is paramount here; any discrepancies can lead to delays or denials.

3. Pay the Current Fee

This is the most critical step to beat the hike. Once you submit, you are locked into the pricing structure at the time of your application fee payment.

4. The Interview Requirement

Once conditionally approved, you must complete an in-person interview. This is often the biggest bottleneck. To mitigate this, consider “Enrollment on Arrival” (EoA), which allows you to complete your interview while clearing customs after your next international flight.

Is Global Entry Still Worth It?

Even with a price increase, the value proposition of Global Entry remains high for anyone who travels internationally more than once or twice a year. When you factor in the value of the included TSA PreCheck, the cost per trip over five years becomes negligible compared to the time saved and the reduction in travel stress.

Furthermore, if you hold certain premium travel credit cards, you might not have to pay the fee at all. Many high-end travel cards offer a statement credit specifically for Global Entry or TSA PreCheck application fees as a perk.

FAQs About the Global Entry Fee Increase

How much will the new Global Entry fee be?

The fee structure is shifting to reflect the actual cost of processing applicants. While the exact dollar amount can vary based on the final federal regulations, the anticipated increase is approximately 20% over the current standard rate.

Can I renew my membership early?

Yes. If your current membership is nearing expiration, you can renew it within one year of its expiration date. Doing so now is the smartest way to bypass the upcoming fee hike.

Does the fee hike affect TSA PreCheck as well?

TSA PreCheck fees are also subject to adjustment. Because Global Entry bundles TSA PreCheck, applying for Global Entry remains the superior value for travelers who cross borders.

How long does the approval process take?

Processing times vary wildly. While some applicants receive conditional approval in days, others wait months. This is exactly why you should apply as soon as possible—the processing time is entirely out of your control, but the fee increase is something you can beat by applying today.
