The timing of the event, mid-year 2025, is critical. The traditional perimeter-based security models are crumbling. The expo aims to address these specific, evolving challenges by bringing together diverse stakeholders who rarely share a single room.

Core Themes and Agenda Highlights

The two-day agenda is meticulously curated to move beyond theoretical discussions into actionable intelligence. The organizers have identified three pillar themes that define the current security posture of the nation.

Theme 1: AI vs. AI – The New Arms Race

As attackers use Generative AI to craft flawless phishing emails and identify zero-day vulnerabilities, defenders must leverage AI for automated response and predictive analysis. Sessions will cover:

  • Deploying Autonomous Security Operations Centers (SOCs).
  • Ethical considerations of using AI in surveillance and threat detection.
  • Case studies of AI stopping real-time attacks in the BFSI sector.

Theme 2: Securing the Critical Infrastructure

With the proliferation of Smart Cities and connected power grids, Operational Technology (OT) security is paramount. Experts will dissect recent grid failures and offer blueprints for securing SCADA systems against state-sponsored threats.

Theme 3: The Human Firewall and Skill Development

Technology cannot fix human error. A significant portion of the expo is dedicated to cybersecurity awareness, culture building, and addressing the massive skill gap. Workshops will offer certification pathways and live hacking demonstrations to educate attendees on the latest social engineering tactics.
