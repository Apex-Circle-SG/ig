Frequently Asked Questions

How does Weitz Investment Management define value in 2026?

Weitz defines value as the present value of all future cash flows that a business is expected to generate. In 2026, this involves looking past current market sentiment to find companies whose intrinsic worth is higher than their current market price, often focusing on firms with durable competitive advantages.

Why is the margin of safety important to the Weitz strategy?

The margin of safety acts as a buffer against unforeseen events and analytical errors. By purchasing assets at a significant discount to their estimated value, the firm reduces the impact of market volatility and increases the probability of achieving satisfactory long-term returns.

How did the interest rate environment affect Q1 2026 performance?

The interest rate environment influenced sector preference, favoring companies with strong cash flows that are less sensitive to borrowing costs. Weitz navigated this by avoiding companies heavily reliant on cheap debt to fund operations, opting instead for balance-sheet strength.

Does Weitz Investment Management use AI in their analysis?

The firm integrates modern analytical tools to process data efficiently, but the final investment decision remains human-led. Weitz believes that true investment insight comes from qualitative judgment, understanding corporate culture, and assessing long-term business strategy, which algorithms cannot fully replicate.

How can individual investors adopt the Weitz approach?

Individual investors can adopt this approach by focusing on long-term time horizons, conducting thorough research before buying, maintaining a diversified portfolio, and resisting the urge to follow short-term market trends. Consistency and patience are the keys to success in this philosophy.
