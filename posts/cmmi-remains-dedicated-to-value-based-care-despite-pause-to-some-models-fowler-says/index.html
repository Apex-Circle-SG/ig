CMMI Remains Dedicated to Value-Based Care Despite Pause to Some Models, Fowler Says

2026-05-08

The Future of Value-Based Care: Insights from CMMI Leadership

In the rapidly evolving landscape of the United States healthcare system, the Center for Medicare and Medicaid Innovation (CMMI) stands as a pivotal entity tasked with testing new payment and service delivery models. Recently, there has been significant discourse regarding the strategic direction of the agency, particularly following the pause of several high-profile initiatives. However, CMMI Director Liz Fowler has been clear: the commitment to value-based care remains stronger than ever.

As the industry transitions from volume-based fee-for-service models to systems that prioritize patient outcomes and cost-efficiency, the role of CMMI is more critical than ever. Fowler’s recent statements serve as a beacon for providers, payers, and policy experts, signaling that while individual programs may be recalibrated, the overarching mission of the agency remains steadfast.

Understanding the Strategic Pivot

The decision to pause or sunset certain pilot programs is not an abandonment of the value-based care agenda. Instead, it represents a data-driven approach to healthcare innovation. CMMI is currently focusing on refining its evaluation metrics to ensure that the models deployed truly deliver on their promise of better health, better care, and lower costs.

Why Some Models Are Paused

The healthcare market is complex, and not every model yields the desired results in every clinical setting. CMMI has identified several reasons for pausing specific initiatives:

  • Lack of Scalability: Some models show promise in small, controlled environments but fail to translate when expanded across diverse geographic regions.
  • Administrative Burden: Feedback from the provider community indicated that the reporting requirements for certain models were overly burdensome, potentially detracting from patient care.
  • Data Integrity: Ensuring that the metrics used to judge success are accurate and equitable is a top priority for the agency.

By pausing these initiatives, CMMI is performing the necessary due diligence to ensure that future iterations of value-based care programs are sustainable and effective for a wider range of healthcare organizations.

The Core Pillars of the CMMI Vision

Under Liz Fowler’s leadership, CMMI has emphasized a holistic approach to care delivery. The agency is looking beyond simple cost-cutting measures and focusing on the social determinants of health, health equity, and the long-term sustainability of the Medicare program.

Prioritizing Health Equity

A central pillar of the current CMMI strategy is the integration of health equity into every new model. The agency recognizes that historical disparities in care have prevented vulnerable populations from achieving optimal health outcomes. Future models are being designed to incentivize providers to reach underserved communities and address the structural barriers that contribute to poor health.

Streamlining Provider Participation

One of the most significant challenges for healthcare providers is the complexity of participating in multiple value-based payment models. CMMI is working to harmonize these programs, reducing the fragmentation that often plagues clinical workflows. By creating a more predictable and unified framework, the agency hopes to encourage greater participation from independent practices and smaller health systems.

The Impact on the Healthcare Industry

The healthcare industry is watching CMMI closely. For hospitals, primary care physicians, and specialists, the agency’s direction informs long-term strategic planning. Fowler’s reassurance that the core mission remains intact provides the stability necessary for organizations to continue investing in the infrastructure required for value-based transformation.

The Role of Data and Analytics

Successful value-based care relies on robust data analytics. CMMI is increasing its focus on providing providers with the actionable data they need to manage patient populations effectively. This includes insights into chronic disease management, preventive care gaps, and utilization patterns that drive costs. Investing in these capabilities is essential for any organization aiming to thrive in the new payment environment.

Collaboration with Stakeholders

Fowler has emphasized that CMMI cannot succeed in a vacuum. The agency is actively seeking input from patient advocacy groups, provider organizations, and commercial payers. This collaborative approach is intended to foster a sense of shared responsibility for the success of Medicare and the broader healthcare ecosystem.

Navigating the Transition: What Providers Should Know

Transitioning to value-based care is a marathon, not a sprint. While the pause in some CMMI models might seem like a setback, it is actually an opportunity for organizations to focus on foundational improvements. Providers should use this time to strengthen their care coordination efforts, enhance their patient engagement strategies, and refine their financial performance monitoring.

Tips for Future-Proofing Your Practice

  • Invest in Care Coordination: Build multidisciplinary teams that can manage high-risk patients across the continuum of care.
  • Enhance Data Literacy: Ensure that your administrative and clinical staff can interpret and act upon performance data.
  • Focus on Patient Experience: Value-based care is ultimately about the patient. Improving satisfaction scores is a key indicator of quality.
  • Stay Informed: Regularly monitor updates from CMS and CMMI to stay ahead of regulatory changes and new model announcements.

FAQ: Common Questions About CMMI and Value-Based Care

What does the pause of CMMI models mean for current participants?

For most participants, the pause of specific models does not mean an immediate cessation of activities. CMMI typically provides clear transition guidance for ongoing programs. Participants should continue to focus on delivering high-quality care and monitoring their performance metrics as outlined in their specific participation agreements.

Is CMMI moving away from value-based care?

No. CMMI is not moving away from value-based care. The agency remains fully committed to the goal of moving the healthcare system toward models that reward value and quality over volume. The recent pauses are part of a strategic effort to refine models for better long-term outcomes.

How does CMMI plan to address health equity in future models?

CMMI is integrating health equity into its models by requiring participants to collect and report demographic data, incentivizing care for underserved populations, and providing additional support for providers serving in health professional shortage areas.

What is the next step for providers wanting to engage with CMMI?

Providers interested in participating in CMMI initiatives should regularly visit the official CMS Innovation Center website. The agency frequently releases Requests for Information (RFIs) and solicitations for new models, providing opportunities for stakeholders to offer feedback and participate in the design of future payment structures.

How does the focus on value-based care affect patient outcomes?

The primary goal of value-based care is to improve patient outcomes by focusing on preventive care, chronic disease management, and the coordination of care across different settings. This shift helps reduce hospital readmissions, improves management of conditions like diabetes and heart disease, and ultimately leads to a better quality of life for patients.

Conclusion: A Steady Course Forward

The journey toward a value-based healthcare system is complex and filled with challenges, but the destination is clear. As Liz Fowler has articulated, CMMI remains a dedicated partner in this transformation. By learning from past initiatives and refining its approach, the agency is ensuring that the future of Medicare is built on a foundation of quality, equity, and sustainable value. For providers, this means that the path forward is one of continued innovation, data-driven decision-making, and an unwavering commitment to the health of the patients they serve.
