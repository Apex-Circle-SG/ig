What Is CleanSpark? A Deep Dive into America’s Bitcoin Miner and Energy Innovator

2025-05-09

CleanSpark, Inc. (NASDAQ: CLSK) is a leading U.S.-based Bitcoin mining technology and energy infrastructure company operating under the brand “America’s Bitcoin Miner®,” with a diversified portfolio of data centers powered by competitively priced energy sources CleanSpark. As of April 30, 2025, CleanSpark maintained an average hashrate of 40.1 EH/s, mining approximately 21.1 BTC per day and holding a treasury of 12,101 BTC CleanSpark Investors. In Q1 FY 2025, the company reported revenue of $162.3 million (up 120% year-over-year) and net income of $241.7 million, with basic EPS of $0.85 and a marginal cost per coin of $34,000 CleanSpark Investors. Leveraging proprietary immersion cooling and firmware optimizations, CleanSpark aims to reach 50 EH/s of total capacity by mid-2025 through ongoing expansions in Tennessee and Wyoming PR Newswire.

Company Overview

History and Evolution

CleanSpark, Inc. was incorporated on October 15, 1987 in Nevada as SmartData Corporation, later rebranded to Stratean Inc. in 2014, before adopting the CleanSpark name in 2016 after acquiring CleanSpark LLC’s assets SEC. The company originally focused on alternative energy software solutions and data center services before pivoting into Bitcoin mining in 2020 under the leadership of CEO Zachary Bradford MarketWatch. Today, CleanSpark is headquartered in Henderson, Nevada, and operates as a pure-play Bitcoin miner with a commitment to sustainability and community engagement StockAnalysis.

Business Model and Operations

Bitcoin Mining

CleanSpark owns and operates a nationwide network of mining facilities optimized for low-cost, high-efficiency Bitcoin mining, leveraging ASIC rigs at scales that deliver competitive hash rates CleanSpark. In March 2025, the fleet achieved an average hashrate of 40.2 EH/s with an efficiency of 17.03 J/Th, producing an average of 22.78 BTC per day and selling 14.23 BTC at $87,742 each CleanSpark Investors. The company’s strategy balances mining rewards with long-term accumulation, as evidenced by its growing Bitcoin treasury CleanSpark Investors.

Energy and Cooling Solutions

Beyond mining, CleanSpark develops dispatchable compute services that modulate mining loads to support grid stability and optimize energy costs for utility partners SEC. Its immersion cooling technology—deployed first in a 20 MW facility in Norcross, Georgia—reduces noise, improves hardware longevity, and lowers overall energy consumption compared to traditional air-cooled setups Axios.

Financial Performance and Stock Information

CleanSpark is publicly traded on NASDAQ under the ticker CLSK, with notable inclusion in sector analyses by Forbes and MarketWatch ForbesMarketWatch. In Q1 FY 2025 (quarter ended December 31, 2024), it reported $162.3 million in revenue (up 120% year-over-year) and net income of $241.7 million (EPS $0.85), while reducing marginal cost per Bitcoin by 6% to $34,000 CleanSpark Investors. Despite a Q2 fiscal loss of $138.8 million (49 cents per share), revenue grew to $181.7 million, reflecting strong top-line momentum amid market volatility Connecticut Post.

Recent Developments

Expansion and Capacity Milestones

In December 2024, CleanSpark energized 1 EH/s of new immersion-cooled capacity in Cheyenne, Wyoming, moving toward a total of 75 MW that will contribute 5 EH/s by Q1 2025 CleanSpark Investors. As of May 8, 2025, the company was on track to hit a 50 EH/s network capacity goal by June, bolstered by site builds in Tennessee and Wyoming Benzinga.

Technological Innovations

CleanSpark continually upgrades firmware on Bitmain S19j Pro rigs—removing stock fans and deploying proprietary code—to boost performance and extend machine lifespans to up to seven years Axios. It also collaborates with third parties to license its immersion cooling designs, targeting opportunities in AI and high-performance computing sectors SEC.

Challenges and Future Outlook

The primary challenges for CleanSpark include electricity competition from hyperscale AI and cloud data centers, which are rapidly securing power assets across the U.S. By offering dispatchable compute services and community-friendly operations, CleanSpark aims to differentiate itself, though rising infrastructure costs and regulatory scrutiny on energy use remain headwinds AxiosSEC. Continued Bitcoin price volatility also impacts profitability, making the company’s dual strategy of mining plus treasury accumulation critical for financial resilience.

Conclusion

CleanSpark, Inc. has evolved from an energy software firm into America’s Bitcoin Miner®, coupling large-scale mining operations with innovative energy solutions. With strong financial performance, strategic capacity expansions, and patented immersion cooling technology, CleanSpark is well-positioned to navigate competitive energy markets and capitalize on growing demand for sustainable compute services.
