Empirical Paper Analysis Skill: A Comprehensive Guide for Legal and Economic Research

2026-03-11

The Empirical Paper Analysis Skill represents a sophisticated framework designed to help researchers systematically analyze empirical research papers in law and economics. This skill provides a structured approach that guides users through the complex process of understanding, evaluating, and synthesizing empirical research findings.

Understanding the Core Framework

The skill operates through a five-stage analytical framework that mirrors the logical progression of empirical research: Problem Statement → Core Empirical Challenges → Identification Strategy → Key Findings → Academic Contribution. This structured approach ensures comprehensive coverage of all critical aspects of empirical research analysis.

Problem Statement Analysis

The analysis begins with identifying the core research question and understanding its motivation. This stage examines what specific problem or phenomenon the researchers are studying, why the question matters from both policy and theoretical perspectives, and what economic or legal intuition drives the research design. The skill helps users articulate the research question clearly and connect it to broader academic and practical contexts.

Core Empirical Challenges

Every empirical study faces methodological obstacles that complicate causal inference. The skill systematically identifies these challenges, which commonly include selection bias (where observed outcomes differ from potential outcomes), omitted variable bias (unobserved confounders), endogeneity (reverse causality), measurement error (difficulty quantifying abstract concepts), external validity concerns, and data limitations. For each challenge, the skill explains why it matters for causal inference and provides concrete examples to illustrate the issues.

Identification Strategy and Research Design

This critical stage examines how researchers address the empirical challenges through their methodological choices. The skill analyzes the identification strategy (whether natural experiments, instrumental variables, regression discontinuity, difference-in-differences, matching, or machine learning approaches), data sources and sample selection, empirical specifications including regression models and key variables, robustness checks and alternative specifications, and any novel methodological contributions. The analysis evaluates whether identification assumptions are plausible and considers remaining threats to validity.

Key Findings and Conclusions

The skill synthesizes the main empirical results, presenting findings with appropriate statistical context including magnitude and significance levels. It examines the robustness of results across different specifications, identifies any heterogeneous effects across subgroups, provides economic and legal interpretations of the findings, and discusses policy implications. The structured format ensures clarity and facilitates comparison across different studies.

Academic Contribution Evaluation

The final stage assesses the paper’s broader significance across multiple dimensions: methodological innovation (new identification strategies or measurement techniques), theoretical contribution (new insights about legal or judicial behavior), policy relevance (implications for legal reform or institutional design), and interdisciplinary impact. The skill also identifies potential directions for future research that the paper opens up.

Target Users and Applications

This skill is specifically designed for researchers in law and economics who regularly engage with empirical papers, particularly those using quantitative methods including econometrics, machine learning, and natural language processing. The framework is especially valuable for PhD students, early-career researchers, and experienced academics who need to critically evaluate empirical work or conduct systematic literature reviews.

Input Requirements and Technical Specifications

To function effectively, the skill requires access to the PDF file of the empirical research paper being analyzed, along with basic publication information including authors, journal name, publication date, and DOI or link if available. The skill processes this information through its analytical framework to generate comprehensive structured outputs.

Output Format and Structure

The skill generates structured markdown documents following a consistent template that includes the paper title, authors, journal information, and five main analytical sections. The output uses precise academic language appropriate for PhD-level analysis, assumes familiarity with econometric concepts and machine learning methods, and provides bilingual output with technical terms in both Chinese and English when first introduced.

Special Instructions and Best Practices

The skill emphasizes critical thinking throughout the analysis process, encouraging users to question assumptions, evaluate identification strength, and consider alternative explanations rather than simply summarizing content. It incorporates mathematical notation when describing models or identification strategies, uses tables and figures effectively when referencing results, and maintains focus on the five core sections without adding unnecessary content.

Workflow and Implementation

The recommended workflow involves reading the entire paper to understand context, extracting the empirical strategy with attention to identification sections, identifying key methodological challenges, tracing how authors solve each challenge, synthesizing findings with appropriate interpretation, and evaluating contribution within the broader literature. This systematic approach ensures thorough and consistent analysis across different research papers.

Benefits and Value Proposition

The Empirical Paper Analysis Skill provides significant benefits for legal and economic research by offering a standardized framework for paper analysis, improving critical evaluation skills, facilitating systematic literature reviews, enhancing understanding of empirical methodologies, and supporting more rigorous academic writing. The skill helps researchers develop more sophisticated analytical capabilities while ensuring comprehensive coverage of all critical aspects of empirical research.

Integration with Research Workflow

This skill integrates seamlessly into various research workflows, including literature review processes, research proposal development, dissertation writing, journal article preparation, and teaching empirical methods. It serves as both a learning tool for students and a productivity enhancement for experienced researchers, helping to standardize and improve the quality of empirical research analysis across the field.

Future Developments and Enhancements

While the current skill provides comprehensive coverage of empirical paper analysis, ongoing developments may include enhanced capabilities for processing different types of empirical methodologies, improved integration with citation management systems, and expanded support for interdisciplinary research applications. The framework continues to evolve based on user feedback and emerging trends in empirical legal and economic research.

Conclusion

The Empirical Paper Analysis Skill represents a valuable tool for researchers in law and economics, providing a structured, comprehensive framework for analyzing empirical research papers. By guiding users through a systematic analytical process, the skill enhances research quality, improves critical evaluation capabilities, and supports more rigorous academic scholarship in empirical legal and economic studies.
