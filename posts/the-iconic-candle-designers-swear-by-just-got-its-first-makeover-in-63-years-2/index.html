The Iconic Candle Designers Swear By Just Got Its First Makeover in 63 Years

2026-04-26

For over six decades, the scent of a specific, unassuming glass jar has wafted through the homes of design aficionados, chefs, and minimalists alike. It is a fragrance that signals sophistication without shouting, presence without pretension. For 63 years, the vessel holding this olfactory masterpiece remained virtually unchanged, a stalwart of mid-century modern aesthetics in a world of fleeting trends. However, the wait is finally over. The iconic candle that top-tier interior designers have sworn by for generations has just received its first major makeover since 1961, and the update is a masterclass in respecting heritage while embracing the future.

This is not merely a change in packaging; it is a cultural moment for the home fragrance industry. When a product maintains such a loyal following for more than half a century without altering its core identity, any modification carries significant weight. Today, we dive deep into the history of this design legend, analyze the subtle yet profound changes in its new iteration, and explore why this refresh matters for your home and your sense of style.

The Legacy of a Design Classic

To understand the magnitude of this update, one must first appreciate the legacy of the original design. Introduced in the early 1960s, the candle was born during an era that celebrated clean lines, functional beauty, and high-quality materials. While other brands focused on ornate holders and heavy perfumes, this brand chose a different path. They opted for a simple, apothecary-style glass jar, a minimalist label, and a focus on pure, single-note fragrances.

The result was a product that transcended its utility. It became a staple on coffee tables designed by Eames, in kitchens renovated by mid-century purists, and in the bathrooms of modernist architects. The longevity of the design is a testament to the principle that true style is timeless. While trends like scented gourds, geometric concrete vessels, and neon-lit jars have come and gone, this iconic candle remained a constant, trusted by those who understand that good design never goes out of style.

Designers have long praised the candle not just for its scent, but for its versatility. The original vessel was designed to be reusable, encouraging owners to repurpose the glass as a drinking tumbler, a vase for wildflowers, or a storage jar for kitchen staples. This ethos of sustainability and multifunctionality was ahead of its time and remains incredibly relevant in today’s eco-conscious market.

Unveiling the 2024 Makeover

So, what exactly has changed after 63 years of consistency? The answer lies in the details. The brand has executed a subtle evolution rather than a radical revolution. The goal was to refine the user experience and enhance the aesthetic appeal without alienating the purists who have loved the original for decades.

A Refined Silhouette

The most noticeable change is in the glassware itself. The new iteration features a slightly more refined silhouette. The glass is now clearer, offering a pristine view of the wax, which has also been updated to a cleaner, brighter white to enhance the visual purity of the flame. The shoulders of the jar have been subtly tapered to feel more ergonomic in the hand, and the base is slightly thicker, giving the candle a more substantial, premium weight on the shelf.

The Evolution of the Label

Perhaps the most talked-about update is the label. For decades, the label was a simple, printed adhesive. The new design utilizes a debossed paper label that offers a tactile experience reminiscent of high-end stationery. The typography has been kerned slightly tighter for better readability, and the ink used is now soy-based, aligning with the brand’s renewed commitment to sustainability. The color palette remains neutral, ensuring it blends seamlessly into any decor scheme, from industrial lofts to coastal cottages.

Enhanced Performance

Beyond aesthetics, the makeover includes performance upgrades. The wick has been redesigned to ensure a cleaner burn with less soot, addressing a common concern among long-time users. Furthermore, the wax blend now incorporates a higher percentage of renewable plant-based waxes, resulting in a longer burn time and a more consistent scent throw. This means the fragrance fills the room more effectively without overwhelming the senses, maintaining that signature subtle diffusion the brand is famous for.

Interior designers are often the gatekeepers of home trends, and their endorsement of this candle is no accident. They appreciate objects that tell a story and stand the test of time. The enduring appeal of this candle lies in its ability to anchor a space. In a room filled with bold patterns or vibrant colors, the simplicity of this candle provides a necessary visual rest.

Leading interior architects note that the new updates make the candle even more versatile. The clearer glass and refined label allow it to sit comfortably alongside both vintage finds and contemporary art pieces. It is a design chameleon that adapts to its environment rather than demanding attention. This humility in design is what makes it a favorite among professionals who curate spaces for a living.

Moreover, the brand’s commitment to maintaining the core identity of the product while making necessary modernizations is a lesson in brand management that designers admire. It shows respect for the consumer’s intelligence and loyalty. It proves that you do not need to chase every trend to remain relevant; you simply need to refine your excellence.

The Scent Profile: Unchanged Perfection

While the vessel has undergone a transformation, the heart of the product—the fragrance—remains untouched. The brand understood that tinkering with the scent profile would be a step too far. The signature scents, ranging from crisp fig and warm tobacco to fresh linen and spicy clove, are formulated with the same high-quality essential oils and aroma compounds used for decades.

This consistency is rare in an industry where formulas are frequently altered to cut costs or follow fleeting scent trends. By keeping the scents identical, the brand ensures that the nostalgia associated with the smell is preserved. For many, the scent of this candle is linked to memories of childhood homes, favorite vacations, or significant life events. Changing that would break the emotional connection that has sustained the brand for 63 years.

Sustainability and the Future

The 2024 makeover is not just about looks; it is a statement on sustainability. The new packaging is 100% recyclable, and the brand has committed to carbon-neutral shipping for these new units. The move towards plant-based waxes and soy-based inks reflects a broader industry shift towards environmental responsibility, but this brand executes it with the same elegance they apply to design.

Consumers today are more educated and demanding regarding the environmental impact of their purchases. By upgrading their materials and processes, the brand ensures its relevance for the next generation of design lovers. It is a strategic move that honors the past while securing the future.

FAQ: Everything You Need to Know About the Update

Is the scent formula exactly the same as the original?

Yes, the fragrance oils and the overall scent profile remain identical to the original formula that has been loved for 63 years. The brand made a conscious decision to keep the smell unchanged to preserve the nostalgic connection customers have with the product.

Can I still reuse the glass jar after the candle is finished?

Absolutely. The new glass is actually more durable and designed specifically for reuse. Many customers use the jars as drinking glasses, vases, or storage containers. The new debossed label is also easier to remove if you wish to repurpose the glass without the branding.

Why did the brand decide to change the design after so long?

The brand sought to enhance the user experience and align with modern sustainability standards. The updates, including the clearer glass, improved wick, and eco-friendly materials, were made to refine the product without losing the essence that made it an icon.

Will the price increase with the new design?

While there may be a slight adjustment to reflect the upgraded materials and sustainable sourcing, the brand has strived to keep the candle accessible. It remains a luxury item that offers high value through its burn time and design longevity.

Where can I purchase the redesigned candle?

The new design is available exclusively through the brand’s official website and select high-end department stores and design boutiques. Due to high demand following the launch, some scents may experience temporary stock shortages.
