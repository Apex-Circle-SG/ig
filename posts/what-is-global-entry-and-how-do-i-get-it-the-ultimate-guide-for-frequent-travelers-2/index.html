What Is Global Entry and How Do I Get It: The Ultimate Guide for Frequent Travelers

2026-05-11

What Is Global Entry?

For the frequent international traveler, the most dreaded part of the journey is often not the flight itself, but the arrival at the airport. Standing in endless lines at U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) after a long-haul flight can be exhausting. This is where Global Entry becomes a game-changer. Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States.

When you participate in the Global Entry program, you no longer have to wait in the traditional processing lines. Instead, you proceed to a Global Entry kiosk, scan your passport or U.S. permanent resident card, provide your fingerprints for verification, and complete a customs declaration on the screen. The entire process takes mere minutes, allowing you to bypass the standard immigration queues and get on your way much faster.

Why You Should Apply for Global Entry

Beyond the primary benefit of faster entry into the U.S., there are several compelling reasons to invest in a Global Entry membership. First and foremost, Global Entry includes TSA PreCheck. This means you gain the benefits of expedited security screening at domestic airports, allowing you to keep your shoes on, leave your laptop in your bag, and move through security lines with ease. It is effectively a two-for-one benefit package.

Furthermore, the program is designed to streamline your travel experience globally. While it is a U.S. government program, it is recognized by several international partners, making your transition through various international borders smoother. For business travelers or those who take multiple international trips per year, the time saved over a five-year membership period is substantial.

How to Apply for Global Entry: A Step-by-Step Guide

The application process for Global Entry requires some patience and attention to detail. Follow these steps to submit your application successfully.

Step 1: Create a Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) Account

The entire application process is managed through the official Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) website. You must create an account to start your application. It is vital to use the official government website ending in .gov to avoid third-party scams that charge extra fees for services they cannot actually provide.

Step 2: Complete the Application

Once your account is set up, select the Global Entry option. You will need to provide detailed personal information, including your employment history, residential history for the past five years, and details regarding your international travel. Be prepared to provide your passport information as well.

Step 3: Pay the Application Fee

There is a non-refundable application fee. It is important to note that many premium credit cards now offer a statement credit for Global Entry fees. Before you pay out of pocket, check your credit card benefits to see if you can get reimbursed for this expense.

Step 4: Wait for Conditional Approval

After submitting your application, it will undergo a rigorous background check by the Department of Homeland Security. This process can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months. Once your status changes to Conditionally Approved, you are ready to schedule your in-person interview.

Step 5: Schedule and Attend Your Interview

The final step is the in-person interview at a Global Entry Enrollment Center. During this meeting, a CBP officer will verify your documents, ask a few questions about your travel habits, and take your fingerprints. Alternatively, many travelers now utilize Enrollment on Arrival (EoA), which allows you to complete your interview during your next international arrival at major airports, saving you a separate trip to an enrollment center.

Important Eligibility Requirements

Not everyone qualifies for Global Entry. The program is reserved for low-risk travelers. You may be disqualified from the program if you:

  • Provide false or incomplete information on your application.
  • Have been convicted of any criminal offense or have pending criminal charges.
  • Have been found in violation of any customs, immigration, or agricultural regulations.
  • Are the subject of an ongoing investigation by any federal, state, or local law enforcement agency.
  • Are inadmissible to the United States under immigration regulation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Global Entry worth the cost?

If you travel internationally more than once or twice a year, Global Entry is well worth the cost. When you factor in that it includes TSA PreCheck, the value proposition for the five-year membership is excellent, especially if you can get the fee reimbursed by a credit card.

How long does a Global Entry membership last?

A Global Entry membership is valid for five years. It is important to keep track of your expiration date, as you can renew your membership starting one year before it expires. If you submit your renewal application before your current membership expires, you can continue to use your benefits while the renewal is being processed.

Can my children use my Global Entry benefits?

No, Global Entry is an individual membership. Every person, including infants and children, must have their own Global Entry membership and their own application submitted to utilize the kiosks.

What is Enrollment on Arrival?

Enrollment on Arrival is a service that allows conditionally approved applicants to complete their required interview upon arrival into the U.S. from an international flight. This eliminates the need to schedule a separate appointment at a dedicated enrollment center, making the process significantly more convenient for those with upcoming international travel plans.
