Anticipating Bitcoin’s Weekend Correction: Why May 10 Could Mark a Healthy Pullback

2025-05-09

Bitcoin is poised for a short-term correction over the May 10, 2025 weekend, driven by overbought technical readings, reduced trading volumes, and historical seasonality patterns that suggest mid-May pullbacks are common.

Market Context Before the Weekend

Following a strong rally that saw Bitcoin surpass the $100,000 mark, traders are bracing for a short-term pullback over the May 10 weekend.
Market seasonality analysis indicates that May tends to underperform relative to other months, with median returns just under 1%, suggesting limited upside momentum in mid-May periods.

Technical Indicators Signaling a Pullback

Technical analysis shows Bitcoin’s RSI has hovered near overbought territory since mid-April, increasing the likelihood of a corrective move to alleviate upward pressure.
Price action broke down from a short-term parallel channel in early May, a pattern often preceding downward corrections as bulls lose momentum.
Trading volume has declined sharply, with CCN reporting a 28% drop in weekly volumes to $32 billion, reflecting waning conviction at current prices.

Fundamental Considerations

Macro factors, including uncertainty over the next Federal Reserve interest rate decision, have prompted traders to take profits before weekend closures.
Asian markets, which typically contribute significant weekend liquidity, are on watch for US–China trade talks scheduled shortly after the weekend, adding to preemptive profit-taking.

Potential Correction Scenarios Over the Weekend

Analysts warn that if Bitcoin fails to hold support at $92,700 on low liquidity, the next key level to monitor is $85,100, where buying interest may re-emerge.
Historical price behavior suggests that weekend corrections often test these liquidity zones before resuming upward trends in the following week.
Short squeezes could occur if rapid profit-taking triggers liquidation of bullish derivatives positions, amplifying price moves downward.

Risk Management Strategies

Traders are advised to set stop-loss orders around $92,500 to protect against sharp dips during thin weekend markets.
Dollar-cost averaging and position trimming ahead of the weekend are prudent approaches to mitigate volatility risks in low-liquidity environments.

Conclusion

In sum, the convergence of overbought indicators, declining volumes, and seasonal trends supports the view that Bitcoin will undergo a corrective phase over the May 10 weekend.
This correction is likely to be healthy, providing a consolidation opportunity before potential further upside as markets digest recent gains.
