The Abel Prize: A Brief Overview

The Abel Prize, often referred to as the “Nobel Prize of Mathematics,” was established in 2002 by the Norwegian government to honor outstanding contributions to the field of mathematics. Named after the Norwegian mathematician Niels Henrik Abel, this award is presented annually by the King of Norway and includes a monetary prize of 7.5 million Norwegian kroner (approximately $750,000 USD).
