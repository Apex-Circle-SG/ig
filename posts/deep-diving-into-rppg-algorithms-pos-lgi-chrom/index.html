Deep Diving into rPPG Algorithms: POS, LGI, CHROM

2025-04-21

Remote Photoplethysmography (rPPG) is a non-contact, non-invasive technology that measures a person’s heart rate and other physiological signals using a video camera. It works by analyzing subtle changes in skin color caused by variations in blood flow. These fluctuations, though imperceptible to the naked eye, contain valuable information about the heart’s activity.

To extract the heart rate signal from video data, rPPG algorithms employ various techniques. The most commonly used algorithms are POS (Pulse Oximetry Signal), LGI (Local Gradient Intensity), and CHROM (CHROMatic analysis). In this article, we will explore what each algorithm is, how it works, and the differences between each one.

POS (Pulse Oximetry Signal) Algorithm

The POS algorithm is one of the most common rPPG algorithms. It works by measuring the changes in red and infrared light reflected from the skin. The difference between the two signals is used to calculate the heart rate.

LGI (Local Gradient Intensity) Algorithm

The LGI algorithm is another popular rPPG algorithm. It works by measuring the changes in the intensity of the red and infrared light reflected from the skin. The LGI algorithm is more sensitive to motion artifacts than the POS algorithm, but it can be more accurate in certain conditions.

CHROM (CHROMatic analysis) Algorithm

The CHROM algorithm is a relatively new rPPG algorithm. It works by analyzing the changes in the color of the skin. The CHROM algorithm is more robust to motion artifacts than the POS and LGI algorithms, but it can be more computationally intensive.

Differences between POS, LGI, and CHROM Algorithms

The main difference between the POS, LGI, and CHROM algorithms is the way they measure the heart rate signal. The POS algorithm measures the difference between the red and infrared light reflected from the skin. The LGI algorithm measures the changes in the intensity of the red and infrared light reflected from the skin. The CHROM algorithm analyzes the changes in the color of the skin.

Applications of rPPG Algorithms

rPPG algorithms have a wide range of applications, including:

• Healthcare

• Wellness and fitness

• Security and surveillance

One of the most promising applications of rPPG algorithms is in the healthcare field. rPPG can be used to remotely monitor patients’ vital signs, such as heart rate and blood pressure. This can be especially beneficial for patients who are unable to visit a doctor’s office or hospital regularly.

rPPG algorithms can also be used to track fitness levels and sleep quality. For example, rPPG-based fitness trackers can measure a person’s heart rate during exercise, while rPPG-based sleep trackers can measure a person’s heart rate variability during sleep.

Conclusion

rPPG algorithms are a powerful tool for measuring vital signs remotely. The POS, LGI, and CHROM algorithms are the most commonly used rPPG algorithms. Each algorithm has its own advantages and disadvantages. The best algorithm for a particular application will depend on the specific needs of the user.
