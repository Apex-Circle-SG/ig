What were the main drivers of Niagen’s revenue growth in Q1 2026?

The primary drivers were the increased adoption of the flagship NAD+ product line and a successful pivot to a direct-to-consumer model, which minimized middleman fees and allowed for higher margins. Additionally, international expansion efforts contributed to a larger customer base.

How is Niagen managing its R&D costs?

Niagen has implemented a tiered R&D strategy that prioritizes high-impact clinical trials while utilizing automation and AI-driven modeling to reduce the time and expense required for initial molecule discovery and testing.

When can investors expect the Phase III trial results?

Management indicated that the final data readout for the primary Phase III trial is expected by late Q4 2026 or early Q1 2027, depending on the speed of patient enrollment and data verification processes.

Does Niagen plan to pay dividends in 2026?

During the Q1 earnings call, the CFO stated that the company remains in a growth phase. Consequently, all excess capital is currently being reinvested into R&D and market expansion. There are no immediate plans for dividend payments in the 2026 fiscal year.

What are the biggest risks identified by the management team?

The management team highlighted regulatory changes in international markets and potential supply chain disruptions as the primary risks. However, they noted that they have diversified their manufacturing facilities to mitigate these issues effectively.
