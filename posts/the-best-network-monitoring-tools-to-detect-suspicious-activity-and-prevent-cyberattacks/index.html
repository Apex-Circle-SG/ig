The Best Network Monitoring Tools to Detect Suspicious Activity and Prevent Cyberattacks

2026-04-24

Why Network Monitoring is Critical for Security

In today’s digital landscape, the perimeter is no longer just a physical firewall. With the rise of remote work and cloud-based infrastructures, network monitoring has become the backbone of enterprise security. Detecting suspicious activity before it escalates into a full-blown data breach is essential for any organization. By utilizing professional-grade monitoring tools, IT teams gain visibility into traffic patterns, unauthorized access attempts, and anomalies that suggest a compromised system.

Top Network Monitoring Tools for Threat Detection

Choosing the right tool depends on your organization’s size, budget, and technical requirements. Below are the industry-leading solutions for identifying malicious behavior.

1. PRTG Network Monitor

PRTG Network Monitor is a comprehensive solution that offers granular visibility into your IT infrastructure. It uses sensors to monitor specific data points, allowing administrators to set up alerts for unusual traffic spikes or unauthorized device connections. Its ability to perform deep packet inspection makes it a powerful ally in spotting suspicious network activity.

2. SolarWinds Security Event Manager

SolarWinds provides a robust platform for log management and threat detection. The Security Event Manager (SEM) excels at correlating events from across the network. If a user tries to access sensitive files during odd hours from an unrecognized IP, SEM identifies this pattern and triggers an automated response, effectively stopping the threat in its tracks.

3. Wireshark

For deep-dive analysis, Wireshark remains the gold standard. While it requires a higher level of technical expertise, it is unmatched in its ability to capture and interactively browse the traffic running on a computer network. Security analysts use Wireshark to perform forensic analysis on suspicious packets to understand exactly what an attacker is attempting to do.

4. Nagios XI

Nagios XI is a powerful, enterprise-class monitoring tool that provides extensive visibility into network health. Its plugin ecosystem allows for highly customized security monitoring, enabling teams to track everything from bandwidth usage to server uptime. By monitoring for performance bottlenecks, Nagios often reveals the presence of malware that consumes network resources.

Key Features to Look For

  • Real-time Alerting: The ability to receive instant notifications via email, SMS, or Slack when an anomaly occurs.
  • Deep Packet Inspection (DPI): Essential for analyzing the payload of data packets to detect hidden malicious code.
  • Behavioral Analytics: Tools that learn your network’s ‘normal’ baseline to automatically flag deviations.
  • Log Management and Correlation: Centralizing logs from firewalls, servers, and endpoints to identify multi-stage attacks.
  • Scalability: Ensuring the tool can handle your network traffic as your organization grows.

How to Identify Suspicious Activity

Monitoring tools are only as effective as the strategy behind them. To effectively catch bad actors, you must look for specific indicators of compromise (IoCs). Common signs include:

  • Unusual Data Exfiltration: Large amounts of data leaving the network during off-peak hours.
  • Unauthorized Access Attempts: Multiple failed login attempts originating from a single internal or external IP address.
  • Unexpected Protocol Usage: Traffic moving through ports that are typically closed or unused in your environment.
  • Geographic Anomalies: Connections originating from countries or regions where your company does not conduct business.

Best Practices for Network Security

Implementing a tool is just the first step. To maintain a secure environment, consider the following strategies:

  • Patch Management: Keep all network devices and software updated to close known vulnerabilities.
  • Network Segmentation: Divide your network into smaller subnets to prevent attackers from moving laterally through your systems.
  • Zero Trust Architecture: Operate under the assumption that every user and device is a potential threat until verified.
  • Regular Audits: Periodically review your monitoring logs and security configurations to ensure they meet modern standards.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between network monitoring and intrusion detection?

Network monitoring generally focuses on the performance and availability of devices, whereas Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS) are specifically engineered to identify malicious activity and policy violations within a network.

Can network monitoring tools stop an attack?

Most monitoring tools are designed to alert you to threats. However, many modern platforms include automated response features—often referred to as Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS)—that can automatically block malicious traffic once it is detected.

Is open-source monitoring as effective as paid software?

Open-source tools like Wireshark or Zabbix are incredibly powerful and often match the capabilities of paid versions. However, paid software typically offers better support, easier installation, and more intuitive dashboards, which can save your IT team significant time.

How often should I review my network logs?

In a high-security environment, logs should be reviewed in real-time using automated correlation tools. For manual review, a weekly audit is the minimum requirement to ensure that no minor anomalies are being overlooked.

Conclusion

The threat of cyberattacks is constant, but with the right network monitoring tools, you can turn your infrastructure into a fortress. By combining automated detection software with proactive security policies, you minimize the risk of data loss and ensure the long-term integrity of your business operations. Choose a tool that fits your current needs, but always keep an eye on scalability to ensure your security measures grow alongside your organization.
