What Is the Double Bottom (Running) Pattern

2025-05-05

In the world of technical analysis, the double bottom pattern is one of the most recognized bullish reversal setups. While the traditional version is widely known, more advanced traders often look for nuanced variations that offer early signals and better trade positioning. One such variation is the Double Bottom (Running) pattern—a faster, momentum-driven version of the classic double bottom that indicates strong institutional buying pressure and minimal price hesitation.

This guide explores the Double Bottom (Running) pattern in detail: how to identify it, when to trade it, and why it can provide early entry opportunities during high-volatility market environments.

Key Characteristics of the Double Bottom (Running) Pattern

The running variation differs subtly but significantly from the traditional double bottom. It is characterized by a rapid recovery from the second bottom, often breaking the neckline swiftly and continuing upward without the usual pause or pullback.

1. Shallow Second Dip

  • The second bottom typically forms higher or at the same level as the first but recovers faster.
  • This suggests aggressive buying and diminishing selling power.

2. Strong Momentum Post-Neckline

  • The breakout above the neckline is usually decisive and sharp, indicating low resistance and high participation.
  • There may be no retest of the neckline, making early recognition key.

3. Low Consolidation

  • Unlike traditional setups, the running double bottom doesn’t consolidate for long.
  • Time between bottoms is often short, and price action is dynamic.

Real-World Examples of Double Bottom (Running) Patterns

1. Tesla (TSLA) – 2020 Pre-Split Surge

Tesla displayed a running double bottom during its 2020 rally. After a sharp drop, the stock quickly bounced back, forming two close lows before breaking out with strong volume. The neckline breakout was immediate, with no retest, and price surged over 100% within weeks.

2. Ethereum (ETH) – 2021 Bull Run

During the early stages of ETH’s rally, it formed a running double bottom at key support around $1,700. The second bottom was shallower, followed by a sharp bullish engulfing candle. The neckline was broken the same day without retesting, leading to a swift move toward $2,500.

Trading the Double Bottom (Running) Pattern: A Step-by-Step Guide

1. Spot the Running Formation Early

  • Use 15-min to 4-hour charts depending on your trading style.
  • Identify the first dip and observe how quickly the price recovers from the second.

2. Watch Volume and Momentum Indicators

  • Confirm the second low has reduced selling volume and potentially bullish RSI divergence.
  • Look for momentum indicators like MACD crossing early before the neckline break.

3. Enter Aggressively or Use Breakout Confirmation

  • Since running patterns often don’t pull back, consider early entry during the bounce or breakout candle.
  • Place a tight stop below the second bottom to manage risk.

4. Set Realistic Targets

  • Measure the height of the pattern from the lowest point to the neckline and project upward.
  • Use Fibonacci extensions or recent resistance levels for exit points.

Why the Running Variation Matters for Traders

  • Early Signal: Provides quicker entries than traditional double bottoms.
  • Momentum-Friendly: Ideal for traders who thrive in fast-moving markets.
  • Efficient Risk-Reward: Tight stop-loss zones due to shallow second bottoms allow for better R/R ratios.

This pattern is particularly useful in bullish market conditions, where waiting for a full traditional setup could mean missing the move entirely.

Conclusion: Capitalizing on Running Double Bottoms

The Double Bottom (Running) pattern is a high-conviction reversal formation that rewards traders who can act swiftly and confidently. With its shallow pullbacks and powerful breakouts, this setup is ideal for markets driven by news catalysts, institutional buying, or macro trend reversals.

By learning to identify the structural clues and act decisively, traders can use the running double bottom pattern to stay ahead of the crowd and catch strong upside momentum as it begins.
