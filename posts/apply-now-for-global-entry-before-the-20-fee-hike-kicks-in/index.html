Understanding the Upcoming Fee Hike

The U.S. government periodically reviews the costs associated with managing trusted traveler programs. Because of the increasing volume of applicants and the need for updated technology and personnel, the Department of Homeland Security has announced a significant restructuring of fees. For many years, the cost of a five-year Global Entry membership remained static, but that era is coming to a close. A 20 percent increase represents a substantial shift for individual travelers and families alike.

Why the Increase is Happening

The primary driver behind the fee hike is the need to modernize infrastructure. As global travel demand surges post-pandemic, the systems processing millions of travelers per year require more robust cybersecurity, faster processing software, and additional staff at Enrollment Centers. By increasing the fee, the government aims to reduce the massive backlog of applications that has plagued the program for years, ideally leading to faster conditional approval times for future applicants.

How Much Will You Save by Applying Now?

If you apply before the new fee structure takes full effect, you are grandfathered into the current pricing. For a program that lasts for 60 months, even a modest increase feels significant when you calculate the cost-per-trip value. By securing your membership today, you are essentially locking in a lower rate for half a decade of stress-free international arrivals.

The Benefits of Global Entry Beyond the Price Tag

While the cost is a major consideration, the value proposition of Global Entry extends far beyond the monetary investment. When you weigh the price against the time saved, the program practically pays for itself within the first few international trips.

  • Expedited Customs Clearance: Bypass the traditional passport control lines entirely by using automated kiosks or facial recognition technology at major airports.
  • TSA PreCheck Included: One of the best-kept secrets of Global Entry is that it automatically includes TSA PreCheck, allowing you to breeze through security lines for domestic flights as well.
  • Reduced Stress: Knowing that you can clear customs in minutes rather than hours transforms the end of a long international flight from a nightmare into a simple formality.
  • Global Recognition: The program is highly respected, and the membership card serves as a valid form of identification in various travel scenarios.

Step-by-Step Guide to Applying Before the Deadline

The application process can feel daunting, but it is manageable if you break it down into simple steps. Do not wait until the last minute to begin your application, as federal processing times can fluctuate.

1. Create Your Trusted Traveler Program Account

Everything starts at the official Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) website. Be wary of third-party websites that charge extra fees to help you fill out the form; the official government site is the only place you should submit your information.

2. Fill Out the Comprehensive Application

You will need your passport, employment history, and address history for the past five years. Accuracy is paramount here. Any discrepancy between your application and your government records can lead to an automatic denial or a lengthy delay in the vetting process.

3. Pay the Current Fee

Complete the payment process immediately to lock in the current rate. Once the payment is processed, your application will be submitted for the background check phase.

4. Schedule Your Interview

Once you receive conditional approval, you must schedule an interview at an Enrollment Center. If you are already traveling, look into the Enrollment on Arrival program, which allows you to complete your interview while clearing customs after an international flight—often saving you the need to make a separate appointment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I apply just before the fee hike?

As long as you submit your application and pay the fee prior to the official change date, your application will be processed at the existing rate. Do not wait until the final hour of the final day, as website traffic or technical glitches could prevent your submission from going through on time.

Is Global Entry worth the cost for occasional travelers?

If you take even one international trip per year, the combined benefit of Global Entry and TSA PreCheck is worth the investment. The time saved at security and customs provides a level of peace of mind that is difficult to put a price on.

Does the fee hike affect children?

Every individual, regardless of age, must have their own Global Entry membership. If you are traveling as a family, you will need to pay for each child individually. This is why the 20 percent hike is particularly impactful for families planning trips abroad.

Can I get my application fee reimbursed?

Many premium travel credit cards offer a statement credit for Global Entry or TSA PreCheck application fees. Check your credit card benefits portal before paying; you might be able to get the entire cost reimbursed, effectively making the program free regardless of the fee hike.

What is the biggest mistake people make during the application?

The most common mistake is providing inaccurate information regarding international travel history or legal records. Even minor errors can trigger a secondary review, which can delay your approval by months. Double-check every entry before you hit the submit button.
