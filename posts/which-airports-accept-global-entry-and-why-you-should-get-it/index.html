Which Airports Accept Global Entry — and Why You Should Get It

2026-04-23

Understanding the Global Entry Program for Frequent Flyers

In the modern era of air travel, time is the most valuable commodity a passenger possesses. Navigating crowded terminals, waiting in endless security lines, and dealing with customs delays can turn an exciting trip into a stressful ordeal before it even begins. This is where Global Entry emerges as a game-changing solution for travelers. Administered by U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP), this trusted traveler program allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States. While many people are familiar with TSA PreCheck, Global Entry offers a more comprehensive suite of benefits that extends beyond domestic security checkpoints to include international border control.

The primary allure of the program lies in its ability to bypass the traditional customs inspection lines. Instead of waiting in long queues to have passports stamped and forms reviewed by an officer, members utilize automated kiosks located in the arrival halls of major airports. By simply scanning their passport or using facial recognition technology, providing fingerprints, and confirming their customs declaration on a touchscreen, travelers can exit the airport in a fraction of the usual time. But the question remains: which airports accept Global Entry, and is the investment truly worth it for your specific travel habits?

Comprehensive List of Airports with Global Entry Kiosks

One of the most common misconceptions about Global Entry is that it is only available at a handful of massive international hubs. In reality, the program has expanded significantly over the last decade. Currently, there are over 75 airports across the United States equipped with Global Entry kiosks. This extensive network ensures that whether you are landing in New York City or arriving in a smaller regional hub with international capabilities, you likely have access to expedited processing.

Some of the busiest and most critical airports featuring these kiosks include:

  • Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport (ATL): As the world’s busiest airport, Atlanta offers numerous kiosks across its international terminal to handle high volumes of arriving passengers.
  • Los Angeles International Airport (LAX): Travelers arriving at LAX can find kiosks in the Tom Bradley International Terminal as well as several other terminals handling international flights.
  • John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK): New York’s primary international gateway provides robust Global Entry support across multiple terminals, essential for managing the city’s heavy influx of global tourists and business travelers.
  • Miami International Airport (MIA): Given its status as the primary gateway to Latin America and the Caribbean, Miami features extensive Global Entry infrastructure.
  • Chicago O’Hare International Airport (ORD): This major Midwest hub offers streamlined processing for travelers connecting through the nation’s interior.
  • Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW): A critical connection point for American Airlines, DFW provides efficient kiosk access for international arrivals.
  • San Francisco International Airport (SFO): Serving as a major tech hub and Pacific gateway, SFO ensures quick processing for its diverse array of international visitors.

Beyond these major metropolitan centers, many other airports including Seattle-Tacoma (SEA), Boston Logan (BOS), Washington Dulles (IAD), and Houston George Bush Intercontinental (IAH) fully support the program. It is important to note that while the list is extensive, not every terminal within an airport may have a kiosk if that terminal does not handle international flights. However, if you are arriving from a foreign country, you will be directed to a terminal or concourse equipped with the necessary technology.

International Preclearance Locations

An often-overlooked aspect of the program is its availability outside the United States. Through the preclearance program, travelers can complete their U.S. immigration and customs formalities before they even board their flight to the United States. This means that upon landing in the U.S., you are treated as a domestic arrival and can skip the customs line entirely. Countries with preclearance locations include Canada, Ireland, the Bahamas, Aruba, Bermuda, and the United Arab Emirates. Major airports like Toronto Pearson (YYZ), Dublin (DUB), and Abu Dhabi (AUH) allow you to clear customs before departure, making your arrival in the U.S. seamless.

Why You Should Prioritize Getting Global Entry

Deciding to apply for Global Entry is more than just a convenience; it is a strategic move for anyone who values efficiency and comfort. The benefits extend far beyond simply skipping a line. Here is why obtaining membership should be at the top of your travel checklist.

Included TSA PreCheck Benefits

Perhaps the most significant value proposition of Global Entry is that it automatically includes TSA PreCheck eligibility. While Global Entry handles your arrival into the United States from abroad, TSA PreCheck expedites your departure security screening at over 200 domestic airports. With this benefit, you do not need to remove shoes, laptops, liquids, belts, or light jackets. For domestic travelers, this alone can save hours of waiting time over the course of a year. Since the application fee for Global Entry is only slightly higher than TSA PreCheck alone, getting the combined package is widely considered the superior financial choice for frequent flyers.

Significant Time Savings

Time savings are the most tangible benefit. During peak travel seasons, such as summer holidays or winter breaks, standard customs lines can take upwards of two hours to clear. In contrast, Global Entry users often clear the process in under five minutes. For business travelers, this time can be the difference between making a crucial meeting or missing a connection. For families, it means less time standing in line with tired children and more time resting in a lounge or boarding the next leg of a journey.

Cost-Effectiveness and Credit Card Perks

The application fee for Global Entry is $100 for a five-year membership. When broken down annually, this amounts to just $20 per year. Furthermore, many premium travel credit cards offer a statement credit that covers the entire application fee for either Global Entry or TSA PreCheck. Cards such as the Chase Sapphire Reserve, American Express Platinum, and Capital One Venture Rewards frequently provide this perk. If your card covers the fee, the program effectively becomes free, offering an incredible return on investment for zero cost.

The Application Process: What to Expect

Obtaining Global Entry requires a rigorous vetting process to ensure the security of the program. The journey begins with an online application via the Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) website. Applicants must provide detailed personal information, including employment history and travel records, and pay the non-refundable $100 fee. Once the initial background check is complete, which can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months, the applicant receives a conditional approval notification.

The final step is an in-person interview. Historically, this required scheduling an appointment at an enrollment center, often located at major airports. Wait times for interviews could be lengthy. However, CBP has introduced Enrollment on Arrival, which allows conditionally approved applicants to complete their interview upon their first return to the U.S. from an international trip, eliminating the need for a separate appointment. Additionally, some applicants may be eligible for an interview via video call, further streamlining the process.

Is Global Entry Right for You?

If you travel internationally even once a year, Global Entry is an absolute necessity. The stress reduction and time savings justify the application effort many times over. Even if your international travel is infrequent, the included TSA PreCheck benefit makes it worthwhile for domestic trips. The program transforms the airport experience from a chaotic hurdle into a manageable part of your journey. With widespread availability at major and mid-sized airports alike, and the potential for the cost to be fully reimbursed by your credit card, there is little reason not to enroll.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Global Entry work at all US airports?

Global Entry kiosks are available at most major international airports in the United States, totaling over 75 locations. However, it does not work at every single airport, particularly smaller regional airports that do not handle international flights. It is always wise to check the CBP website for the specific airport you are visiting, though if you are arriving from another country, the airport will almost certainly have the necessary infrastructure.

Can I use Global Entry when traveling with children?

Yes, but every traveler, regardless of age, must have their own Global Entry membership. Children are not exempt from the application process or the fee. Each child must have their own account, pay the application fee, and complete an interview. Parents or legal guardians must accompany minors during the interview process.

How long does the Global Entry card last?

Global Entry membership is valid for five years from the date of your birthday following your approval date. You can renew your membership up to one year before it expires. It is highly recommended to renew early to avoid any lapse in benefits, especially since processing times for renewals can vary.

What happens if my Global Entry application is denied?

If your application is denied, you will receive a letter explaining the general reason, though specific details regarding security checks may be withheld. You may be able to appeal the decision or request a review, but the initial application fee is non-refundable. Common reasons for denial include incomplete application information, criminal records, or carrying prohibited items across the border in the past.

Is Global Entry valid for land and sea crossings?

Yes, Global Entry members can use their Known Traveler Number (KTN) to access expedited lanes at many land border crossings and sea ports of entry. Additionally, members receive a RFID-enabled card that can be used at NEXUS and SENTRI lanes when entering the U.S. from Canada and Mexico, respectively, provided the vehicle is registered for those programs.
