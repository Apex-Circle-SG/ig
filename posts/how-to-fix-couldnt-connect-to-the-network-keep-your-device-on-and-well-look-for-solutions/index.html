How to Fix Couldn’t Connect to the Network, Keep Your Device On, and We’ll Look for Solutions

2026-04-27

Understanding the Error

The error message Couldn’t connect to the network, keep your device on, and we’ll look for solutions is a common frustration for users across various platforms. Whether you are updating your operating system, trying to activate a new device, or attempting to connect to a cloud service, this notification signifies a communication breakdown between your hardware and the server. This guide will walk you through the diagnostic steps to resolve this issue permanently.

Common Causes for Network Connectivity Failures

Before diving into the fixes, it is important to understand why this happens. Most often, the issue is not with your device itself, but with how it interacts with your local network or the target server. Common triggers include:

  • DNS configuration errors preventing your device from resolving server addresses.
  • Firewall or antivirus software blocking outgoing connection attempts.
  • Outdated network drivers that fail to maintain a stable handshake with your router.
  • Server-side outages where the service you are trying to reach is currently down for maintenance.
  • IP address conflicts within your local area network.

Step-by-Step Troubleshooting Guide

1. Perform a Power Cycle on Your Networking Hardware

The first and most effective step is to refresh your network hardware. Unplug your router and modem from the power source for at least 30 seconds. This clears the temporary cache and forces your ISP to re-assign a fresh connection path. After plugging them back in, wait for all lights to stabilize before attempting to reconnect your device.

2. Check Your Date and Time Settings

It sounds simple, but an incorrect date and time setting is a frequent cause of SSL certificate failures. If your device time does not match the server time, the handshake will be rejected for security reasons. Ensure your device is set to Set time automatically in your system preferences.

3. Flush Your DNS Cache

Sometimes, your computer holds onto stale or corrupt IP addresses for specific domains. Flushing your DNS forces your machine to request a fresh address from your ISP. On Windows, open the Command Prompt as an administrator and type ipconfig /flushdns. This simple command often resolves the Couldn’t connect to the network error immediately.

4. Disable VPN and Proxy Settings

If you are using a VPN or a proxy server, your traffic might be getting routed through an overloaded or restricted node. Temporarily disable these services to see if the connection issue persists. If the error disappears, you may need to switch to a different VPN server or check your proxy configuration settings.

5. Update or Reinstall Network Drivers

Corrupted drivers are a silent killer of network stability. Visit the manufacturer’s website for your device, download the latest network adapter drivers, and perform a clean install. This ensures that your hardware is communicating efficiently with your operating system.

Advanced Network Diagnostics

If the standard steps do not work, you may need to look at your IP configuration. Using the command prompt, run ipconfig /release followed by ipconfig /renew to reset your local connection. Additionally, consider resetting your Winsock catalog by using the netsh winsock reset command, which repairs the software interface between your OS and your network.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Why does this error appear during system updates?

System updates require a stable, high-speed connection to verify digital signatures. If the connection drops or is throttled by your router, the server terminates the request, resulting in the Couldn’t connect to the network notification.

Can a slow internet speed cause this error?

Yes. If your bandwidth is extremely low, your device may time out while waiting for a response from the server. This is common during peak hours or when multiple devices are streaming content on the same network.

Is my device broken if I see this message?

Almost certainly not. This is a software-level notification. Unless you have recently dropped your device or spilled liquid on it, this is purely a network configuration issue that can be solved via software adjustments.

Does changing my DNS server help?

Switching to a reliable public DNS like Google (8.8.8.8) or Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) can significantly improve your connection stability and resolve issues where your ISP’s default DNS is slow or unreliable.

Conclusion

While the message Couldn’t connect to the network, keep your device on, and we’ll look for solutions can be annoying, it is usually a temporary hurdle. By systematically following these steps—starting from a simple router reboot and moving into advanced driver and DNS settings—you should be able to restore your connectivity. If the problem persists, it is worth checking the official status page of the service you are trying to access, as the issue might lie entirely outside of your control.
