Mastering Ichimoku Cloud Signals: Strong vs. Weak Buy and Sell Setups Based on Cloud and Price Line Positions

2025-05-08

The Ichimoku Cloud, or Ichimoku Kinko Hyo, is a comprehensive indicator that provides insight into trend direction, momentum, and potential support/resistance zones—all in a single glance. While the system may appear complex at first, it becomes a powerful tool when you understand how to interpret its cloud formations (Kumo) and the relative position of price lines.

This article focuses on a specific and simplified strategy using Ichimoku to determine weak and strong buy/sell signals, based on whether the cloud and key lines (like price) are above or below each other.

Understanding Ichimoku Components

Before diving into the signal strategy, here’s a quick refresher on the main elements:

  • Kumo (Cloud) = Formed by Senkou Span A & B
  • Tenkan-sen (Conversion Line) = Short-term trend
  • Kijun-sen (Base Line) = Medium-term trend
  • Chikou Span (Lagging Line) = Past price reference
  • Price Line = Current price in relation to the cloud

Ichimoku Cloud Signal Strategy

This method classifies trade signals into weak and strong, depending on where the price and cloud are in relation to each other.

🟥 Weak Sell Signal:

  • Price is above the cloud, but Tenkan/Kijun lines signal a sell
  • Cloud (Kumo) is also above the price
  • Market is showing resistance, but upward trend still exists — trade with caution

🟩 Weak Buy Signal:

  • Price is below the cloud, but lines are forming a bullish cross
  • Cloud is below price — signals early entry in a recovering trend
  • Potential reversal, but lower conviction

🔴 Strong Sell Signal:

  • Price is below the cloud, and Tenkan-sen < Kijun-sen (bearish cross)
  • Cloud is also above — confirming downward trend
  • Indicates strong bearish sentiment and momentum

🟢 Strong Buy Signal:

  • Price is above the cloud, and Tenkan-sen > Kijun-sen (bullish cross)
  • Cloud is also below — trend confirmation for a powerful upward move

How to Trade the Signals

✅ Entry Rules:

  1. Identify the signal type (strong or weak buy/sell)
  2. Confirm direction of Kumo (cloud) – expanding cloud indicates strength
  3. Check Chikou Span – should not be blocked by candles for cleaner signal
  4. Optional Confirmation – candle pattern, RSI, or volume spike

✅ Exit Strategy:

  • Exit when the cloud reverses or the price re-enters the cloud
  • Use previous swing high/low for setting stop-loss
  • Target using Fibonacci levels or next Kijun-sen intersection

Example Scenario

Asset: ETH/USD
Timeframe: 4H

  • Price is above the cloud, and Tenkan-sen > Kijun-sen
  • Cloud is below the price and widening
  • Chikou Span is also above past candles

Signal: Strong Buy
Action: Enter long trade with stop-loss below Kijun-sen and set take-profit at next resistance level

Advantages of This Strategy

Tips for Successful Ichimoku Trading

  • Trade in the direction of the cloud – always respect the dominant trend
  • Use higher timeframes (4H, Daily) for stronger signals
  • Avoid signals inside the cloud – they represent uncertainty
  • Combine with RSI or MACD for additional momentum validation

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Ignoring the Chikou Span – if it clashes with price candles, signal is weak
  • Entering trades within the cloud – avoid sideways congestion
  • Trading weak signals in choppy markets – wait for clear breakouts

Conclusion

The Ichimoku Cloud is a powerful all-in-one indicator that, when used properly, delivers high-probability setups. By classifying signals into strong and weak categories using the cloud and price line alignment, traders can better filter their entries and focus on momentum-backed moves.

This strategy simplifies Ichimoku without stripping its depth, making it ideal for both beginners and experienced traders seeking structured, reliable trades.
