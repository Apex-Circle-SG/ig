Sigma Announces Two More Prime Lenses for Canon Cameras, But I’m Still Waiting…

2026-02-25

Introduction

Sigma has been a significant player in the camera lens market, consistently delivering high-quality optics for various camera systems. Recently, the company announced two new prime lenses for Canon cameras, which has sparked interest among photography enthusiasts. However, despite these new releases, I find myself still waiting for more options that cater to specific needs and preferences.

The New Sigma Prime Lenses

The two new prime lenses announced by Sigma for Canon cameras are the Sigma 35mm f/1.2 DG DN Art and the Sigma 65mm f/2 DG DN Contemporary. Both lenses are designed for Canon’s RF mount, which is used in their full-frame mirrorless cameras.

Sigma 35mm f/1.2 DG DN Art

The Sigma 35mm f/1.2 DG DN Art is a fast wide-angle prime lens that promises exceptional optical performance. With a maximum aperture of f/1.2, it is designed to excel in low-light conditions and provide a shallow depth of field for creative effects. This lens is part of Sigma’s Art line, known for its superior image quality and robust build.

Sigma 65mm f/2 DG DN Contemporary

The Sigma 65mm f/2 DG DN Contemporary is a mid-telephoto prime lens that offers a versatile focal length for various photography genres. With a maximum aperture of f/2, it strikes a balance between low-light performance and portability. This lens is part of Sigma’s Contemporary line, which focuses on compactness and everyday usability.

Why I’m Still Waiting

While these new lenses are impressive, there are still some gaps in Sigma’s lineup for Canon users. For instance, there is a demand for more telephoto prime lenses, especially in the 85mm to 135mm range, which are popular for portrait and event photography. Additionally, some photographers are looking for more affordable options without compromising on quality.

The Need for More Telephoto Primes

Telephoto prime lenses are essential for many photographers, particularly those specializing in portraits, weddings, and sports. While Sigma has released some excellent telephoto options, there is still room for more choices, especially at focal lengths that are highly sought after.

Affordable Options

Another area where Sigma could expand is in offering more budget-friendly lenses. While their Art and Contemporary lines are top-notch, not every photographer can afford high-end optics. A line of affordable yet high-quality lenses would be a welcome addition to their lineup.

Conclusion

Sigma’s announcement of two new prime lenses for Canon cameras is exciting news for the photography community. The Sigma 35mm f/1.2 DG DN Art and the Sigma 65mm f/2 DG DN Contemporary are both promising additions to their lineup. However, as a photographer, I am still waiting for more options that cater to specific needs, such as telephoto primes and affordable alternatives. I hope Sigma continues to innovate and expand their offerings to meet the diverse demands of photographers worldwide.
