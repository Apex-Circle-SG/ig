Ivy Parks of BD-Canada Appointed Chair of the Medtech Canada Board of Directors: A New Era for Healthcare Innovation

2026-05-10

A Strategic Leadership Shift in Canadian Medtech

The landscape of the Canadian medical technology sector is undergoing a significant transformation with the recent announcement that Ivy Parks, a prominent leader from BD-Canada, has been appointed as the Chair of the Medtech Canada Board of Directors. This appointment marks a pivotal moment for the industry, signaling a renewed focus on innovation, cross-sector collaboration, and the strengthening of healthcare systems across the nation. As Medtech Canada continues to advocate for policies that improve patient outcomes, the addition of Parks to the helm brings a wealth of experience and a strategic vision that is expected to resonate throughout the healthcare ecosystem.

Who is Ivy Parks?

Ivy Parks is a seasoned executive whose career has been defined by a deep commitment to advancing medical technology. With her tenure at BD-Canada, she has navigated the complexities of the global medical device market, driving initiatives that enhance clinical efficiency and patient safety. Her leadership style is characterized by a blend of operational excellence and a forward-thinking approach to digital health integration. By stepping into the role of Chair, Parks is positioned to leverage her industry expertise to address the most pressing challenges currently facing the Canadian healthcare sector.

The Role of Medtech Canada in the Healthcare Ecosystem

Medtech Canada acts as the national voice for the medical technology industry, representing companies that are at the forefront of providing life-saving diagnostic, therapeutic, and monitoring technologies. Under the guidance of the Board of Directors, the association works tirelessly to ensure that innovative technologies reach the patients who need them most. The appointment of a leader from BD-Canada reinforces the strong alignment between the industry’s manufacturing expertise and the broader goals of national health policy.

Key Priorities for the New Chair

As the new Chair, Ivy Parks is expected to prioritize several key areas that are vital to the health of the Canadian medical technology market:

  • Accelerating Digital Health Adoption: Promoting the integration of artificial intelligence and data-driven solutions into clinical workflows.
  • Strengthening Supply Chain Resilience: Addressing the vulnerabilities exposed by recent global health crises to ensure a consistent flow of essential medical devices.
  • Advocating for Value-Based Procurement: Moving away from cost-centric purchasing models toward systems that prioritize long-term patient outcomes and clinical value.
  • Fostering Partnerships: Enhancing collaboration between government health authorities, academic institutions, and private industry players to drive R&D.

Ivy Parks brings the exact experience needed to bridge the gap between private sector innovation and the public sector’s need for scalable, sustainable healthcare solutions.

Looking ahead, the collaboration between the Board, government regulators, and clinical stakeholders will be critical. Addressing Regulatory and Economic Hurdles

One of the most significant challenges for the medtech industry is the slow pace of regulatory approval and the fragmented nature of procurement across different provinces. Under the chairmanship of Parks, it is anticipated that Medtech Canada will continue to push for harmonized standards and more transparent procurement pathways. This would not only benefit manufacturers but also ensure that hospitals and clinics can acquire the necessary tools to provide optimal care without unnecessary delays.
