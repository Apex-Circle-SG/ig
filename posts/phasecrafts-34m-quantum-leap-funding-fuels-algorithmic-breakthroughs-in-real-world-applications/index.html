Phasecraft’s $34M Quantum Leap: Funding Fuels Algorithmic Breakthroughs in Real-World Applications

2025-09-03

Quantum computing is on the cusp of shifting from experimental novelty to tangible utility—and Phasecraft, a UK-based quantum algorithms firm, is accelerating that shift. On September 2, 2025, the company raised $34 million in Series B funding to supercharge its development of efficient, hardware-agnostic quantum algorithms and bring meaningful applications closer to reality.

1. What the Funding Means for Phasecraft

This capital injection, co-led by Plural, Playground Global, and Novo Holdings’ Quantum Fund,—with continued backing from LocalGlobe, AlbionVC, and Parkwalk Advisors—marks a pivotal moment in Phasecraft’s journey. It underscores both investor confidence in quantum software innovation and a strategic pivot toward algorithm development over hardware creation.

Phasecraft plans to expand its team—currently around 40 employees—doubling down on R&D and scaling commercial outreach, including an expanding presence in the U.S., following its hiring of a former AWS quantum scientist.

2. The Power of Hardware-Agnostic Quantum Algorithms

Unlike companies manufacturing quantum processors, Phasecraft focuses on software—specifically quantum algorithms optimized for NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum) devices, which are imperfect but available now.

Their approach is hardware-agnostic, meaning their algorithms can run across platforms like Google Quantum AI, IBM, Quantinuum, and QuEra. This flexibility reduces dependency on a single technology path and increases compatibility across evolving quantum hardware.

3. Targeted Applications — Where Phasecraft is Making a Difference

Phasecraft is already collaborating with industry leaders in multiple domains:

  • Materials science: Simulating complex materials with algorithms that make computation up to millions of times more efficient.
  • Energy systems: Partnering with organizations like the UK’s National Energy System Operator and BT to optimize energy grid structures—vital for climate resilience.
  • Pharmaceuticals and chemistry: Novo Holdings’ investment signals Phasecraft’s work is aligned with molecular simulation and drug discovery applications. Hybrid quantum-classical algorithms using approaches like density functional theory enable deeper insights into chemical behavior.

These partnerships indicate early-stage commercialization—and important proof-of-concept validations—for quantum algorithms in real-world scenarios.

4. Messaging and Market Position

Phasecraft is positioning itself as a bridge builder between today’s imperfect quantum systems and tomorrow’s practical applications. CEO Ashley Montanaro foresees “scientifically important” quantum calculations by spring 2026, with commercial applications following within a few years.

Investors echo this optimism. Peter Barrett of Playground Global emphasizes Phasecraft is moving the industry from discovery to design—ushering in a new era of dynamism in chemistry, materials science, and medicine. Ian Hogarth of Plural lauds the firm’s ability to achieve algorithmic efficiencies by factors of millions.

Novo Holdings highlights the potential for quantum algorithms to dramatically shorten drug development time, a game-changer for healthcare.

5. Challenges and the Road Ahead

Despite the optimism, caution persists. Experts warn quantum computing remains uneven in maturity, requiring tempered expectations. The journey to quantum advantage—where quantum systems can outperform classical ones in meaningful tasks—is still unfolding.

Moreover, transitioning from R&D partnerships to full-scale commercialization requires institutional buy-in and proof of consistent value beyond academic validation

Nevertheless, the convergence of investor backing, industrial partnerships, and algorithmic innovation puts Phasecraft in a strong position to lead in quantum software.

Conclusion: Why This Matters

Phasecraft’s $34M Series B funding symbolizes more than a cash infusion—it represents a seismic shift in quantum computing’s trajectory. By focusing on software-driven impact, the firm is bringing powerful algorithms—and, with them, real-world applications—into clearer view. From battery materials to life-saving drugs and smarter energy systems, Phasecraft is building bridges that make quantum computing useful today—not just a promise for tomorrow.
