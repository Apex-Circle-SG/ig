The Metaverse Isn’t a Place: Why Animoca’s Yat Siu Says the Future is 100 Billion AI Agents

2026-05-11

Redefining the Metaverse: Beyond Virtual Geography

For years, the concept of the metaverse was dominated by visions of expansive virtual worlds, high-fidelity 3D avatars, and immersive VR headsets. However, industry visionary and Animoca Brands co-founder Yat Siu has introduced a paradigm-shifting perspective that challenges these spatial assumptions. According to Siu, the metaverse is not a place; it is an ecosystem defined by 100 billion AI agents. This shift in focus from geography to intelligence marks a critical evolution in how we conceive of digital existence, ownership, and interaction.

The Fallacy of the Virtual Destination

The early narrative of the metaverse focused heavily on the idea of a singular, interoperable virtual space where users could live, work, and play. While this vision captured the imagination of tech giants, it often ignored the practical limitations of human bandwidth and the reality of how we interact with digital tools. Yat Siu argues that treating the metaverse as a place creates unnecessary barriers to entry. Instead, he proposes that the metaverse is better understood as a network of agency—a digital layer powered by autonomous entities that facilitate trade, interaction, and content creation.

The Role of AI Agents as Digital Citizens

If the future of the metaverse is built on 100 billion AI agents, these entities cannot simply be background NPCs. They must act as autonomous agents capable of managing assets, executing smart contracts, and navigating digital environments on behalf of human users. In this vision, an AI agent functions as a personal assistant, a financial manager, and a creative collaborator. These agents operate within the Web3 framework, utilizing blockchain technology to ensure that the actions taken by these agents are transparent, secure, and verifiably owned.

Why Agency Matters More Than Graphics

The obsession with high-end graphics and photorealistic environments has often distracted from the true value proposition of the metaverse: utility. By prioritizing the development of AI agents, developers can create ecosystems that provide actual value to users. An agent that can autonomously negotiate a purchase or organize a cross-platform inventory is far more valuable than a beautiful but empty virtual room. This is the cornerstone of the Animoca Brands philosophy: digital ownership combined with artificial intelligence creates a functional economy that transcends physical constraints.

The Intersection of Web3 and Artificial Intelligence

The marriage of blockchain technology and artificial intelligence is where the most significant innovation is currently taking place. While AI provides the capability for autonomous action, blockchain provides the necessary infrastructure for trust and value transfer. When you have 100 billion AI agents operating in a decentralized network, the ability to track ownership and provenance becomes essential. Without blockchain, these agents would be prone to manipulation and centralization. With it, they become the building blocks of a truly sovereign digital economy.

Ownership as the Foundation

A recurring theme in the discourse surrounding the metaverse is the concept of digital property rights. Yat Siu has long championed the idea that users should own their digital assets, from in-game items to social identities. When AI agents are integrated into this system, they become tools that enhance the value of those assets. An AI agent does not just interact with your digital items; it manages, optimizes, and potentially increases their value over time. This creates a symbiotic relationship where the user owns the asset, and the AI agent acts as the engine of that asset’s utility.

Scalability and the 100 Billion Agent Milestone

Reaching a scale of 100 billion AI agents is not merely a technical challenge; it is a structural one. It requires a decentralized network that can handle the massive computational load while maintaining the security of decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols. Animoca Brands is betting that by distributing this workload across a network of interoperable platforms, the metaverse can scale in a way that centralized cloud providers cannot. This decentralized approach ensures that no single entity controls the intelligence layer of the internet.

The Future of Digital Interaction

As we look toward this future, the way we perceive digital interaction will fundamentally change. We will no longer be logging into a platform to see what other humans are doing; we will be interacting with a vast, intelligent fabric of agents that anticipate our needs. This is not the death of human social interaction, but rather the augmentation of it. By offloading mundane, repetitive tasks to AI agents, humans are freed to focus on high-level creative and social endeavors.

The Transformation of the Digital Economy

The economy of the future will be largely driven by machine-to-machine transactions. When AI agents manage the majority of digital commerce, the speed and efficiency of the economy will increase exponentially. This will create new marketplaces where human users set the parameters and AI agents execute the trades. This model removes the friction of traditional commerce and allows for a truly liquid global market that operates 24/7 without human intervention.

Privacy and Security in an AI-Driven Metaverse

Of course, a world populated by 100 billion AI agents brings significant challenges regarding privacy and security. How do we ensure these agents act in our best interests? How do we prevent bad actors from creating malicious agents? The answer lies in the use of decentralized identity and cryptographic verification. By ensuring that every agent is tied to a verifiable digital identity and that its actions are recorded on a ledger, we can build a system that is transparent and accountable. The security of this future depends on our ability to build robust, open-source frameworks for AI behavior.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Yat Siu mean when he says the metaverse is not a place?

Yat Siu is challenging the idea that the metaverse must be a virtual 3D destination like a game or a social hub. Instead, he suggests it is a decentralized network of autonomous agents and services that facilitate digital interactions and economic activity across the internet.

Why are AI agents considered the key to the metaverse?

AI agents provide the necessary autonomy to handle complex digital tasks, manage assets, and interact with other agents at a scale that humans cannot achieve on their own. They transform the metaverse from a passive environment into an active, functional economy.

How does blockchain fit into this AI-driven future?

Blockchain provides the essential layer of trust, ownership, and provenance. It ensures that the actions and assets managed by AI agents are secure, transparent, and truly owned by the users, preventing central authorities from controlling or manipulating the network.

Is this vision of 100 billion agents achievable?

While ambitious, the trend of exponential growth in AI development combined with the increasing adoption of Web3 infrastructure suggests that a massive, decentralized network of agents is technically feasible. The challenge lies in building the interoperability standards necessary for these agents to work together seamlessly.

Will human interaction disappear in this metaverse?

No, the goal is to enhance human interaction by removing the burden of repetitive tasks. By delegating administrative and economic duties to AI agents, humans can focus on the creative and social aspects of digital life that are uniquely human.
