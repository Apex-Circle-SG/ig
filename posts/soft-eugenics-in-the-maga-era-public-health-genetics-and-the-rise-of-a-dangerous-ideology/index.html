Soft Eugenics in the MAGA Era: Public Health, Genetics, and the Rise of a Dangerous Ideology

2025-05-05

In recent years, under the influence of hardline populist ideologies like those associated with the “Make America Great Again” (MAGA) movement, there has been a disturbing re-emergence of policies and rhetoric that mirror the tenets of eugenics—not in its overt, 20th-century form, but in a subtler, more insidious variant: soft eugenics. Rather than explicit sterilization or racial classification, today’s soft eugenics often operates through systemic neglect, underfunded healthcare, and implicit support for genetic determinism.

This article explores how MAGA-aligned policies and cultural narratives are reinforcing a worldview where the vulnerable are left behind—with sickness and poverty seen as personal failings—and how this supports a modern eugenic logic that promotes the survival and reproduction of the “clever” or “fit” over the weak or disadvantaged.

Understanding Soft Eugenics: More Than a Buzzword
Unlike historical eugenics, which relied on government-mandated sterilizations and overt racial hierarchies, soft eugenics promotes genetic and social stratification by omission. It’s often justified through libertarian rhetoric—smaller government, fewer public services, personal responsibility—while the cumulative effect is that access to resources like healthcare, education, and reproductive services becomes stratified along socio-economic lines.

When politicians downplay pandemics, cut public health funding, or vilify welfare recipients, the outcome isn’t random. The sick, poor, and marginalized are increasingly exposed to risk and denied basic protections, while those with wealth, intelligence, or access to technology are encouraged to thrive and reproduce.

MAGA and the Reframing of “Fitness”
At the heart of MAGA ideology lies an appeal to self-sufficiency, strength, and survivalism. There is a glorification of the “strong individual” and a corresponding disdain for perceived weakness, whether physical, economic, or ideological. Public health efforts—vaccination campaigns, disability protections, reproductive healthcare—have often been framed as government overreach or as giving “free handouts” to those who don’t deserve them.

This framing subtly aligns with eugenic principles: let nature take its course, let the weak fail, and let the strong prevail.

For example:

  • Resistance to pandemic precautions (masking, vaccination) is often defended under the banner of freedom, but results in disproportionate harm to the elderly, disabled, and immunocompromised.
  • Cuts to reproductive and maternal health services hit marginalized communities the hardest, while wealthier families maintain access to advanced fertility and genetic screening technologies.
  • Encouragement of “traditional family values” and high birth rates among select groups supports a kind of selective reproduction narrative.

Genetic Fitness in the Digital Age
Soft eugenics today isn’t just about survival—it’s increasingly about breeding for traits. Advances in prenatal genetic testing, embryo selection, and even speculative ideas about CRISPR-based enhancement are gaining traction among those who can afford them.

In MAGA-influenced circles, there’s an emerging cultural undercurrent that celebrates intelligence, entrepreneurial success, and genetic advantage—while stigmatizing those with disabilities, mental illness, or chronic disease. This is not always stated outright, but it emerges in memes, influencer narratives, and policy positions that funnel resources to the “deserving” and pull the ladder up behind them.

Consequences: Inequality and the Loss of Collective Ethics
If left unchecked, soft eugenics could create a society where only the genetically privileged can afford to thrive, and where the vulnerable are seen as expendable. This logic undermines public health systems, erodes empathy, and legitimizes a brutal form of social Darwinism.

Moreover, it draws us away from collective ethical frameworks that emphasize equity, care, and human dignity, replacing them with hyper-individualism and quiet cruelty.

Challenging the Narrative: Advocacy and Awareness
Recognizing soft eugenics is the first step. Advocacy for:

  • Universal healthcare
  • Equitable access to reproductive technology
  • Protection of disability rights
  • Ethical oversight of genetic technologies

…are essential to push back against this ideological drift.

Public discourse must challenge the idea that weakness is a personal failing and instead embrace the value of diversity, interdependence, and care for all.

Conclusion: Resisting a Quiet Repetition of History
History has shown us the dangers of eugenic thinking. While the MAGA era may not resemble the coercive eugenics of the past, its quiet encouragement of genetic and social stratification is no less dangerous. We must remain vigilant against the normalization of policies and rhetoric that devalue human life based on perceived strength, intelligence, or productivity.

The fight against soft eugenics begins with awareness—and with a recommitment to the idea that society is stronger when it uplifts all its members, not just the so-called “fit.”
