Global Entry Fees Increase for the First Time: Here is What Travelers Should Know

2026-05-06

The Era of Cheap Global Entry Ends: Understanding the New Fee Structure

For years, the gold standard for international travelers has been the Global Entry program. Managed by U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP), this program has allowed millions of frequent flyers to skip the grueling customs lines upon returning to the United States. However, in a significant shift for the travel industry, the fees for this highly coveted program have officially increased. If you are planning your next trip abroad, it is essential to understand what these changes mean for your budget and your travel strategy.

The price adjustment marks the first time since the program’s inception in 2008 that the fee has been raised. While nobody enjoys paying more for government services, understanding the ‘why’ behind the increase can help travelers appreciate the value still embedded in the program. This article breaks down everything you need to know about the new costs, the application process, and why your investment in Global Entry remains one of the smartest travel moves you can make.

Why Did Global Entry Fees Increase?

The decision to raise the application fee was not made lightly by the Department of Homeland Security. According to federal officials, the primary driver for the increase is the need to modernize infrastructure and keep pace with the rising costs of conducting comprehensive background checks and managing physical enrollment centers. The program has expanded significantly over the last decade, and the administrative burden of vetting millions of applicants requires substantial investment in technology and personnel.

By increasing the fee, the CBP aims to improve the efficiency of the enrollment process. Many applicants have reported long wait times for conditional approval and difficulties in scheduling in-person interviews. The additional revenue is earmarked to streamline these operations, potentially leading to faster processing times and a more user-friendly digital experience for future applicants.

How Much Does Global Entry Cost Now?

As of the recent policy update, the cost for a five-year Global Entry membership has increased from $100 to $120. While a $20 increase may seem modest, it represents a significant shift for a program that had maintained a stagnant price for over fifteen years. It is important to note that this fee covers the entire five-year duration of the membership, meaning the cost per year is effectively very low, especially when compared to the value of saved time and reduced stress at the airport.

The Impact on Families and Children

One of the most notable changes accompanying the fee hike is a shift in how children are charged. Previously, children were often included under their parents’ applications or faced different requirements. Under the updated guidelines, all applicants, regardless of age, must pay the full fee. This is a critical point for families who are planning to apply for the entire household at once, as the total cost of enrollment will now be higher than in previous years.

Is Global Entry Still Worth It?

Even with the new price tag, Global Entry remains an indispensable tool for international travelers. When you consider the average cost of international airfare and the value of your time, the $120 fee is a relatively small investment. If you travel internationally even once a year, the ability to bypass long customs lines upon arrival in the U.S. is worth its weight in gold.

Furthermore, Global Entry membership includes TSA PreCheck benefits. This means that not only are your international arrivals smoother, but your domestic departures are also significantly faster. By paying for Global Entry, you are effectively covering the cost of two major travel programs in one. For frequent flyers, the time saved in security lines and customs queues can add up to dozens of hours per year, making the $120 fee a bargain in the long run.

How to Offset the New Global Entry Fee

If you are worried about the $120 price tag, you should check your wallet before applying. Many premium travel credit cards offer a statement credit for Global Entry or TSA PreCheck application fees as a standard perk. Cards such as the Chase Sapphire Reserve, Capital One Venture X, and The Platinum Card from American Express all provide this benefit.

To take advantage of this, simply pay the application fee using your eligible credit card. The bank will typically detect the charge and issue a statement credit for the amount within a few billing cycles. This effectively makes the program free for cardholders. Be sure to check the specific terms and conditions of your credit card rewards program to ensure you are eligible for this benefit.

Step-by-Step Guide to Applying for Global Entry

Applying for Global Entry involves a few distinct steps. It is not as simple as filling out a form; it requires a rigorous background check and an in-person interview. Here is the process:

  • Create a Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) Account: Visit the official government website and create your account.
  • Complete the Application: Fill out the detailed online form, providing your travel history, employment information, and residential history for the past five years.
  • Pay the Fee: Submit the $120 non-refundable application fee.
  • Wait for Conditional Approval: This process can take anywhere from a few weeks to several months depending on current demand.
  • Schedule an Interview: Once conditionally approved, you must schedule an interview at an enrollment center. You can also look for ‘Enrollment on Arrival’ options at many international airports.
  • Complete the Interview: Bring your passport and proof of residency to your appointment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does the new fee apply to renewals?

Yes, the $120 fee applies to both new applications and renewals. Every five years, when your membership is due for renewal, you will be required to pay the current fee to maintain your status in the program.

Are there any discounts for seniors or students?

Currently, there are no discounts offered for seniors, students, or other groups. The fee is a flat rate for all applicants, regardless of age or status. However, remember to check if your credit card covers the cost.

How long does the Global Entry membership last?

Your Global Entry membership is valid for five years from your next birthday after the date of approval. It is important to keep track of your expiration date to ensure you can renew your status without a lapse in service.

What happens if my application is denied?

If your application is denied, the $120 fee is non-refundable. This is why it is crucial to ensure all information provided is accurate and that you meet all eligibility requirements, such as having a clean criminal record and valid travel documentation, before submitting your application.

Can I use my Global Entry benefits for my spouse?

No, Global Entry membership is individual. Each traveler must have their own membership and their own Global Entry card. You cannot transfer your benefits to family members or travel companions.

Final Thoughts on the Future of Travel

While price increases are never welcome, the necessity of the Global Entry fee hike reflects the reality of modern travel. As global security requirements become more complex, the investment in technology and human resources to maintain these programs must also grow. By staying informed and utilizing credit card benefits to offset costs, you can continue to enjoy the seamless travel experience that Global Entry provides. As you prepare for your next adventure, consider if the time you will save at the border is worth the cost of the application—for most, the answer is a resounding yes.
