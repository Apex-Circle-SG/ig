FAQ: Understanding Zero Knowledge Proofs

What is a Zero Knowledge Proof?

A Zero Knowledge Proof is a cryptographic method that allows one person to prove to another that a specific statement is true without revealing any information beyond the validity of the statement itself. It is a cornerstone of modern privacy and security.

Why is ZKP considered better than standard blockchain privacy?

Standard privacy often relies on obfuscation, which can be broken by advanced chain analysis. ZKP uses mathematical proofs that provide absolute privacy while maintaining the ability to verify that the transaction is legitimate, making it both secure and compliant.
