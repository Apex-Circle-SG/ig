Unpacking Bitcoin’s May 8 Surge Past $100K: Drivers, Indicators, and Outlook

2025-05-09

The cryptocurrency market witnessed a watershed moment on May 8, 2025, as Bitcoin briefly broke through the $100,000 threshold—its first close above that level since February. This breakout was propelled by a confluence of factors: optimism over a newly announced U.S.–U.K. trade agreement, sizable institutional inflows into Bitcoin ETFs, favorable on-chain metrics, and bullish technical signals. Together, these drivers not only reignited investor confidence but also underscored Bitcoin’s resilience amid recent volatility.

Market Reaction to the U.S.–U.K. Trade Deal

The primary catalyst behind Bitcoin’s sharp uptick was enthusiasm over a bilateral trade agreement announced by U.S. President Donald Trump and U.K. Prime Minister Keir Starmer, which eased fears of renewed tariff escalations. Reuters reported that this development led Bitcoin to surge to $101,329.97—a 4.7% daily gain—after dipping to $74,000 in April, as risk assets rallied on the news. Business Insider highlighted that the deal’s reduction of U.K. import tariffs to 1.8% and maintained moderate U.S. tariffs helped calm market uncertainties that had previously dampened crypto sentiment.

Institutional Inflows and ETF Impact

Institutional demand played a pivotal role in sustaining the rally. Standard Chartered data cited by Business Insider indicated that spot Bitcoin ETF inflows reached $5.3 billion over three weeks, underscoring Wall Street’s appetite for regulated crypto exposures. Fingerlakes1 reported over $1 billion in daily inflows into BTC products on May 8, reflecting robust institutional conviction. Moreover, Barron’s noted that crypto-linked equities—such as Robinhood and Coinbase—jumped 8.6% and 6.1% respectively, mirroring Bitcoin’s momentum and signaling broader market euphoria.

On-Chain Metrics and Technical Indicators

On-chain data provided further evidence of strengthening fundamentals. According to Cointelegraph, Bitcoin’s market dominance rose above 60% at the peak of the rally, a level not seen since early 2021, indicating capital rotation back into BTC from altcoins. Finance Magnates pointed out that realized capitalization hit an all-time high of $891 billion, suggesting increased long-term holder conviction. Technical analysts on Binance’s official blog observed that the Relative Strength Index (RSI) for BTC moved back into bullish territory, and trading volumes spiked ahead of the breakout—classic precursors to sustained price advances.

Macro and Geopolitical Considerations

Beyond trade optimism, broader macro factors contributed to the uptick. Business Insider linked the surge to easing global tariff concerns, which had triggered April’s volatility, and noted that renewed risk-on sentiment extended to equities and commodities alike. Bitcoin.com reported that Chinese stimulus measures and talks between U.S. Treasury officials and Chinese counterparts also helped alleviate geopolitical tensions, creating a more supportive backdrop for crypto assets.

Implications and Future Outlook

The $100K milestone has important ramifications for the crypto ecosystem. Barron’s coverage of the rally emphasized that continued institutional interest—combined with upcoming developments like potential U.S. spot Ether ETF approvals—could sustain upward momentum. TheStreet cautioned, however, that low trading volumes and lingering tariff uncertainties could inject volatility, urging traders to manage risk even amid bullish trends. Looking ahead, many analysts believe Bitcoin could test its previous high of $109,000 if current catalysts persist, while maintaining awareness of macro shifts and regulatory news that may impact market psychology.

Altogether, Bitcoin’s May 8 breakout above $100,000 was not an isolated phenomenon but the culmination of intertwined drivers—trade diplomacy, institutional flows, on-chain strength, and technical validation—suggesting the cryptocurrency is poised for its next chapter in the bull market.
