Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Breakthrough Prize?

The Breakthrough Prize is an international set of awards that recognizes major achievements in life sciences, fundamental physics, and mathematics. It was founded to celebrate scientists and generate excitement about the pursuit of knowledge.

How are the Breakthrough Prize winners selected?

Winners are chosen by a selection committee comprised of previous laureates. The process is highly competitive and involves a confidential peer-review system that evaluates the long-term impact of the research.

Why is the 2026 prize amount significant?

With over $18 million awarded, the 2026 Breakthrough Prize continues to be one of the most lucrative scientific awards in the world. This funding is critical for independent research and allows scientists to expand their labs and hire more talent to solve complex problems.

Can anyone apply for the Breakthrough Prize?

The nomination process is open, but the criteria are extremely strict. Nominations are typically submitted by peers and colleagues who are familiar with the nominee’s contributions to their specific field of study.
