Twist Bioscience Launches Circulating Tumor DNA Reference Controls for Development of Liquid Biopsy Assays

2026-04-28

Revolutionizing Cancer Diagnostics with Precision Controls

In the rapidly evolving field of oncology, liquid biopsy has emerged as a cornerstone technology for non-invasive cancer detection and monitoring. By analyzing circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) from a simple blood draw, clinicians can track tumor dynamics and therapeutic responses in real time. However, the development of robust, high-sensitivity assays requires standardized, high-quality reference materials. Twist Bioscience has recently addressed this critical industry need by launching a new line of ctDNA reference controls, marking a significant milestone in genomic medicine.

The Critical Role of ctDNA in Liquid Biopsy

Liquid biopsy represents a paradigm shift from traditional tissue biopsies. Because tumors shed DNA into the bloodstream, ctDNA analysis provides a comprehensive view of the tumor’s genetic landscape without the risks associated with invasive surgery. However, ctDNA is often present in extremely low concentrations, making detection technically challenging. This is where reference controls become indispensable for assay development, validation, and quality control.

Overcoming Technical Challenges in Assay Development

Assay developers face significant hurdles when attempting to detect low-frequency variants in a complex background of healthy cell-free DNA. Without reliable controls, it is impossible to determine the limit of detection (LoD) or the accuracy of variant calling. Twist Bioscience’s new reference controls provide a synthetic, highly characterized standard that allows researchers to:

  • Calibrate instrumentation for maximum sensitivity.
  • Validate bioinformatics pipelines for variant detection.
  • Ensure consistent performance across different clinical laboratories.
  • Reduce the risk of false positives and false negatives.

Features of Twist Bioscience’s New Reference Controls

Twist Bioscience has utilized its proprietary DNA synthesis platform to create reference standards that mimic the complexity of human samples. These controls are designed to support the development of Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS) based assays, which are currently the gold standard for high-throughput genomic analysis.

Synthetic Precision at Scale

Unlike cell-line derived materials, which can suffer from batch-to-batch variability and genetic drift, Twist’s synthetic controls offer unparalleled consistency. These controls are engineered to represent specific variant allele frequencies (VAFs), allowing developers to test their assays against known concentrations of tumor-derived mutations. Key features include:

  • Customizable VAFs: Ability to simulate different tumor burdens for rigorous testing.
  • High Complexity: Mimics the fragmentation patterns observed in natural plasma samples.
  • Broad Coverage: Includes common cancer-associated mutations to ensure comprehensive assay validation.
  • Scalability: Manufactured with high precision to meet the growing demand for clinical-grade diagnostics.

Impact on the Future of Oncology

The introduction of these reference controls is expected to accelerate the commercialization of liquid biopsy tests. As the industry moves toward earlier cancer detection and personalized treatment strategies, the standardization of testing methodologies becomes paramount. By providing a reliable benchmark, Twist Bioscience is essentially lowering the barrier to entry for diagnostic companies and research institutions alike.

Advancing Precision Medicine

Precision medicine relies on the ability to match the right patient with the right therapy. Liquid biopsies enable longitudinal monitoring, which is critical for identifying resistance mutations as they emerge during treatment. With standardized controls, oncologists can have greater confidence in the results provided by liquid biopsy platforms, ultimately improving patient outcomes and therapeutic decision-making.

Ensuring Quality and Reliability in Clinical Labs

For clinical laboratories, the adoption of Twist Bioscience’s controls facilitates compliance with regulatory requirements. As diagnostic tests undergo validation for clinical use, having documented, reliable control materials is essential for satisfying the rigorous standards set by bodies like the FDA and CLIA. The use of these controls helps laboratories document their assay performance metrics, including sensitivity, specificity, and precision.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What are circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) reference controls?

ctDNA reference controls are engineered DNA materials that simulate the presence of tumor-derived DNA in a background of healthy DNA. They serve as a gold standard to calibrate and validate the performance of liquid biopsy assays used in cancer diagnostics.

Why are Twist Bioscience’s controls better than cell-line based controls?

Twist Bioscience uses synthetic DNA synthesis technology, which eliminates the variability and genetic drift inherent in cell-line derived materials. This ensures that every lot is consistent, stable, and precisely quantified, leading to more reliable diagnostic assay development.

How do these controls help in developing liquid biopsy assays?

They provide known quantities of specific cancer mutations, allowing developers to test their assay’s ability to detect low-frequency variants. This helps in determining the limit of detection (LoD) and ensuring that the assay is sensitive enough for clinical application.

Can these controls be used for regulatory submission?

Yes, because these controls are highly characterized and manufactured under strict quality control standards, they are suitable for use in the validation studies required for regulatory submissions and laboratory accreditation.

How does this launch affect the oncology field?

By standardizing the validation process, this launch enables faster development of high-quality liquid biopsy tests. This leads to more reliable, non-invasive cancer monitoring and earlier detection, which are critical components of modern precision oncology.

Conclusion

Twist Bioscience’s commitment to providing high-quality genomic tools is evident in the launch of their new ctDNA reference controls. By providing the precision and consistency necessary for the next generation of diagnostics, they are empowering researchers and clinicians to push the boundaries of what is possible in cancer care. As liquid biopsy technology continues to mature, these reference materials will undoubtedly play a vital role in ensuring that the next wave of cancer diagnostics is both accurate and accessible, ultimately benefiting patients worldwide.
