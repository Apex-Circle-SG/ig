Save Old Candle Lids For Useful Upcycled Coaster DIY: A Creative Guide

2026-05-08

Why You Should Save Your Old Candle Lids

In our modern era of home fragrance obsession, most of us have a drawer overflowing with empty candle jars. While the jars themselves are often repurposed, the lids are frequently tossed into the trash without a second thought. However, these metal, wooden, or glass discs are the perfect size, shape, and weight to be transformed into upcycled coasters. By saving your old candle lids, you are not only reducing household waste but also creating a bespoke piece of decor that fits perfectly with your home aesthetic.

The Benefits of Upcycling Candle Lids

Choosing to upcycle is an act of environmental stewardship. When you repurpose items like candle lids, you extend the lifecycle of materials that would otherwise end up in a landfill. Furthermore, DIY coasters allow for endless creativity. Whether you love a minimalist look, a farmhouse vibe, or a bohemian style, you can customize your coasters to match your furniture and personality. Plus, the cost is minimal, often requiring only items you already have at home.

What You Will Need for Your DIY Project

Before you begin, gather your supplies. The following items are essential for creating high-quality, durable upcycled candle lid coasters:

  • Cleaned candle lids (metal, wood, or ceramic)
  • Adhesive felt or cork sheets
  • Decorative paper, fabric, or paint
  • Mod Podge or clear sealant
  • Hot glue gun or industrial-strength glue
  • Scissors or a craft knife

Step-by-Step Guide to Creating Candle Lid Coasters

Step 1: Preparing the Lids

The most important step in any DIY upcycling project is preparation. Ensure your candle lids are completely free of wax residue. You can achieve this by placing the lid in the freezer for an hour, which causes the wax to harden and pop off easily. Once the wax is removed, wipe the surface with rubbing alcohol to ensure it is clean and dry. This ensures that your adhesive or paint will bond properly to the material.

Step 2: Designing Your Coasters

This is where your creativity shines. If your candle lids have a logo or brand name that you want to hide, consider painting them with a high-quality metallic spray paint or acrylic paint. Alternatively, you can decoupage decorative paper or fabric onto the top surface. For a more sophisticated look, consider gluing a thin piece of marble-patterned contact paper or even a slice of decorative tile to the top of the lid.

Step 3: Adding Protection

A coaster is only useful if it protects your furniture. Since most candle lids are made of metal or rigid wood, they can scratch delicate surfaces. To prevent this, cut a circular piece of adhesive cork or felt slightly smaller than the base of the lid. Attach this to the bottom of your lid. This adds a non-slip grip and ensures your coffee table remains pristine, even when holding a cold beverage.

Step 4: Sealing the Finish

If you have used paper or fabric to decorate your upcycled coasters, you must apply a sealant. Use a waterproof sealer like Mod Podge Outdoor or a clear acrylic spray. This prevents condensation from a cold glass from ruining your beautiful design. Apply at least two thin coats, allowing for proper drying time in between, to ensure maximum durability.

Creative Ideas for Your Coasters

If you are looking for inspiration, consider these unique themes for your DIY coasters:

  • Vintage Map Coasters: Cut out circles from old maps to create a nostalgic, travel-inspired set.
  • Pressed Flower Coasters: Place dried flowers under a clear resin pour for a botanical, elegant finish.
  • Minimalist Metallic: Use gold or copper spray paint for a sleek, modern look that complements high-end interiors.
  • Fabric Scraps: Use leftover fabric from sewing projects to create a colorful, eclectic set of coasters.

Maintaining Your Upcycled Coasters

Even though these coasters are made from repurposed materials, they can last for years with proper care. Avoid submerging them in water, as this can degrade the glue and the finish. Instead, wipe them down with a damp cloth if they get dirty. If you used a high-quality sealant, they will be resistant to moisture, but it is always best to wipe up spills immediately to preserve the integrity of your hard work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use plastic candle lids for these coasters?

While you can technically use plastic lids, they are often less durable and may not handle heat as well as metal or wooden lids. If you choose to use plastic, ensure you use a heat-resistant sealant and check that the material is thick enough to act as a stable base for a glass.

What is the best glue to use for these projects?

For attaching felt or cork to the bottom, a strong craft glue or E6000 works best. If you are attaching decorative elements to the top, Mod Podge is ideal for paper, while a hot glue gun is better for heavier embellishments like buttons or beads.

Are these coasters heat-proof?

Most candle lids are designed to withstand heat, but adding decorative layers like paper or fabric can change their heat tolerance. For hot coffee mugs, ensure your coasters are thick enough to insulate the table surface. Using cork as a base layer provides excellent thermal protection.

Can I make these to give as gifts?

Absolutely! A set of four upcycled candle lid coasters wrapped in twine makes a thoughtful, eco-friendly gift for housewarmings or birthdays. People love receiving handmade items that have a story behind them.

These coasters are not just functional items; they are conversation starters that showcase your creativity and commitment to the environment.
