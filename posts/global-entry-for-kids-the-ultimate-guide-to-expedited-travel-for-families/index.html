Global Entry for Kids: The Ultimate Guide to Expedited Travel for Families

2026-04-28

Is Global Entry Worth It for Families?

Traveling with children can be a logistical challenge, especially when navigating crowded airports and long security lines. Many families are turning to Global Entry, the U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States. A common question parents have is whether their children need their own membership. The answer is a resounding yes. Unlike some other programs, every individual, regardless of age, must have their own Global Entry membership to utilize the dedicated kiosks.

While the process requires an initial investment of time and money, the benefits for frequent travelers are significant. By securing Global Entry for kids, you can bypass traditional customs queues, reduce travel-related stress, and ensure your entire family moves through the airport efficiently. This guide will walk you through the eligibility, application process, and essential tips for a successful enrollment.

Understanding Global Entry Eligibility for Children

The most important rule regarding Global Entry is that there is no minimum age requirement. Whether you are traveling with a newborn or a teenager, your child is eligible to apply. However, there are specific criteria that every applicant must meet to be approved.

The Eligibility Requirements

  • Citizenship: Applicants must be U.S. citizens, lawful permanent residents, or citizens of select countries that have an agreement with the U.S. government.
  • Clean Record: All applicants must undergo a rigorous background check. This includes a review of criminal history and law enforcement databases.
  • No Prior Violations: Any history of customs, immigration, or agricultural violations can lead to an automatic denial of the application.

It is worth noting that even if a child has a clean record, they are still subject to the same vetting process as adults. Parents should ensure that all information provided in the application is accurate and matches the child’s official government documentation.

Step-by-Step Guide: How to Apply for Global Entry for Kids

Applying for your child is a straightforward process, but it requires attention to detail. Follow these steps to ensure your application is submitted correctly.

Step 1: Create a Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) Account

To begin, you must create an account on the official Trusted Traveler Program (TTP) website. Parents should manage the account for their children. While you can use one email address to manage multiple accounts, it is often easier to create separate TTP accounts for each family member to avoid confusion during the renewal process.

Step 2: Complete the Online Application

Once logged in, select the Global Entry option. You will be asked to provide detailed information about your child, including passport details, residential history, and travel history. Be prepared to scan your child’s passport and provide proof of residency if applicable.

Step 3: Pay the Application Fee

The non-refundable application fee must be paid during the submission process. Many premium travel credit cards offer a statement credit for Global Entry application fees, so check your credit card benefits before paying to see if you can be reimbursed for the cost.

Step 4: Schedule and Attend the Interview

After your application reaches the ‘conditionally approved’ status, you must schedule an in-person interview at an enrollment center. Every child must be present for the interview, regardless of age. During the interview, a CBP officer will verify the documentation and take the child’s fingerprints (or a photograph for younger children who cannot provide fingerprints).

Essential Tips for a Smooth Interview

The interview is the final hurdle in obtaining Global Entry for your children. Here are some expert tips to ensure everything goes smoothly:

  • Bring All Original Documents: Always bring the child’s current passport, birth certificate, and proof of address for the parents. Having these documents organized in a folder will speed up the process.
  • Prepare Your Child: If your child is old enough to understand, explain that they will be meeting a government official who will ask simple questions about travel. Keeping the child calm and cooperative makes the experience much easier for the officer.
  • Arrive Early: Enrollment centers are often busy. Aim to arrive at least 15 minutes before your scheduled appointment time.
  • Use Enrollment on Arrival: If you have an upcoming international trip, you might be able to complete your interview at the airport when you land. The Enrollment on Arrival (EoA) program allows conditionally approved applicants to complete their interview without a prior appointment at select airports.

The Benefits of Global Entry for Families

Beyond the obvious time savings at customs, Global Entry offers several secondary benefits that make family travel more manageable.

TSA PreCheck Integration

One of the most valuable perks of Global Entry is that it includes TSA PreCheck. This allows your family to use the expedited security lines at domestic airports. You won’t have to remove your shoes, laptops, or light jackets, which is a massive relief when you are trying to manage children, strollers, and carry-on bags simultaneously.

Reduced Travel Anxiety

The predictability of Global Entry and TSA PreCheck significantly reduces the chaos of air travel. Knowing that you have a streamlined path through the airport allows you to plan your travel day with more confidence, leading to a more relaxed experience for both parents and children.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions About Global Entry for Kids

Does my baby need their own Global Entry membership?

Yes. Every traveler, regardless of age, must have their own individual Global Entry membership. You cannot add a child to a parent’s account.

What happens if my child’s passport expires?

If your child’s passport expires, you must update the information in your TTP account. You can usually do this online, but sometimes you may need to visit an enrollment center to have the new passport verified.

Is the Global Entry fee refundable if my child is denied?

No. The application fee is non-refundable, regardless of whether your child is approved or denied by the CBP.

Can I bring my child to my own interview?

If your child is also conditionally approved, you should try to schedule your interviews together. However, you must have an appointment for each person, and you cannot simply bring a child to your appointment if they have not been processed for an interview themselves.

Does Global Entry work for domestic flights?

Global Entry is specifically for international customs, but the included TSA PreCheck benefit is valid for all domestic flights departing from U.S. airports.

Final Thoughts on Global Entry for Families

Investing in Global Entry for kids is a smart move for any family that travels internationally even once or twice a year. The time saved in security and customs lines is invaluable, and the added benefit of TSA PreCheck enhances the entire travel experience. While the application process requires some effort, the long-term rewards of stress-free airport navigation make it well worth the investment. Start the process early, keep your documentation organized, and get ready for smoother family adventures ahead.
