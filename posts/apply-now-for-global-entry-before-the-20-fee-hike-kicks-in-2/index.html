Understanding the Global Entry Fee Increase

The U.S. government periodically reviews the costs associated with managing the Trusted Traveler Programs. To maintain the security standards and operational efficiency of these programs, officials have determined that a price adjustment is necessary. For years, the fee remained stagnant, but the upcoming shift represents a significant jump for new applicants and those looking to renew.

By applying before the deadline, you are not just saving a percentage of the cost; you are ensuring that your membership is active for the next five years at the lower rate. Even if your current membership is not expiring immediately, it is worth investigating whether you can renew early or if you should act now to avoid the budgetary impact of the new fee structure.

Why Global Entry Remains a Travel Essential

Even with a price increase, the benefits of Global Entry far outweigh the cost for anyone who leaves the country more than once a year. The program provides several key advantages:

  • Expedited Customs Processing: Skip the long lines at immigration by using automated kiosks or facial recognition technology.
  • TSA PreCheck Included: Every Global Entry member automatically receives TSA PreCheck benefits, allowing you to breeze through domestic security checkpoints without removing your shoes, laptops, or liquids.
  • Time Savings: On average, Global Entry members clear customs in a fraction of the time it takes standard travelers, often moving from the plane to the curb in minutes.
  • Peace of Mind: Knowing you have a fast-track option at the airport reduces the stress associated with tight connections and long international flights.

Step-by-Step Guide to Applying for Global Entry

The application process can seem daunting, but it is straightforward if you have your documents ready. Here is how to get your application submitted before the price hike.

1. Create a Trusted Traveler Program Account

The first step is visiting the official TTP website. You will need to create a login.gov account. This portal serves as the hub for all your Global Entry needs, from initial application to renewal and status updates.

2. Fill Out the Application Carefully

The application asks for extensive details regarding your employment history, residential history, and international travel records. Accuracy is paramount. Even minor errors or inconsistencies can lead to delays or, in some cases, a denial of your application. Take your time to ensure all addresses and dates are correct.

3. Pay the Application Fee

This is the most important step regarding the price hike. By paying the current fee now, you secure your spot in the queue. Keep in mind that many premium credit cards offer statement credits that reimburse the cost of the Global Entry application. Check your credit card benefits portal to see if your card covers this expense, effectively making your membership free.

4. Schedule Your Interview

Once your application is conditionally approved, you must schedule an in-person interview at a Global Entry Enrollment Center. During this interview, a Customs and Border Protection officer will verify your documents, take your fingerprints, and ask a few questions about your travel history.

Tips for Securing an Interview Faster

One of the biggest hurdles in the Global Entry process is finding an available interview slot. High demand often leads to months of waiting. Here are some strategies to speed up the process:

  • Check for Cancellations: Enrollment centers frequently have last-minute cancellations. Check the portal daily to snag an opening that someone else gave up.
  • Enrollment on Arrival: If you are already planning an international trip, you can complete your interview upon returning to the United States at participating airports. This is often the fastest way to get approved.
  • Remote Interviews: Keep an eye on government announcements regarding remote interview options, which have been expanded to help clear backlogs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the fee hike already in effect?

The fee increase is scheduled to take effect soon. It is highly recommended to check the official U.S. Customs and Border Protection website immediately to confirm the exact date and ensure your application is submitted before the deadline passes.

Does the Global Entry fee include TSA PreCheck?

Yes, Global Entry membership includes TSA PreCheck at no additional cost. This is one of the primary reasons the program is considered such a high-value investment for travelers.

Can I get reimbursed for the fee?

Many premium travel credit cards, such as the Chase Sapphire Reserve, Amex Platinum, and Capital One Venture X, offer a credit for the Global Entry application fee once every four or five years. Check your card benefits to see if you are eligible.

What happens if I apply but do not get approved?

If your application is denied, the application fee is non-refundable. This is why it is essential to ensure all information provided on your application is accurate and truthful. Ensure you do not have any disqualifying criminal records or customs violations on your history.

How long does the Global Entry membership last?

Once approved, your Global Entry membership is valid for five years. You should begin the renewal process several months before your membership expires to avoid any lapse in your benefits.
