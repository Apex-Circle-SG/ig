What are Real World Assets?

Real World Assets refer to tangible or intangible assets that exist outside of the digital blockchain ecosystem but are brought on-chain via tokenization. This includes real estate, precious metals, fine art, private equity, and government bonds. By converting these assets into security tokens, issuers can enable fractional ownership, increase liquidity for historically illiquid assets, and provide 24/7 global trading capabilities.

Why Tokenization Matters for Investors

  • Fractional Ownership: Allows smaller investors to gain exposure to high-value assets like commercial real estate or fine art.
  • Increased Liquidity: Assets that were previously locked for years can now be traded in secondary markets.
  • Transparency: Blockchain ledgers provide an immutable record of ownership and transaction history.
  • Cost Reduction: By removing intermediaries such as brokers and clearinghouses, the cost of asset management is significantly lowered.
