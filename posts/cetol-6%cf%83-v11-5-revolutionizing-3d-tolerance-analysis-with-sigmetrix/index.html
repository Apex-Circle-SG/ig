Why Tolerance Analysis Matters in Modern Engineering

Tolerance analysis is more than just checking if parts fit together; it is a strategic approach to quality assurance and cost management. When designs are finalized without a rigorous understanding of dimensional variation, the consequences can be severe. From excessive rework and high scrap rates to inconsistent product performance, the costs of poor tolerance management are significant.

The Role of Variation Management

Every manufactured part contains inherent variation. Whether it is due to material properties, machine setup, or environmental factors, these deviations accumulate across an assembly. CETOL 6σ v11.5 provides engineers with a sophisticated environment to model these variations accurately. By utilizing advanced mathematical algorithms, the software predicts how individual part tolerances impact the final assembly, allowing for data-driven decisions that balance performance with manufacturing costs.
