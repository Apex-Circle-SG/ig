How and When to Renew Your Global Entry for Smooth Travels: A Complete Guide

2026-05-03

Understanding the Global Entry Renewal Process

For frequent international travelers, the Global Entry program is an essential tool. By providing expedited clearance through customs and border protection, it saves countless hours at busy airports. However, many travelers are caught off guard when their membership expires. Understanding the Global Entry renewal timeline is critical to ensuring you maintain your trusted traveler status without interruption.

Membership in the Global Entry program is valid for five years. Because the renewal process can sometimes involve administrative delays or the need for a new interview, it is vital to be proactive. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about keeping your status active.

When Should You Renew Your Global Entry?

The U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) allows you to submit your renewal application up to one year before your current membership expires. It is highly recommended that you submit your application as soon as that one-year window opens.

Why Early Renewal Matters

If you submit your application before your current membership expires, you are granted a grace period. Even if your current membership technically expires while your renewal is still being processed, you can continue to use your Global Entry benefits for up to 24 months after the expiration date. This ensures that you do not lose your expedited screening privileges while waiting for the CBP to finalize your background check.

How to Renew Your Global Entry Step-by-Step

The renewal process is managed entirely through the Trusted Traveler Programs (TTP) website. Follow these steps to ensure a smooth transition:

  • Log in to your TTP account: Navigate to the official government portal and sign in with your Login.gov credentials.
  • Locate the renewal option: Once logged in, you will see a button labeled Renew Membership if you are within the one-year eligibility window.
  • Update your personal information: Review your current details, including your passport information, address, and employment history. Ensure that all data is current and accurate to avoid processing delays.
  • Pay the renewal fee: As of the latest update, the fee is 120 USD. Many premium travel credit cards offer a statement credit that covers this cost, so check your benefits before paying out of pocket.
  • Submit the application: After reviewing your information, submit the application and keep a copy of your confirmation number.

What Happens After You Submit Your Renewal?

Once your application is submitted, it enters the review phase. In many cases, the process is automated, and you may receive an approval notification within a few weeks. However, some applicants are flagged for a more thorough review, which may require an in-person interview.

The Interview Requirement

If you are selected for an interview, you will receive a notification in your TTP account. You must then schedule an appointment at a Global Entry Enrollment Center. Keep in mind that appointment slots can be limited, so check back frequently if you do not see an immediate opening. Alternatively, you may be eligible for Enrollment on Arrival, which allows you to complete your interview during your next international return trip at participating airports.

Tips for a Smooth Renewal Experience

To avoid common pitfalls, keep these best practices in mind:

  • Keep your contact info current: If your email address changes, update it in your TTP account immediately so you do not miss important notifications regarding your status.
  • Check your passport validity: Ensure your passport has at least six months of validity remaining. If you renew your passport, you must update your passport details in the TTP portal.
  • Check for credit card benefits: Many travelers forget that their credit card pays for this. Check your card agreement to see if you are eligible for a reimbursement.
  • Monitor your status: Log in to your TTP account every few months to check the status of your application if you have not heard back after 60 days.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if my Global Entry expires before I renew?

If your membership expires and you have not submitted a renewal application, your benefits will terminate immediately. You will no longer be able to use the Global Entry kiosks or receive TSA PreCheck benefits. You will have to reapply as a new applicant, which may involve a longer processing time and a full new background check.

Is there a grace period for renewal?

Yes, but only if you submit your renewal application before your expiration date. If submitted on time, you are granted a 24-month grace period to continue using your benefits while your renewal is pending.

Do I need to visit an office for every renewal?

Not necessarily. Many members are approved without an additional interview. However, the CBP reserves the right to request an interview for any applicant, regardless of their history.

Can I renew if my personal information has changed?

Yes. In fact, you are required to update your information during the renewal process. Whether you have moved, changed jobs, or obtained a new passport, ensure that all information in your TTP profile is 100% accurate before clicking submit.

Does Global Entry include TSA PreCheck?

Yes, Global Entry membership includes TSA PreCheck benefits. When you renew your Global Entry, your TSA PreCheck privileges are automatically renewed as well, provided your Known Traveler Number (KTN) is correctly entered into your airline reservations.

Final Thoughts on Keeping Your Status Active

Global Entry is a major convenience for those who travel internationally. By staying on top of your renewal timeline and ensuring your profile is always up to date, you can avoid the headache of losing your status. Remember to mark your calendar for one year before your expiration date to ensure you have the best possible experience. Safe travels!
