The Best Time to Renew Global Entry—and How to Do It

2026-02-25

Introduction

Global Entry is a program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States. With the increasing popularity of international travel, renewing your Global Entry membership at the right time is crucial to avoid any disruptions in your travel plans. In this comprehensive guide, we will discuss the best time to renew your Global Entry and provide you with a step-by-step process to ensure a smooth renewal.

Understanding Global Entry

Before diving into the renewal process, it’s essential to understand what Global Entry is and its benefits. Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States. Members can use automated kiosks at select airports, bypassing long lines and reducing wait times.

Benefits of Global Entry

  • Expedited clearance at U.S. airports
  • Access to TSA PreCheck benefits
  • Reduced wait times
  • Convenient and efficient travel experience

When to Renew Global Entry

The best time to renew your Global Entry is approximately 6 months before your membership expires. This timeframe allows ample time for processing and ensures that your membership does not lapse. Here are some key points to consider:

Processing Time

The renewal process can take several weeks to a few months, depending on various factors such as background checks and interview availability. Starting the renewal process early helps avoid any last-minute issues.

Avoiding Lapse in Membership

Renewing your Global Entry 6 months before expiration ensures that you do not experience any lapse in membership, which could result in longer wait times at customs.

Travel Plans

Consider your upcoming travel plans when deciding when to renew. If you have international trips planned, ensure your Global Entry is active to take advantage of the benefits.

How to Renew Global Entry

Renewing your Global Entry is a straightforward process. Follow these steps to ensure a smooth renewal:

Step 1: Check Your Eligibility

Before starting the renewal process, ensure that you are still eligible for Global Entry. Eligibility criteria include being a U.S. citizen, U.S. lawful permanent resident, or citizen of certain other countries.

Step 2: Log in to Your GOES Account

Visit the Global Online Enrollment System (GOES) website and log in using your existing credentials. If you don’t have an account, you will need to create one.

Step 3: Complete the Renewal Application

Once logged in, navigate to the renewal section and complete the application form. Ensure that all information is accurate and up-to-date.

Step 4: Pay the Renewal Fee

The renewal fee for Global Entry is $100, which is valid for five years. Payment can be made online using a credit card.

Step 5: Schedule an Interview (if required)

In some cases, you may be required to attend an interview as part of the renewal process. If an interview is necessary, schedule it at a convenient time and location.

Step 6: Await Approval

After submitting your renewal application and completing any required interviews, await approval from CBP. You will receive an email notification once your renewal is approved.

Tips for a Smooth Renewal Process

Here are some additional tips to ensure a smooth renewal process:

Keep Your Information Updated

Ensure that your personal information, such as address and contact details, is up-to-date in your GOES account. This helps avoid any delays in processing your renewal.

Be Prepared for the Interview

If an interview is required, be prepared to answer questions about your travel history and any changes in your circumstances since your initial application.

Monitor Your Application Status

Regularly check the status of your renewal application through your GOES account. This helps you stay informed about any updates or additional requirements.

Common Issues and Solutions

While the renewal process is generally straightforward, some common issues may arise. Here are a few potential problems and their solutions:

Delayed Processing

If your renewal is taking longer than expected, contact CBP for assistance. They can provide updates on your application status and any potential delays.

Eligibility Concerns

If you encounter any issues with your eligibility, review the criteria and ensure that you meet all requirements. If necessary, seek guidance from CBP.

Technical Issues

If you experience technical difficulties with the GOES website, try clearing your browser cache or using a different browser. If problems persist, contact the GOES helpdesk for support.

Conclusion

Renewing your Global Entry membership at the right time is essential to ensure a seamless travel experience. By following the steps outlined in this guide and considering the tips provided, you can navigate the renewal process with ease. Remember to start the renewal process approximately 6 months before your membership expires to avoid any disruptions in your travel plans.
