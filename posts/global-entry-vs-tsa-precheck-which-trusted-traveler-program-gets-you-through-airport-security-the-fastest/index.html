Global Entry vs. TSA PreCheck: Which Trusted Traveler Program Gets You Through Airport Security the Fastest?

2026-03-21

Introduction

Standing in long airport security lines can be the most frustrating part of air travel. Between removing shoes, taking out laptops, and waiting for your turn at the scanner, the process can eat up valuable time before your flight. This is where trusted traveler programs like Global Entry and TSA PreCheck come in. Both programs are designed to expedite your airport security experience, but which one actually gets you through the fastest? In this comprehensive comparison, we’ll break down the key differences between Global Entry and TSA PreCheck to help you decide which prescreening option is right for your travel needs.

Understanding TSA PreCheck

TSA PreCheck is a U.S. government program that allows pre-approved travelers to access expedited security screening at participating airports. The program was created to enhance security while improving the passenger experience by reducing wait times and streamlining the screening process.

Benefits of TSA PreCheck

  • Shorter security lines dedicated to PreCheck members
  • Keep shoes, belts, and light jackets on
  • Leave laptops and compliant liquids in your carry-on
  • Typically wait times under 5 minutes
  • Valid for 5 years

Cost and Application Process

The application fee for TSA PreCheck is $78 for a 5-year membership. The process involves:

  1. Online application submission
  2. Background check
  3. In-person appointment for fingerprinting and verification

Most applicants receive approval within 3-5 days, though some may take longer depending on background check results.

Understanding Global Entry

Global Entry is a U.S. Customs and Border Protection program that allows expedited clearance for pre-approved, low-risk travelers upon arrival in the United States. While it includes all the benefits of TSA PreCheck, it also provides expedited entry at U.S. customs when returning from international travel.

Benefits of Global Entry

  • All TSA PreCheck benefits
  • Expedited entry at U.S. customs using automated kiosks
  • No paperwork or long lines when re-entering the U.S.
  • Valid for 5 years

Cost and Application Process

The application fee for Global Entry is $100 for a 5-year membership. The process includes:

  1. Online application submission
  2. Background check
  3. In-person interview at a Global Entry enrollment center or enrollment on arrival at certain airports

The approval process typically takes 1-2 weeks, though interviews may need to be scheduled weeks in advance depending on location.

Key Differences Between Global Entry and TSA PreCheck

Coverage and Convenience

The most significant difference is that Global Entry includes TSA PreCheck benefits plus international customs clearance. If you travel internationally even occasionally, Global Entry provides more comprehensive coverage. However, if you only travel domestically, TSA PreCheck might be sufficient for your needs.

Cost Comparison

While TSA PreCheck costs $78 for 5 years, Global Entry costs $100 for the same period. The $22 difference essentially gives you international customs benefits, making Global Entry the better value for most travelers who might take an international trip within the 5-year period.

Application Process and Wait Times

TSA PreCheck typically has a faster, simpler application process with more available appointment times. Global Entry requires an in-person interview, which can be harder to schedule and may have longer wait times for approval, especially in busy metropolitan areas.

Availability at Airports

TSA PreCheck is available at over 200 airports and through 85 airlines in the U.S. Global Entry is available at more than 75 airports and through all major U.S. airlines. Both programs are continually expanding their airport coverage.

Which Program Gets You Through Security the Fastest?

Domestic Travel

For domestic travel, both programs offer similar security screening benefits. TSA PreCheck members typically experience wait times under 5 minutes, while Global Entry members enjoy the same expedited screening since Global Entry includes TSA PreCheck. In this case, both programs are equally fast for getting through security.

International Travel

When it comes to international travel, Global Entry is significantly faster overall. Not only do you get the expedited security screening, but you also bypass long customs lines when returning to the U.S. Using Global Entry kiosks, you can complete your customs declaration in minutes rather than waiting in potentially lengthy immigration lines that can exceed an hour during peak times.

Real-World Time Savings

Consider this scenario: A family of four returns from an international vacation. Without Global Entry, they might wait 45-60 minutes in customs. With Global Entry, they could be through customs in under 10 minutes, saving 35-50 minutes. For domestic connections or those with time-sensitive commitments, this difference is substantial.

Who Should Choose TSA PreCheck?

TSA PreCheck might be the right choice if:

  • You only travel domestically within the U.S.
  • You want the simplest, quickest application process
  • You’re not interested in international travel
  • You prefer lower upfront costs
  • You value convenience over comprehensive coverage

Who Should Choose Global Entry?

Global Entry is the better option if:

  • You travel internationally at least once every few years
  • You want the most comprehensive travel benefits
  • You frequently return to the U.S. from abroad
  • You value time savings at both security and customs
  • You want the best overall value for your investment

Additional Considerations

Premium Credit Card Reimbursements

Many premium travel credit cards offer statement credits for application fees for both TSA PreCheck and Global Entry. Cards like Chase Sapphire Reserve, Capital One Venture, and American Express Platinum frequently cover the full cost of either program, making the financial decision easier.

Family Members

Children under 18 can accompany a Global Entry or TSA PreCheck member through expedited screening without their own membership. However, for Global Entry specifically, all minors must have their own membership when traveling with the family, as the customs kiosks require individual processing.

Mobile Passport vs. Global Entry

Some travelers wonder about Mobile Passport, a free app that offers similar customs benefits. While Mobile Passport is free and doesn’t require pre-approval, it’s available at fewer airports than Global Entry and may have longer wait times as it becomes more popular.

Conclusion

When comparing Global Entry vs. TSA PreCheck for getting through airport security the fastest, both programs offer excellent expedited screening benefits. For domestic travel, they’re essentially equivalent in terms of security line speed. However, Global Entry pulls ahead significantly when considering overall travel efficiency, especially for international travelers.

The $22 price difference between the programs is minimal when considering that Global Entry includes all TSA PreCheck benefits plus international customs clearance. For most travelers, especially those who might take an international trip within the 5-year membership period, Global Entry represents the better value and fastest overall airport experience.

Ultimately, your travel patterns should guide your decision. If you’re certain you’ll only travel domestically, TSA PreCheck offers a simpler application process. But if there’s any chance you’ll travel internationally, Global Entry provides the fastest, most comprehensive airport experience from curb to gate.

FAQ

Can I use TSA PreCheck or Global Entry for every flight?

TSA PreCheck and Global Entry benefits are subject to availability and may not appear on your boarding pass for every flight. Factors include airline participation, airport facilities, and random security protocols. However, most members receive PreCheck benefits on the majority of their flights.

How long does it take to get approved for these programs?

TSA PreCheck approval typically takes 3-5 days after submitting your application and completing your in-person appointment. Global Entry approval generally takes 1-2 weeks, though this can vary based on background check results and interview availability.

Can I renew my membership before it expires?

Yes, both programs allow renewal up to one year before your membership expires. It’s recommended to start the renewal process 6-8 months before expiration to ensure continuous coverage without any gaps.

Do these programs guarantee I won’t wait in line?

No program can guarantee zero wait times. While both TSA PreCheck and Global Entry significantly reduce typical wait times, you may occasionally encounter lines, particularly during peak travel periods or at airports with limited expedited screening lanes.

Can I transfer my membership to someone else?

No, TSA PreCheck and Global Entry memberships are non-transferable and tied to your specific identity. You must use your own membership and cannot share benefits with others who aren’t traveling with you.
