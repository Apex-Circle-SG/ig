Ivy Parks of BD-Canada Appointed Chair of the Medtech Canada Board of Directors

2026-05-12

Historic Leadership Appointment: Ivy Parks Takes the Helm at Medtech Canada

In a landmark decision that signals a new era for the Canadian medical technology sector, Ivy Parks, the Country President of BD-Canada, has been appointed as the new Chair of the Medtech Canada Board of Directors. This strategic appointment underscores the critical role that innovative leadership plays in navigating the complex landscape of healthcare delivery, regulatory frameworks, and technological advancement. As the head of one of the world’s leading medical technology companies operating within Canada, Parks brings a wealth of experience, strategic vision, and a deep commitment to patient outcomes to this prestigious volunteer role.

The appointment comes at a pivotal time for the industry. Canada’s healthcare system is undergoing significant transformation, driven by the need for digital integration, supply chain resilience, and equitable access to cutting-edge treatments. With Parks at the helm, Medtech Canada is poised to amplify its advocacy efforts, ensuring that policy decisions reflect the realities of modern medical practice and the urgent needs of patients across the country. Her leadership is expected to foster stronger collaboration between industry stakeholders, government bodies, and healthcare providers.

Serving as the Chair of the Medtech Canada Board is not merely an honorary title; it is a position of immense responsibility. Medtech Canada: Advocating for Innovation and Access

Medtech Canada serves as the national voice for the medical technology industry, representing companies that develop, manufacture, and distribute the devices and technologies essential to modern healthcare. The organization plays a crucial role in advocating for policies that facilitate timely access to innovative treatments for Canadian patients. With over 140 member companies, Medtech Canada is a powerful conduit for dialogue between the private sector and public policymakers. The appointment of Ivy Parks as Chair strengthens the organization’s capacity to influence positive change.

The organization focuses on several key areas, including regulatory modernization, intellectual property protection, and the promotion of Canadian innovation on the global stage. Under new leadership, there is an expectation that Medtech Canada will intensify its efforts to address the backlog in regulatory approvals and ensure that Canadian patients have access to the same advanced technologies available in other developed nations. The synergy between Parks’ operational expertise and Medtech Canada’s advocacy mandate creates a potent combination for driving progress.

Priorities for the Future of Canadian Medtech

Looking ahead, the collaboration between Ivy Parks and Medtech Canada is expected to focus on several strategic priorities. First and foremost is the acceleration of regulatory reform. Additionally, the organization will likely address the issue of supply chain security. Parks’ experience in managing complex logistics and global operations will be invaluable in crafting strategies to mitigate future risks. Finally, fostering a culture of innovation through increased investment in research and development remains a core objective. By supporting Canadian startups and encouraging established players to innovate locally, the sector can drive economic growth while improving health outcomes.
