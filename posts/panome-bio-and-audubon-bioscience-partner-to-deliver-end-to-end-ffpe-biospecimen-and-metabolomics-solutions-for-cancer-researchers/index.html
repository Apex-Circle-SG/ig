Panome Bio and Audubon Bioscience Partner to Deliver End-to-End FFPE Biospecimen and Metabolomics Solutions for Cancer Researchers

2026-04-22

Revolutionizing Oncology Research: The Synergy of Panome Bio and Audubon Bioscience

The landscape of cancer research is undergoing a profound transformation as scientists move toward more precise, data-driven methodologies. A critical hurdle in this journey has been the integration of high-quality biological samples with advanced analytical techniques. Recently, Panome Bio, a leader in metabolomics, and Audubon Bioscience, a premier global provider of biospecimens, announced a strategic partnership that promises to bridge this gap. By offering an end-to-end solution for FFPE (Formalin-Fixed Paraffin-Embedded) biospecimens and metabolomics, this collaboration empowers researchers to unlock deeper insights into tumor biology and metabolic pathways.

The Critical Role of FFPE Biospecimens in Modern Research

For decades, FFPE tissue blocks have been the gold standard for clinical pathology. These samples provide a vast archive of patient outcomes and clinical histories. However, extracting high-quality molecular data from these samples has historically been a significant challenge due to the preservation process, which can degrade biological molecules. Audubon Bioscience brings a wealth of experience in the ethical procurement and processing of these vital samples, ensuring that researchers have access to well-annotated, high-integrity specimens that are essential for accurate study results.

Why Metabolomics Matters in Cancer Biology

Metabolomics is the comprehensive study of chemical processes involving metabolites—the small molecule substrates, intermediates, and products of cell metabolism. In cancer, metabolic reprogramming is a hallmark of tumor growth and survival. By analyzing the metabolomic profile of a tumor, researchers can identify unique biomarkers for early detection, monitor therapeutic efficacy, and uncover potential new drug targets. The partnership between Panome Bio and Audubon Bioscience ensures that the metabolomic analysis is performed on samples that have been handled with the utmost precision, mitigating the risks of experimental bias.

How the Panome Bio and Audubon Bioscience Partnership Works

This collaboration creates a seamless workflow that simplifies the research process from start to finish. Researchers no longer need to coordinate with multiple vendors to procure tissue and subsequently outsource analysis. The integrated approach ensures quality control at every stage.

  • Strategic Procurement: Audubon Bioscience utilizes its global network to source specific, high-quality FFPE specimens tailored to the researcher’s inclusion and exclusion criteria.
  • Advanced Processing: Samples are handled according to rigorous clinical standards, preserving the integrity of the metabolic content within the FFPE tissue.
  • High-Resolution Metabolomics: Panome Bio applies its proprietary metabolomics platforms to generate comprehensive data sets, allowing for the quantification of hundreds of metabolites simultaneously.
  • Integrated Data Interpretation: The partnership provides researchers with a holistic view of the biological state of the tissue, combining clinical data with metabolic insights.

Accelerating Precision Medicine and Drug Discovery

The ultimate goal of this partnership is to accelerate the development of precision medicine. By understanding the metabolic signatures of different cancer types, clinicians can better predict how a specific patient will respond to immunotherapy, chemotherapy, or targeted treatments. This end-to-end service reduces the time-to-discovery, allowing pharmaceutical companies and academic labs to move faster from hypothesis to clinical application.

Impact on Translational Oncology

Translational research requires a bridge between the laboratory bench and the patient bedside. The Panome Bio and Audubon Bioscience partnership provides this bridge. By providing researchers with the ability to analyze the metabolome of archival FFPE samples, they are essentially unlocking the potential of millions of existing samples that were previously considered too difficult to analyze with traditional methods. This is particularly vital for rare cancer types where sample availability is limited.

Technical Advantages of the Integrated Platform

The technical synergy between the two companies addresses several common pain points in the laboratory:

  • Standardization: Minimizing variability between samples through optimized collection and analysis protocols.
  • Sensitivity: Utilizing state-of-the-art mass spectrometry to detect even low-abundance metabolites within challenging FFPE matrices.
  • Scalability: The ability to conduct large-scale cohort studies with consistent data quality, which is essential for statistical significance in clinical trials.
  • Regulatory Readiness: Ensuring that all biospecimens are collected in compliance with international ethical standards, facilitating easier translation to clinical pipelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are FFPE biospecimens and why are they important?

FFPE stands for Formalin-Fixed Paraffin-Embedded. These are tissue samples preserved in paraffin wax, commonly used in hospitals worldwide. They are crucial for research because they can be stored for years and are linked to extensive patient clinical data, allowing researchers to study the progression of diseases like cancer over time.

How does Panome Bio perform metabolomics on FFPE samples?

Panome Bio utilizes advanced extraction and mass spectrometry techniques designed specifically to overcome the challenges associated with formalin fixation. Their platform is optimized to identify and quantify a wide range of metabolites, providing a detailed metabolic snapshot of the tissue sample.

How can researchers access these services?

Researchers interested in these services can contact either Panome Bio or Audubon Bioscience directly. The partnership is designed to act as a single point of contact for project scoping, sample selection, and analytical execution, streamlining the procurement and research process.

Does this partnership cover all types of cancer?

Yes, the partnership is designed to be platform-agnostic regarding cancer types. Whether researchers are studying solid tumors, rare cancers, or hematological malignancies, the integrated solution can be customized to meet specific project needs and biospecimen requirements.

What are the benefits of this collaboration for the pharmaceutical industry?

For pharmaceutical companies, this partnership provides a high-throughput, reliable way to analyze clinical trial samples. This leads to faster data generation, better target identification, and more robust validation of biomarkers, ultimately reducing the risks associated with drug development.
